Chapter 1


Seven heart beats in ten seconds.
Asher multiplied it by six. Forty-two beats per minute. She was getting worse again.
He would need to speak with cleric Ogerius about her condition.
His mother lying on her bed, the strong brown flowing hair faded into a mute gray,
bones showed underneath her paper thick skin. Eyes sunken into their sockets with
dark bags underneath them. He barely remembered her any different. Her breathing
was shallow and silent, her chest barely moving.
"Asher", her voice was like a soft rustle of the wind through wet leaves. Her eyes
opened just enough for him to see blood.
"Mother, I am here." He pressed her hand softly trying not to break her weak bones.
"Your .. hair", she whispered. The rain outside thumping down onto the clay roof
almost swallowed her words. His hair was a dark brown, just like her mother's had
been. His face with a well defined jaw line for a seventeen year old made him popular
with the girls in Yevum.
"What's with my hair?", he asked. Silence. Her eyes still closed, her mouth half open.
"Mother, are you still there?" What a foolish question it was. She wasn't there
anymore. She was a mere shadow of herself. It had been two years since she had been
able to leave the bed at all. A spark of hope, a bright light to pull away the dark
clouds looming over their lives. It didn't last. It was a miracle in itself, that she
survived as long as she did. But in the end the disease grew stronger again,
swallowing her.
"I need to go Mother", Asher said brushing of a few strains of hair from her face. For
a moment he saw something in her face. Was it pain? She didn't have pain anymore,
her body to weak to feel. Was it shame? Lying there on the bed in her own filth,
unable to move or speak or care for herself. He shook it off. There was no reason to
dwell on these things for too long, it made it even harder for him. He put a wet cloth
to her lips and a few drops of water and mixed in medicine from the clerics flowed
into her mouth. Her plate of wet bread crumbs was still half full and standing on the
table beside her bed. Soon the straw underneath her would need changing again, it
stank.
Asher stood up, grabbed the plate and cloth with one last glance blowing over of
what remained and went downstairs.
"Is she any better", Elyas asked with childish hope in his voice. His green eyes eager
and alive. Bread and chees on the wodden table with a pattern of stars across the top.
Their father sitting across the table of Elyas silently eating his last bits.
"She is doing a bit better today I think", Asher said avoiding his brothers eyes out of
shame. Lying to him was the worst, but what could he do? He was the only one
having any hope left and he wouldn't be the one who took that away from him. Not
only for his brother but also for himself. He held onto it like it was the most precious
item in the world. Worth more than an iron sword, well worth more than an army, a
kingdom.
"You are late again", his father grunted. John was a bulky, muscular man who got his
physique from working at the lumber mill all his life. His rough hands let Asher's
look like children hands. He knew these hands too well. He never asked how their
mother was doing. Sometimes Ashen though he didn't care about her at all anymore,
he just wanted her gone. But he never forbid him to tend to her, to feed her or to
change her clothes and bed straw. At least on some level he hadn't given up yet.
"Try some of the bread", Elyas said. "I made it myself yesterday and Rollant let me
take it with. It's not even burned this time, look." He held the rest of the bread in the
air and turned it so he could see its perfect crust. How fast he learned working for the
lord's cook.
"Leave the bread and cheese, Asher. There is no time. I won't let you be late for work
again."
Asher quickly grabbed his green raincoat made out of leather and sealed with wax.
He walked over to his little brother, kissed him on the head and got an almost serious
angry, "Ey, I am too old for this now." His father already out the door, he followed
quickly into the rain.
It was normal for Yevum to have rain at least eight days out of ten. But it was that
way everywhere. He knew of no place where it didn't rain so much and he didn't want
to have it differently. The rain was part of their life just as everything else. The
church wanted to make them believe, the rain was punishment for their sins. God
avoided looking at his creation, banning them from sight with dark rain clouds. The
simply folk didn't believe in it. Sometimes rain was just rain and nothing more.
With hands in his side pockets, he walked after his father to the lumber yard always
making sure there was enough space between them. His green rain coat covered
almost his entire body, reaching down underneath his knees and ending just high
enough to not drag through the dirt. Deep trenches on either side of the sloped road
made sure the water had somewhere to go and not make the roads into a
untraversable mess.
Reaching the lumber yard he arrived before his father. He would, as he did every day,
stop by the Singing Starling inn where he got his first beer of the day. It was always
stocked with the famous strong beer from Raspborrow. That rainin' man.
Luckily for his father the lumber yard had always enough work to pay enough to feed
a family and a father's questionable habits. The rain always ruined something. The
hundreds of wood bridges spanning over from tiny creaks to big flowing rivers were
in constand need of repairing. The wood didn't last long in these conditions,
eventhough they tried to make them last longer with oils and wax. The water and
wind always found a way through.
The other men hacking and sawing at the lumber nodded towards him when they saw
Asher. He nodded back. His work was not sawing lumber or felling trees, but to carve
wood into intricate figures and patterns. Not a man's kind of work for many. He was
sitting for hours on a chair, or standing working away with his knives and tools to
carve away at the wood. Nicely covered by a roof from the rain and with four walls
protecting him against the winds. Many of the other men needed to go out into the
woods.
He sat down in his work station, the smell of freshly cut wood in the air, and got to
work on a small chest with an unfolding forest oneither of its sides. The wood spoke
to him, or so he felt when working. He didn't learn from masters of this craft, there
was never anyone capable of doing what he could in all of Yevum. Maybe
somewhere in the bigger cities he could have found someone else, but here he learned
it on his own. Starting out as a child with a knife he got from his father for his fifth
birthday, he began to shape wood into things he saw out in nature or at home. His
mother was still well and lively back then. The memory hurt him. His mind oftened
began to wander to different times when his father was a different man, his mother a
different woman. A time without his brother Elyas.
Away with these thoughts. He needed to concentrate on his work.
After four hours of work, his hands began to ache. Sawdust and scraps of wood piling
up next to his feet, it was time for lunch break. The men all went to the Singing
Sterling to get their lunch meal, and for some a bit of beer too. Or in Asher's father's
case more of the same.
Entering the inn, Asher hung his soaked rain coat to dry on the wall. The smell of wet
hair, sweat and freshly cooked deer stew hang in the air. The noise was almost
unbearable, loud laughter, angry discussions, spoons on clay bowls and clanks of
glasses smashing together. The low hanging ceiling supported by dark brown wodden
beams made the place look smaller than it actually was. Not a single nail was seen
anywhere wether on the support structure or the furniture. They had to manage
without any iron nails, iron was too precious.
He took one of the free tables near one of the corners quite far away from the fire, sat
down and waited for Ameline to bring him some of that delicious stew. It normally
had little meat in it but the herbs and spices made it good nevertheless. With grain
and other vegetable it was hearty and filling too.
Ameline came out from the kitchen carrying ten bowls of stew on a large plate. Her
hair as blond as the sun when it finally showed behind a curtain of clouds. Freckles
covering her cheeks and nose and even some other parts of her body, but only Asher
knew. She saw him sitting alone, smiled at him but served some of the other men
first. Asher felt cold, eventhough the air was warm and thick from dozens of bodies
cramped into a small space. Hopefully he wouldn't get rainfever. But that was amost
impossible. Only children got rainfever and he was an adult now.
After serving all of the other men, some from the lumber yard, some soldiers and
some women from the weaving mill, Ameline walked over to Asher's table holding
only one steaming bowl in her hand. She sat down with a bright smile. Her white and
blue working dress followed her figure and covered her neck up to her chin as it was
tradition for women to do so.
"I hope you like it", she said. "My mother let me do it all by mself this time. Nobody
complained yet but I feel like I could give them anything mildly resembling a warm
stew and they will eat it all to the last spoon."
After taking one slow spoon full to his mouth, he sniffed and put it in. His face a
grimace of disgust and pain. "Oh stop that you rainin' fool", she giggled.
"In all honesty", he said, "I think it is better than your mother's." It really was. A slice
of bread went with it.
"Well, thanks for the lie." Still smiling at him she added, "But I will take it. Tell me,
will you come to Balin's barn later tonight? My mother said I could go." She tried to
put some strains of her blond hair out of her face, but it fell right back in front of it.
"You know I will be there if you are. But I can't stay long, you know why."
"Yes", she sighed, "but we at least have a little time to enjoy ourselves. I will wear
my new blue dress with white stitchings. My mother worked for weeks on it. It is so
beautiful."
"I can't wait to see it", he said with honest curiosity in his eyes.
"I have to go now" she said giving him a quick kiss on the cheek and ran off towards
the kitchen. Asher looked after her with a smile on his worried face. He looked
through the room, searching for his father. He saw him with a bunch of the other men,
drinking beer. Probably not his first sadly. He worried about tonight. Ameline in a
beautiful new dress, dancing and singing. It sounded so seductive, he couldn't not go.
They would manage like they always did.
After washing himself outside the house, Asher dried himself of next to the fire. On
the backside of their house one side of the roof would slope down to one edge and
release a constant stream of cold rain water. It was cold but he was used to it. The fire
would drive out the last of his goosebumps soon. Elyas wasn't home yet. He was
always working longer hours than him and their father. His father wasn't home. A
pleasant detail.
He didn't have many clothes, because his mother was unable to sew anything for
years, he had to manage with what they had and with what money he made from
working. A pair of brown trousers which were a bit too short, a white tunic with
ruffles on his shoulders and long sleeves to cover his arms. Eventhough the rain made
it feel a lot colder than it actually was, a simple tunic would keep him warm enough
when he was in a dry place. For the last touch he fished one coal out of the fire, let it
cool before he used it, and drew the contour of his lower and upper eye lid with a
thick black line. A bowl filled with water acting as a mirror.
Grabbing his green raincoat he walked out the door and made his way across the
town to Balin's barn. The old farmer hosted dance gatherings every time he felt like
it. Despite his age Balin lived and breathed for dancing and singing.
Faint music squeezed its way through the falling rain and made Asher quicken his
steps. He didn't have much time left. In a couple of hours his brother would come
home and he wanted to be there before him. But there was still time to enjoy the
gathering with Ameline.
Entering the barn, the smell of hay, cows and beer in the air, loud music, laughter and
singing, he hung his coat on the wall next to all the others to dry. Scanning the room
there were about thirty people here. Young and old alike and in the midst of all,
Ameline in her blue dress with white details across her chest and a black belt tieing it
together at her waist. Her neck covered by blue cloth. She was dancing with her
father, not noticing Asher had arrived. He made his way through the crowd and
Ameline stopped mid turn when she saw him. A smile washed over her face, she
whispered something in her father's ear, he bowed slowly, nodded towards Asher and
walked away looking for a new partner or something to drink.
"You made it", she shouted trying to battle against the noise.
"I wouldn't have missed it for anything", he said. "You look absolutely stunning in
that dress." She blushed slightly and held a hand towards her mouth. Little blue
threads were woven inbetween her hair. Her eyes glowing in a muted blue as well.
And then they danced and danced until their feet hurt but they didn't stop. Laughter,
singing and the occasional fragments of sentences shouted into each others ears
trying to stand up to the noise in the barn. It was a time to relax, a time for happiness,
a time to let go of everything else and forget for a moment his family. And Asher did.
He knew he would feel guilty afterwards enjoying this moment as much as he did,
but he didn't care. With Ameline in his arms he was beginning to feel alive again.
Ameline's dress was moving with them as they danced, her laughter ringing in his
ears and before he knew it, he was smiling himself. Laughing even. Her mouth
slightly open, sweat shining on her forehead, she seemed the most beautiful girl in all
of Yevum. He knew the stories of queens who blinded others with their beauty but
how could they even come close to comparing with Ameline? No, nobody could.
Over the noise he shouted in her ear, he wanted to go outside for a bit. She happily
accepted and they went over to grab their raincoats. Her's was brown. The rain hadn't
stopped, unsurprisingly so. They walked besides the Haythorn river, one of Yevums
biggest. Asher didn't know all of the names for all the creaks and rivers carving their
way throught the rocky land like long blue snakes, but the bigger ones everybody
knew. It rain less now, only being a light mist of tiny raindrops.
"My birthday isn't long off now", Ameline said the hood shrouding her face as she
spoke. He knew what that meant. She didn't need to say it out loud, which was
frowned upon anyway. On her sixteenth birthday, Ameline would reach adolescence
and with that she was allowed to wed. He already got something planned for the big
proposal, but first he would need her father's and mother's blessing. He could only
ask them after her birthday though.
"I know", was everything he could say. His throat tightening and heartbeat rising.
They were fooling around with each other for months now, and the occasional kiss on
the cheek was a good sign. But would Ameline want to wed him? Or was she talking
about something else?
She nudged him with her elbow, playfully. The contact of her arm with his side
brought him out of his thoughts. The water rushing down the riverbed beside them,
about five metres wide.
He stumbled to his right exaggerating the blow immensly. She laughed, "Stop that
you ranin' fool." She nudged him again, this time actually harder, Asher stumbled to
the right, the steep cliff towards the river, a two meter drop, coming closer and closer.
His boot on the edge.
"Come back from that ledge before you fall into the water", she said. Looking at him.
Her eyes sparkling or so it looked for Asher.
"You are right", he said lifting up one foot to get closer to Ameline. With his right
foot in the air, his left foot lost its grip on the ground. A second of panic washed over
Asher's body. His arms flailing trying to prevent the inevitable. Ameline openeing her
mouth to shout, reaching with her arms towards him. It was too late. He fell.
His back hit the rocky ledge first, the pain made him moan but before a sound could
escape his open mouth the cold water surrounded him. He couldn't hear anything but
the sound of rushing water. The current pulled at him, his head hitting the rocky
ground making him lose all perception of what was up and what was down. He
struggled trying to pull himself up, his lungs beginning to hurt. It was too dark to see,
everything direction seemed to go deeper and deeper into the water. He turned and
turned, panic rising. His lungs at their threshhold. He wouldn't make it much longer.
Then, a moment of clarity. The water didn't seem cold anymore. He was floating now,
he had given up the struggle. He was calm. His body had given up, why? He saw his
father putting him up onto his shoulders to see something of the show when the
theater group was in town. His mother looking with a smile at them. Elyas as a baby,
so small and innocent. How could anyone give him any fault? His father beginning to
drink more and more. His mother fading away.
Then darkness took him. He didn't feel anxious, no he felt warm and welcome. Was
this death, he wondered. The air around him, or whatever was around him in this dark
abyss, getting warmer and warmer. He felt like he decended downwards, not that he
would feel any resistance, no wind blowing around his face, nothing. The dark around
him, smooth and without any marker to give him a sense of place, but he knew he
was sinking to the bottom of this. He looked down and saw something come close to
him, something red and yellow. Flames, giant flames licking at the not-air, filling the
bottom of this dark abyss. Asher saw them coming closer and closer, his body felt to
hot, sweat forming on his forehand and chest, back and arms. Everything felt too hot.
The flames drawing closer, so close he could feel their mighty heat. He had never
seen anything like this. It wasn't looking like a gigantic fire, no it was more like the
fire was a living thing, waiting for him at the bottom. He tried to shout, but no words
came from his mouth. The silence around him hurt his ears. The flames were so close.
It hurt, it burned him. The small fingers of the fire touched his feet, burning through
his boots and burning him alive. He tried to scream and paddle away, like he was in
water. He wanted to swim away, but the flames drew him closer.
He felt a sudden blow to his chest, his body arching forward trying to minimize the
pain. Water streaming out of his mouth, some vomit came with it. He breahted in
deeply, he couldn't get enough. Ameline crouching next to him, tears in her eyes and
his hand in hers. A familiar man towering over him.
"What..", he coughed some more water. His throat felt raspy.
"Fished you out of the river, lad", the man said. "Was a close one. You hit one of the
pillars of the bridge. You rainin' lucky, boy. Fished out a couple of lads in my life,
women too, but I thought you were gone for sure hanging like a sack around that
pillar." He knew the man, it was Salan, one of the men working with his father at the
lumber yard.
He saw movement to his right but the slap hit him. With a ringing in his head he
looked towards Ameline.
"Why.."
"You rainin' fool had me rainin' worried there." She was still crying, her whole body
sobbing.
"Well I leave you two to it then", Salan said. "You two should celebrate. Something
like this doesn't happen often. Take it as a blessing of the Enlightening." He winked at
Asher, walking away. Just then he noticed them man was drunk, he couldn't keep a
straight line. He was gone too quickly, Ashe still unable to use his voice, too late to
thank him now. He would have to do that in the morning then.
"I am sorry", he said to Ameline.
"Don't be sorry. Promise me you will never, ever do that again?"
"I promise." And he meant it.
"Even if the clouds stop raining?"
"Even then."
"Let's go back to the barn", she said. "I want you out of these wet clothes immediatly.
Don't want to lose you to a rainfever after all this."
"Oh, do you?", he said with a smirk.
"Don't get any ideas. I am still angry at you. You could have died. Like a fool falling
into a river. What kind of way to go is that?"
Asher stood up, his clothes soaked with water, his fair clinging to his skin and his
body shivering. They walked silenty over to the barn, he glancing over to her every
now and then. Her face sternly looking foward, shrouded by the hood. How could
that has happened? A beautiful evening ruined just because he wanted to make a little
joke. Gone were the feelings of laughter and dance, back was the normal Asher.
Back at the barn some people laughed at Asher when he came in soaked to his
underpants. Ameline insisted on removing every bit of clothing save his underpants
and dry them next to one of the fires that lit and warmed the room. The dark was
slowly creeping in. The sun was setting in the distance.
"Do you need a blanket or something", she asked. Her face looked concerned but her
cheeks were reddening.
"No, it's okay like this. The fire will warm me right up", he said.
They sat there silently watching the fire dance around the wood. Smoke rising in
swirls towards the high ceiling. The music, laughter and singing remained in the
background.
"I really thought you would die", she whispered. Barely enough to hear above the
noise but it hit him like she had screamed it in his face. He thought he had died too.
He got a little closer towards her, their arms almost touching. "I have big dreams for
us both when we are married." It was not custom to talk about marrigage if the
woman was not sixteen yet.
"What kind of dreams?"
"Well, you know. I see a house for us and our children. Three of them. One daughter
as beaituful as me and two sons as talented as you. You made all our furniture
yourself with images and patterns we both love." She hesitated. "I see you happy with
only smiles on your face."
Only smiles? He couldn't imagine how that would feel. A tear running down
Ameline's cheek. He put his hand to her cheek and she put her head into his hand. A
warm feeling originating in his chest, filled his body. He felt it rise to his face, his
legs and hands. It was so invigorating he stopped shivering.
The first thing he noticed was the scream. Ameline was screaming, pushing his hand
away from her face just to scream again. It went into Asher's bones he didn't
understand. Then he noticed the smell. Burned flesh. Amelines left side, the side his
hand was one, burned to the bone. The music stopped, people gathering around them.
Ameline's father rushing towards her, holding his daughte in his arms. The croud was
silent, save her screams. Asher looked around him. Angry faces watchin him, some
spitting on the ground next to him.
"Look", a woman shouted, "his hair is turning red. Elightening, protect us from your
accursed."
It couldn't be. These stories were myths, dating back thousands of years. He wasn't
one of them, his hair. It was no longer the same brown his mother used to have. It was
a vibrant deep red.
No, he thought. Please not me, not now.
Ameline.
