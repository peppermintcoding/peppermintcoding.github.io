Chapter 7


Cleric Saratsu's lessons were always long and exhausting. They learned a new way to
clean and stitch up deep cuts. It was a hassle without touching the pig patien, but
Asher felt getting more and more confident. An tried to talk about yesterday's evening
but Asher didn't bite the bait. He was still angry at basically all of them, but
especially Lilith. Eventhough she found her sitting beside his bedroom door, already
sleeping like baby. He sneaked into his room without disturbing her alcohol induces
sleep.
Because Asher was the only one not drinking yesterday he had an unfair advantage at
listening to the cleric's directions. If the cleric noticed anything different with his
students, he didn't say.
"Asher could you stay with me a few more minutes?", Saratsu asked while the Patric,
Kathhrine and An lef the classroom. Eventhough the windows were wide open, the
smell of blood lingered heavily.
"You expressed a wish to write to your family. A messenger dog will leave tomorrow
morning to make a round. It will pass through Yevum so now is the best time to write
to your family. What would you want to write?" He took out a clean piece of paper,
dipped the feather in ink and waited.
What wanted he to write? He knew what he wanted to know, but wasn't sure how to
write it. After all Ogerius would be the one reading it to his father and hopefully his
brother. He simply wanted to know if they were doing fine. His brother must be going
through the worst time of his life. Alone with their father, an ill mother to care for and
noone to talk about it.
"It doesn't have to be much. Write I want to know how they are doing, especially my
mother and brother. And tell them I am doing fine here, I already found some friends
and I will be coming home soon."
"You will be coming home soon?", Saratsu raised an eyebrow.
"Just write it down. I will find a way to come back home."
"I suppose you can dream and hope, but to be honest with you, you won't be going
back to Yevum."
"You don't know that", Asher said and tried to contain his anger.
"Oh, but I do know. Nobody ever gets back to their home town. Because you will not
survive there long. What do you think will your townspeople do with you? They
know what you are, they know what you have done already. They fear you. What
does an angry town mob do when they are afraid of someone with a special gift? It's
no surprise that in this institute there are no more than fourteen people. Nine small
children and you five older ones. But the fire comes to so many more these days."
Would they actually kill him? Asher knew these people since he was a baby. He
worked with them, ate and drank. On the day of awakening Ogerius hid him from the
people in the monastary. They were marching on Asher's home, with weapons.
"Write it anyway, if you may cleric. It will be good for my brother."
"As you wish." Asher went out the door of the classroom.
On the opposite wall Lilith was leaning. Her hair a mess like always, her long neck
covered by the cold grey cloth they all had to wear. Her eyes not meeting his own. He
wanted to walk past her, leaving leaning there without a word.
"Please wait", she said and held out an arm to block his way, still careful not to get
too close. "I wanted to talk with you last night."
"Really?", he said. "Didn't look like it last night."
"You could have woken me and we could have talked, but you were the one sneaking
around me. Didn't you see I wanted to talk to you? I even waited outside your room."
"Now it is my fault for not wanting to talk? What should I have done? Tapped on
your rainin' shoulder? It's not like we can do that. In any case, did you consider I
didn't want to talk to you last night?"
"Alright, just listen. I came here to tell you, you were right. It is not a competition
who has more dirt in their past and it doens't mean that you were not hurting just as
much as we others. It is simply different I suppose. We never tasted the life like you
had and that breeds envy, I guess. Nobody of us can imagine how it must feel to get
that taken away from you, we only know the other side of the coin. So I guess I got a
bit angry because, you know, I got a bit angry."
"Is that it? Can I go now?"
"Come on, don't be like that. I apologized. I also wanted to offer you my help with
learning how to deal with your talent. An might be a good teacher to you, because
you don't know any better, but they all learned from me. I know it best, so let me
teach you. The start is always hard, but I promise I can get to over it."
Asher wasn't feeling An to be a great teacher, but he wasn't bad either. He simply
didn't find the rigth words, Asher thought. Maybe it could benefit him to learn from
Lilith. After all she actually knew the most about this. Maybe he could get some more
information about this talen of his. But spending more time with Lilith wasn't
something he looked forward too.
"Alright, you show me how I can use my talent and I will accept your apology."
"Good, you want to do it now? I got some time before I will have to go on with my
training."
"Sure."
"Let's go to my room, we won't be disturbed there." So they walked aside each other
with plenty of distance between them. But for the first time, Asher felt the distance to
be much closer than it had been.
"By the way", Asher asked, "what is it you do in your training? It doesn't seem to
have anything to do with medical procedures. What kind of use did Lord Amberic
find for you?"
Lilith hesitated. "It's hard to explain. We can talk about it another time, alright?"
"Sure." Lilith opened the door to her room. It was simple, like Asher's own room. A
bed with feather matraze, a little fire place and a closet for clothing. No fancy
bookshelf like in An's room. It felt empty.
"So there we are", she exclaimed and waved her arms around the room. She noticed
Asher's look. "Expected something more? Not actually fit for a princess is it?"
"Princess? I never-"
"Don't worry. I know it's how An and others call me sometimes. Truth be told, I am
the furthest from a princess you can think of." They sat down in front of the fireplace,
a few pieces of wood stacked on top of each other, ash covering the ground.
"So how's your mother, if I am allowed to ask?"
"My mother?", Asher was taken aback. This came out of nothing.
Lilith eyes avoided his again. "I kind of dropped eves on you while talking with
Saratsu. Apologies."
"I see." Asher wasn't sure if he felt angry about it or relieved. After all now they were
beginning to talk like normal people, eventhough the questions were highly personal.
"My mother, she got ill when my little brother was born. She isn't doing very well."
"Oh no, the mother's curse?"
"Sadly, yes. She had been doing better after suffering for two full years. She was even
standing again, walking around. I had the highest hopes she had beaten the illness.
But in the end it took her again, shackling to her bed. It's been hard."
"I feel for you", she said meeting his gaze.
"Do you, or do you simply say because it's what people always say?"
"I mean it how I said it. Truth is I don't know your pain, I never had a mother."
"Did she die when you were born?"
A long pause. Asher shifted his legs a bit. Sitting crosslegged on the hard floor made
his legs ache.
"I don't know what happened to her." Lilith looking down, fumbling with her hands.
"My parents didn't want me. They put me in a basket, a piece of cloth in my mouth so
I couldn't cry and left me in a canal. When the rain came I was to suffocate under the
water. And I almost did, if not a group of clerics fished me out of the water. They
took me in, into an orphanage. They cared for me the best they could eventhough I
was a danger to everybody."
"So that's when you awoke then?"
"Yes. I never knew any other life than the life of a Scorched. Never felt a human
touch. Or any others for that matter. I remember so vividly, we had a flock of sheeps
at the orphanage. They had thick wool on their bodies and the children loved
touching them. They looked so soft and warm. I was longing to touch them, but I
wasn't allowed. The risk to high for me to injure one of them. They gave me some
wool from the sheep on its own as consolation. But it wasn't the same. I wanted to
touch a real sheep." Asher's hand automatically went over to Lilith, but he stopped
mid air and pulled them back. It hurt him physically in his stomach not being able to
touch her right there, right then.
"I feel for you", he finally said. Lilith's eyes meeting his. No tears in her's.
"But let's not dwelve into the past too much. We are here and that is what counts.
After all, you are here to learn and not to relieve the past. So, are you ready?"
"Yes I am. Let's try it. See who is the better teacher after all."
"Well, this is not a competition at all. An doesn't know what he is doing at all. You'll
see. Take a piece of wood out of the fireplace."
Asher did so, "I couldn't even bring dry tinder to ignite, how am I suppose to get this
piece of wood to burn?"
"It's not about the material, you can create flames on everything you set your mind to.
You simply need to know how. Now close your eyes, hold the wood in your hands
and feel it's texture. The sharp edges. It's weight. Now look inside of yourself and
find the flame."
"So far, you are not doing anything different to An."
"Hold your words and keep looking. The first time is always the hardest. Once you
know where it is hiding inside your body, you will be able to call upon every time
you want. So hush and hear inside your body. Think about how you move your arms,
how you command your legs to put one foot in front of the other. How you can roll
your eyes at my directions. It's hiding somewhere."
Asher tried to locate something he had no idea how it would feel. Again, he went
through his whole body, asking where the flame might hide. It was ridiculous. Maybe
he didn't have one after all, awoke to late and now has to live with only the curse side
of it without any benefits.
"Let's try something else." Her voice was soothing, it sounded like the rain.
"Sometimes you find the flame where your feelings are. Anger is one of the best ways
to find it. Think of something or someone who made you rainin' mad and then see if
you can find the flame where that feeling of anger originates."
Anger. He had lots of anger inside. About last night, about Lilith and actually all the
others too. But that wasn't real anger he understood. He was angry at them in the
moment, it was fleeting anger. What he felt for his father went deeper inside his
bones. He could feel the heat of anger. It was in his chest, pressing down and making
it harder to breathe deep. He thought of his drinking, how he was when he came
home drunk and let his anger out on his children. Angry for something they weren't at
faul. And there he found a different kind of heat. Underneath all this anger something
small but burning hot was hiding. He could feel its fiery tounges licking, could feel
the heat. He tried to maneuver the heat towards his hands, to the piece of wood
waiting there.
"Open your eyes, you got it", Lilith said. Asher openen his eyes and saw flames
gnawing at the wood. They were faint, a yellow and red swirl. But they were here. He
did it. He looked at Lilith, who was smiling at him. Smiling, for the first time he saw
her smile.
"You should smile more often. It looks good on you", Asher said but it did the
opposite of what he would have anticipated. Lilith'S smile froze and vanished.
"You should go now."
"Did I say something wrong?", he asked. The flames had vanished completely. They
left the piece of wood almost unchanged, a thin layer of dark charcoal formed on one
side.
"Just leave me. I am tired and need some rest. We can talk more tomorrow at
breakfast."
Asher left Lilith's room with three pieces of wood he grabbed from her. One for more
practicing and two for carving. But why was she so nice the one moment and so
pissed the next? He didn't know what he did wrong.
Weeks went by and Asher was desparetly waiting for an answer to his letter home.
His dreams revolved all around his mother and brother, how they were doing. Mostly
nightmares they were. Saratsu's lessons in medicince further enhanced Asher's skills.
He was able to clean and stitch all kinds of cuts in record time. Sterializing his tools
with his own flame was getting easier and easier with every try. He had practiced
every evening in his room. It became almost like using a limb like his arm.
And then there was Lilith. He tried to make it up to her with carving something for
her. He wasn't sure what he needed to apologize for but he wanted to anyway. So he
carved a sheep out of the wood he took from her room. It was small, only as big as a
fist, but Asher got all sorts of details in there. He knew sheep from Balin's farm and
he knew how they felt when touched. Not as soft and perfect as Lilith thought herself
but more oily and rough. The miniature wooden sheep had a snout, ears and even
eyes. The body was carved into as close to sheep wool as Asher could get it.
When he went over to her room to give her the sheep, it didn't went like he expected
it to happen. He was knocking on her door, waiting with the sheep in his hand.
"What do you want so late, Asher?", she asked. She was obviously getting ready to
sleep. Her eyes worn down from the day. The cold grey robe was gone, only her
underwear showing, with a long sleeve shirt which covered her neck and a slip.
"I wanted to give you this", Asher handed her the wooded sheep. He tried not to
wander with his eyes down her body, but focussed on her eyes. For a second he saw
them light up with joy, he could tell. But shortly after, she frowned and look at him
funnily.
"That's a sweet gesture of you, but you shouldn't have", she said. "Take it back
please."
"No, I made it for you. I won't take it back." He made a step backwards.
"You need to stop treating me differently than the others. I haven't seen you make An
or Kathrine a present like this."
"Because they didn't help me find my flame like you have. An tried, for sure, but
that's about it. You helped me, so I got you something. You were kind of angry at the
end, and I don't know why."
"And you think a present of a sheep would help me get less angry?"
"I don't see why it would make you more angry at me, but delight me with an
explanation." They voices grew louder and louder. The other's must be able to hear
this.
"I don't want you to look at me like that everytime we are alone together. I don't want
presents from you, that make me smile and forget for a second what I am. What we
are. Because we will never be able to change it. Don't you understand? I don't want to
end up like Patric and Kathrine. Madly in love, but unable to touch one another. It's
the most cruel torture I have ever witnessed." She broke off. Slammed the door in
Asher's face.
After that Asher tried to keep more distance between himself and Lilith. Though he it
was harder than before. He didn't want to stay away from her. It was right there, he
truly understood. They couldn' fall for each other. The smallest spark must be
extinguished at first sight.
Asher tried to stay away from Lilith as much as possible. It was easy sometimes,
because they had lessons in different parts of the building, but sometimes it cost him
quite a lot of constraint. He still didn't know what she was learning while he was
training to be field medic.
Listening to a monologue of cleric Saratsu about the dangers of battle made it easy
for Asher's thoughts to wander. Saratsu went on and on about one detail he tried to
cement in the students brains. The number one cause of death on a battlefield was not
by a sword, spear or club, it was infections and diseases that came from lying in the
mud and dirt of the battlefield. Infections spread fast and killed fast. After class he
wanted to speak with Asher alone, he even closed the door this time for more privacy.
"So Asher, I have been given a letter from your cleric in Yevum." He took out a single
piece of paper from a drawer. He put it on the table, marked with symbols in black
ink. Only the rich could afford to give their children education in writing and reading.
Or if you decide to be a cleric.
"What does it say?", Asher asked. He was at the edge of his seat. Finally some
answers to his questions. Finally some certainty parting the clouds in his mind.
Without proper knowledge his mind made up the most horrible fantasies. His mother
was dead, his brother with her. Ameline's father was out to kill him. Now he maybe
got some proof.
"My apologies to tell you this, but it says your mother is doing worse again." These
words. He had been imagining them hundreds of times in his mind, but he still wasn't
prepared for the feelings rising in his body and mind. His chest was to tight, his
stomach was rebelling and his mind blank like the white quartz walls. No thought
spinning through his busy mind. Nothing. She was dead? No, no he just said she was
feeling worse. But that was nothing new. In any case he had hoped for some better
news.
"Aplogies", he tried. V oice breaking, tears dropping slowly. "Ehm, are you sure that
what is says?"
"I feel for you, son", Saratsu answered, "but it leaves no room for interpretation.
Ogerius describes her condition as very serious, but she is still alive and fighting."
She was always fighting, her whole life she had to.
"Anything else, he wrote. Some words about my brother?"
"Your brother tells you he is doing fine. Caring for your mother is hard on him but he
makes it work. And he writes your father is doing good as well, he is helping with
your mother a lot." What? He was helping? Something felt very wrong here.
"Are you sure cleric?", he asked again.
"I am. No room for interpretation."
"Yes, you said that already. Did my brother write anything about his work? How he is
been doing in his service?" This was their secret phrase they would be using when
communicating something their father shouldn't know. Work is good, means
everything really is good and the other way around. The last time Asher had seen
Elyas was so long ago, it felt to him. They saw each other just before he had to leave,
Elyas came to say goodbye to Asher in the monastsery where Ogerius was hiding
Asher before sending him off to Asta.
Saratsu looked down for a moment, back on the letter, "No there is no mention of
your brother's work. I have told you everything that is written here."
Could Saratsu be lying to him? Asher looked into Saratsu's eyes, no sign of any
emotion but empathy. Didn't he want to tell Asher the cold hard truth? How could he
be sure? He needed to get home to find out the truth, but how? "Thank you for your
troubles. Could you write another letter to my family then?"
"Of course, Asher", he said and grabbed a new piece of paper. "What would you like
to write to your father and brother?"
