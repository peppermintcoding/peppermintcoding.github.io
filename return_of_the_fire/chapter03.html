Chapter 2


Four strange men were waiting for her. Malina was about to venture into the
infamous Cloud Forest for the first time, accompanied by these scruffy looking men.
Why did her father want her to go? She had no expirience in fighting, no knowledge
of plants, herbs or mushrooms. She had a slender figure, short brown curls reaching
just to her shoulders and a small nose.
"You'll be careful out there", Richart, her father, said. "Keep to Dago, you know him
right?"
She knew him alright, he had visited their family home quite often in the last few
years. His visits were short and he always left something behind. Dreamshrooms.
"I know, but father", she began but was quickly interrupted.
"No but's, Malina. You know we are short on money. It is time you pull some of the
weight of this family, too. You are eighteen now, old enough to work for sure. Dago
promised it is safe. The savages haven't been spotted in these parts of the woods for
years. Keep close to him and all will be fine. Watch out for the red mushrooms. Get
as many as you can, okay?"
She wasn't convinces at all, but did she have any options? One does not talk back to
their father, not in their circles. The family of Scarra fell from graze and wealth a
century ago but that didn't stop every generation trying their best to hold up
appearances.
"Go now, the others are waiting. I love you with all my heart, my pearl."
"I love you too, father. I will do my best."
She walked over to the impatiently waiting men. Dago was wearing a hat against the
rain, black with colorful feathers all around. Green as grass, blue as the ocean and red
like blood. She had never seen anything like it. The other three men weren't so
fashionable. Their cheap raincoats had patches and holes in them, one was missing
part of his sleeve. The men were looking her up and down, making her feel
uncomfortable.
"Aye, let's go", said Dago. He was older than her father with grey strains in his
otherwise black hair. His nose looked like it had been broken before and a misshaped
beard gave him a sort of dirty look.
The three other men got packs on their backs. For firewood and food, she presumed.
"You will look after her, alright?", Richart shouted after them but he didn't get an
answer. Malina looked back once more and waved. For a second he saw something
on her father's face but was unable to make sense of it. She was never good at reading
facial expressions. They remained a mystery to her.
Her father had come all the way to the edge of the Cloud Forest, but no further. He
had to take care of some other business while she was gone. He would have come
with Dago if he only had the time. Or so he told Malina. But lately, he wasn't as
trustworthy as he once was.
The thick forest building up in front of here. So close to the edge, the trees were high
and had wide spanning canopies. Crossing the border between woods and grassland,
she notices the rain stopped. The constant noise of drops hitting her hood were gone,
only occasionally she heard a single one drop down. What a weird feeling it was,
outside while it rained without feeling the constant contact with her body.
The men kept shooting glances back at her, one of them walking behind Malina sent
shivers down her spine. How could her father let her go with these men? What kind
of company was that for a classy young woman of the upper class?
"You alright, Missy?", Dago asked. He had been falling behind slowly to get to her.
"I am quite fine", she responded. "Though I wonder how much longer do we need to
go uphill. My legs are beginning to ache already."
"Well, the shrooms we want don't grow near the border, but further in. So we will be
walking for about half the day. Before-"
"Half the day?", she was perplexed. "Always walking up this steep inclince? You got
to be joking with me. And do I might add, it is not funny."
"Meant no joke at all, Missy." The way he pronouced 'Missy' was making her upset.
"They call it Cloud Forest for a good reason." He laughed. Indeed they did. The forest
was spanning over the land high up to the tops of mountains. The ground would come
so high, they would be on the same level as the clouds on some days. Giving the
forest and even more mysterious and dangerous face.
Hours later her legs were burning, her lungs working as hard as they could to breathe.
The altiutde made it harder and harder to breathe though. They had two breaks for
resting, but they weren't enough for Malina. Everytime she thought she would be
unable to stand up from the fallen tree they were resting on. Evertime Dago pulled
her to her feet and told her to keep walking. The other kept looking at her in a funny
way. She didn't know what it meant.
The ground was soft but littered with branches, ferns, moss and ferns. They
constantly tuck on her clothes, some poking into her flesh. Everytime she cried out
feeling the pain in her legs. Dago ordered her to keep walking. What choice did she
have? Go back alone? She would get lost immidiatly. She didn't want to be alone in
these woods especially not if the dark set in. She constantly saw movement in the
corners of her eyes. Something was watching them, she was sure of it.
"Okay", Dago said. "We are getting close. Keep your eyes on the ground for anything
red. Pick it up with you knives. I want clean cuts, bring the shrooms to me and I will
look if they are the right ones and if they are any good. Don't go to far, if you out of
shouting distance you could get lost. That applies especially to you, Missy."
The men fanned out in all directions leaving Malina and Dago alone.
"Here, your knife." He gave her a real knife, with a sharp blade and a wodden handle.
A cloth bag as well to put in the shrooms she presumed. She reached for the knife and
sack. It felt heavy in her hand. She had never been allowed to hold a knife before. It
wasn't fit for a woman of the right status to use sharp blades. Meals were cut by their
parents when they were young, and thir husbands when they got older. Of course, she
snucked in the kitchen sometimes at night and held one anyway just to feel. Still, she
grew afraid of the thing in her hand.
Dago must have interpreted her face correctly, "You either cut shrooms like the
others, or I leave in these woods tied to a tree, you hear me?"
She nodded, terrified by the pure thought of it. Why was she here anyway? Why did
her father not hire some more men to go on this quest? Why sent her of all the
people? She was his precious pearl, the last family he had and the last chance of
revitalizing their house. Her marriage must be planned carefully to rise up to the old
glorious days again.
She was alone. Dago had left her standing, she saw his silhouette vanishing between
the trees. She hurried along. Something red. It should have been easy to spot
something red in these woods. They weren't far enough into the forest to lose vision
because of clouds and the surrounding plants were all in browns and greens. She tried
to keep an eye on where Dago was walking. Always keeping him on her right side
she was sure, to not lose sight of him. These woods scared her. She heard terrible
stories of giant cats you would pounce on you from far away. Wolves roaming
between the trees in giants packs. And then there were the savages. Folk who fled
from the nine kingdoms into the forest long ago to make their life their, away from
the Scorched kings and queens of that time.
She saw one. It was hiding underneath some fern but the color shone through enough
for her to notice. It had white dots all over the top. Did Dago mention the white dots?
No he only said with a red top. But what would be the harm? She could bring it with
her anyway and he would throw it away or not.
Grabbing the head with her hand she cut the stem as far down as she could and put
the little mushroom in her bag. She didn't know what they were for exactly. Her
father would sometimes lock himself in the basement of their house and wouldn't
come out for a day or so. Malina had seen him take these shrooms inside though.
A branch broke with a loud sound behind her. She turned around frantically almost
losing her footing. Nothing there. Nothing but trees and more trees. Their trunks not
wider but their canopy closer towards the ground. She hadn't noticed the change
before now. Trees looked all the same to her.
She looked for Dago but couldn't see him. She felt panic rising in her stomach. Her
hands began to tremble just a bit. She shouted for him. No answer. Then again. No
answer.
She began to walk towards were she thought the others might be. The trees impairing
her field of view immensly. Big drops of water falling around. She heard another
branch snap. She turned around still shouting for Dago. Where were he? How could
she have been seperated so easily? Her hands clenching the knife close to her body,
the bad dangling from her left hand. She heard sounds in every direction. Something
was coming. She could feel it. The ground seemed to vibrate. Something big, she
thought.
"You rainin' girl!"
She knew that voice. Dago was standing to the left of her. Looking at her with furious
eyes.
"You do not shout like a little brat in these woods. They are safe because we are silent
and quick. If you want to attract whatnot then keep on crying like a little brat. But do
it far way from me because I don't want any more company."
"I.. I am sorry", she said feeling tense but more relaxed now. The ground was still, the
woods quiet safe the raindrops and the occasional birds singing.
"How many you got?"
"What?", she asked.
"How many shrooms you got in your little bag. It looks like you got nothing in
there."
"Oh the bad. I got one in there." Now that she said it, it sounded like nothing.
Dago shook his head. "Keep on looking", he grunted and walked away. Malina close
on his heels.
After an eternity of looking for more shrooms they were finally done for the day
when Dago signalled them to find a place to spend the night. The other three men
came back with full bags of shrooms, Dago sorting through them, throwing some
away but mostly he was happy with the outcome. Malinas bag held four mushrooms.
Two of them Dago immediatly threw over his shoulder, the other he put with the
others. She felt shame bubbling underneath her skin. The men eyeing her weirdly
from the side. They were whispering to each other. She grew more and more tense.
A small crackling fire gave warmth and some light as the sun went down. They didn't
bring enough dry wood to make a real fire but this was better than nothing. Malina
warmed her feet and hands and let her clothes dry. Her covered neck felt sweaty and
sore from walking and picking through the shrooms. A large tarp prepared with wax
to make it water resistant span over them. Dago placed himself between her and the
other men which made Malina relax a little bit. She felt he was better company than
the others.
"So, boss, the night is young. We are all tired but a little fun should lighten our minds
and bodies", one of the man said. A scar over his forhead, black hair and even darker
beard covering most of his face. His eyes glued on Malina.
"A little fun, eh?", Dago asked. His eyes wandering to Malina. For a moment she saw
his face change, his eyes glinting with fire in them. Malina pulled her legs to her
chest. "I think we should all go to bed early, get our strength back for the way home.
And you three should try not to forget what I said. Dago doesn't talk with empty
words." His eyes still hovering over Malina while he spoke.
The other men hesitated for a while but then stood up and went over to their
makeshift tent. Malina breathed out heavily. She didn't realize she had held her breath
the whole time.
"Dago, if I may", she said, "what were you talking about?"
"You look like a clever girl, eh?" He pulled out a knife and began to peel an apple.
"By the way, do you like your apples peeled or not? I am just curious here."
"Peeled of course." What savage would eat the hard and bitter skin if they could get
the sweet and delicious inner part on their own. "But that doesn't answer my
question."
Dago hesitated, peeling his apple. "Your father's a good man, right? I mean he's good
to you, eh?"
Was her father a good man? "He is the most honest man I know. He is educated well,
knows the proper way around social events and has always provided for me
eventhough our family has been struggling severly." She shouldn't have said that. Her
family image was all that was left of their legacy. How could she give up a detail like
that. Dago didn't seem to be surprised by that at all.
"Here's another question for you-"
"No, why do you keep dodging mine. If I have to answer one first, that's alright. But
now I deserve some information, too. This is not the proper way to treat a woman of
class." Eventhough her body was still tense and frightened, her voice was stern and
clear. A sound in the woods made her jump.
"Ah, the proper ways, eh? You think I don't know how to treat a woman rigth? Should
learn something from your father then."
"To be frank, you absolutely should. It is not right to leave me picking throught he
dirt in the Cloud Forest and doing so basically alone. I was frightened to death before
when I lost sight of you."
"Why do you think your father has sent you with us on this trip. I mean, a woman of
class working as a shroom picker, in the Cloud Forest with no guards or honorable
men around. Does that fit your description of your father right?"
The question had been fermenting in her mind all this time. Why did he insisted on
sending her on this trip? She knew he had business to attend in the city, so he couldn't
part take in this endeavour himself. But why not send someone else? A worker
perhaps? Well, they had no money to spend on these things anymore, but a woman
with no experience at all? It made no sense.
"Ah, I seed the little pretty head of yours cooking. At what conclusion did you arrive,
Missy?"
"I", she hesitated, "don't know. Why don't you tell me already?" A feeling in her
stomach told her not to ask. Not to dwelve deeper into this conversation, to just stop
and go to sleep, walk back to Nigita and leave this all behind. Another part of her
wanted the answer.
Dago gave her a piece of his apple, she accepted with a thank you. The apple was
good, juicy and sweet. "Four lonely man, in the woods with nothing to do at night."
He looked down at his apple, pulling one piece after another into his mouth. "And
then there is a lonely woman with them, who doesn't know how to forage, doesn't
know how to fight, doesn't even know how to look after herself." Malina felt a pinch
in her heart. Then it dropped down to her knees, her face became pale like the white
moon Ocreis behind the clouds. Her hands shivering, not of the cold.
"No", she gasped.
Dago met her eyes. "What a rainin' world we liv' in, eh?"
No.
