Chapter 13


The war machinery of Highlord Rember was beautiful and frightening to Asher.
Everything worked perfectly together, all the parts fit together like the quartz blocks
of Asta, without enough space for a piece of paper between them. It was like getting
to the last part of building a closet, without the help of nails, the pieces were built to
weeve together, locking tightly without the help of anything else. It felt like that.
Leaving Asta with the Highlord himself was like a grand parade. People were
standing around the main street, throwing flowers and singing songs for the soldiers.
When finally outside the city, with every cross road more and more soldiers, lords
and waggons joined the Highlords army. It was smooth and without delay.
The Highlord has commanded all of his available troops to get to the eastern border
to Nigita, where the war would take place. This was more than the usual border
skirmishes, where a couple of hundred soldiers of the lower ranked lords would fight
over one bit of land. This was bigger, grander than anything Asher had seen before.
He had heard stories of war and how heroes were made and buried therelike, but this
was different. It wasn't a bedtime story to frighten or entertain children. This was real
life and he was right in there with them.
Asher and An were moving with the army, while Lilith was left behind to keep up her
training, whatever that meant. He still didn't know and was afraid to ask. Her powers
exceeded his own by an indefinite amount. The army must have been thousands of
men strong, maybe even scratching the numbers of ten thousand. A huge army, with
an even larger trail of provision, workers, shelter, siege equipment and women. Asher
kept his distance to the soldiers, their eyes looked either scared or frightened at them.
But with the word of the Highlord nobody would harm them.
"Are you nervous," An asked, walking next to him through the rain. The ground was
so muddy it was hard to walk. Thick pieces of mud clinging to the boots.
"Aren't you? I don't know if I am ready for a war," Asher said. His red hair was
hidden underneath a deep hanging hood, but the red belt around his waist was enough
to identify him as a Scorched.
The road to the border was long. They travelled for one week already and were
supposed to not arrive till the late evening in their war camp. A tross of waggons was
pulled by strong bulls, slowly making it along the muddy road.
"Did you say goodbye to Lilith before you left?" An asked.
"Well, she wasn't at breakfast that morning and I haven't seen her after we met in
your room, so sadly no."
"Why not? Don't you think that's kind of rude?"
"She told me to not treat her differently like the others. She wanted space between us
so I thought I give her that."
"That might be true, but don't you think it would be the proper way to give her a
personal goodbye. I mean after all, we were kind of friends and you don't plan to
come back with us."
"Be quiet about that," Asher hissed. He looked around anxious somebody could have
overheard their conversation. The plan was risky and he wanted to keep it as secretive
as possible to be achievable. "Let's not talk about our plan, alright?"
"If you say so, sure." They walked in silence for a bit. The only noises coming from
the bulls in front and behind them and the rain on their hoods, like little drums.
"So you think I should have given her a proper goodbye?" Asher asked, but the words
felt wrong in his mouth. Why didn't Lilith joined them for breakfast that last day? She
knew they would be leaving an hours later. Where was she? For Asher it was clear:
she didn't want to see them off. The reasons might be shrouded in mist, but he was
certain that was what Lilith intended with her actions.
"I think she would have liked for you to say goodbye to her," An said. "She is a little
weird sometimes, but she actually likes you. So it was a bad move on your part."
"Hmm. I didn't get that vibe from her."
"Of course not. It's hard for us to feel close to others, because we can't act on feeling
that might arise. But it doesn't mean we shouldn't look for any closeness with other
people. Even Lilith wants company sometimes."
Asher was thinking about An's words and how Lilith has acted towards him. He
wasn't sure what it all meant, but now that he was out on the road with the army,
moving towards the war, from which he hoped not to return to Asata, he regretted not
saying goodbye.
A group of soldiers passed them on their right and they cursed at Asher. One spat out
and they went on their way. The words they used were never pleasant, but it mostly
didn't bother Asher much. An on the other hand was growing increasingly angrier and
with it more bold. He threw some words back to the group of soldiers, but they didn't
turn around. Even with the words of the Highlord himself, the soldiers and other folk
didn't trust, neither liked them. Asher couldn't blame them. They must have heard
stories or expierenced them themselves. Story like the one with Ameline.
The more time Asher spend on this trip with people from all over Nagano, the more
he was puzzled by the amount of personal stories the people shared about Scorched.
Apparently all over the country more and more people awoke to their curse and most
of them, sadly, were killed within hours of their change. It felt weird, to have
survived, what most others didn't seem to be able to do.
When they finally arrived at their final camp, so close to the border, the land of Nigita
could be seen with their own eyes, Asher and An were showed towards the medic's
tents. Huge tents with hundreds of beds packed next to each other were being set up.
This, unfortunaly, was not the place to work for Asher and An. Their talents would be
valuable on the battlefield, where it would be impossible for a medic to light a fire
due to the heavy rain and Asher and An would try and heat up instruments for the
medics to use. It should lessen the effect of infections for the wounded soldiers. At
least that was the theory, nobody had ever tested it before this war.
While in the camp it was harder to keep space between Asher himself and the others
soldiers, most of them were not accustomed to the way Asher and An had lived back
in Asta. They knew to hold their distance but with the narrow streets between rows of
tents and others makeshift buildings, it was hard. Even harder when inside the
medic's tents. Asher and An were wearing more clothing them normal and it was hot
underneath them. Lord Amberic was under the impression the layers of clothing
could lessen the impact of their curse, but up to this point, cloth had not been
successful in holding back. Not even metal would hold for long. The flames would
find their way through the thick metal and burn whatever was behind it. Somehow,
the flames felt there was flesh in their reach and they were calling for it, finding their
way through everything.
"There you are," a slender man with the blue colors of the medics corps said. His hair
was short, almost bald. "You two will be paired up with the two best of our field
medics. Anderius, who is that?" An raised his hand and smiled half heartidly.
"Alright, you will teamed up with Coran and your friend, Asher, will be paired up
with San. You will meet them tomorrow morning, the have been delayed and have not
yet arrived at the war camp. Quite unusual if I might say, but that doesn't concern
you." He looked around nervously, scanning the room. "Oh," he said, "you are still
here? You are dismissed. I have important matters to attend to, so please not get in my
way and burn me alive, if you can." He walked away, letting An and Asher behind,
standing lonely in the turmoil.
They made their way back towards the center of the warcamp, where Highlord
Rember had his own majestic tent. It span a wide radius, perfectly round with a high
ceiling. It could easily harbour a hundred people or more at the same time. The colors
where of red and gold, the Highlords personal color scheme. It was real gold plated
and woven into the fabric to make intricate patterns. Some were plants, some were
symmetrical in nature and some where representations of people, mostly the
Highlords family and deceased wifes.
Asher and An were given a tent close to the Highlord so they would be close and
protected, eventhough Asher didn't feel like he was valued here at all. Not by the men
at least. All he got was sorry eyes or angry ones, mostly both actually. Even An wasn't
his natural cheerful self, the smile on his face barely visiting and his eyes deep in
thought most of the time.
"What do you want to do?" Asher asked when they entered the tent together. Two
makeshift beds of straw and some pelts. A fireplace in the middle with an exhaust
right above it. The top of the tent was cut of and then raised by wodden supports so
the smoke could travel outside but the rain wouldn't fall down on the fire.
"I don't know," he said and sat down on his bed. His hands supporting his head from
falling down. His red hair matching the red belt. "Normally I would suggest to drink,
what else can you do on the eve of battle really. But I don't like drinking alone."
"Apologies," Asher said and meant it. He felt sorry. Lately he had been debating the
topic of alcohol more and more. On same days he came to the conclusion he didn't
want his father to have this kind of power over him. Just because he was worse off
with drinking, he himself shouldn't suffer anymore under this than he already did. But
then again, the simple thought of smelling the sharp smell of liquor was enough to
turn his stomach and bring memories up.
"No, don't. It really isn't your fault. I mean, what would it change anyway, rigth? I
think we should rather talk about your plan." Asher took a piece of wood from the
fireplace and held it in his hands. He lit it on fire within a heartbeat and put it together
with the others to let the fire burn. Lately he had been practicing it every morning and
evening, and it showed. He was quick about guiding the flames from within him to
the outside. "So, did you think about how you want to stage your death and run off
towards your home?"
"If you put it like this, it sounds stupid."
"Well, it kind of is. I know I am the one who suggested it, but the way back to Yevum
will be long and hard. You have no money, no map, no idea how to hunt, you can't
interact with normal people, because they would see your hair and disgard you in a
second. So how do you intend to solve any of these problems?"
"Thank you for making me worry even more," Asher answered and hung his rain coat
next to the fire to dry. He dropped of his boots and lay down on the bed, looking
towards the ceiling. "I don't know anything. The only thing I really know is, I need to
get back to Yevum. I need so see if my brother is alright, I fear for his well being."
"I see. So let's break it down, money first. What should we do?"
"We could steal some?"
"Great plan, Asher. Let's steal from the soldiers here, who despise us already. Do you
think the Highlord would leave us unpunished during war? You know the punishment
for any offense is death, for stealing it is at least giving up your hands. And I don't
know if we will be any good without our hands."
"Alright, why not shoot down every idea I have? Do you have anything to
contribute?"
Asher looked at An from the side and An told him what they would do. The plan
maifesting in Asher's mind into something tangible. Would this work?
Sleep came slowly to Asher and was short lived.
A horn woke Asher from his dreams. He had seen terrible things with his brother and
needed a moment to collect himself, An was already up and getting ready. If the horns
were sounded, everything needed to move fast. The enemy wasn't waiting on the
battlefield for long, until they would try and all out attack on the camp. A disordered
army was easy picking for the enemy.
Asher put on his boots and coat and he wanted to go out the tent when An told him to
wait a second.
"Good luck out there and don't try anything on the first day. The battle will drag on
for weeks at miminmum, so keep out of trouble for now, before we figure everything
else out, alright?"
"Sure," Asher said. "Good look to you to, I will be looking forward to seeing you
after the battle. Don't play the hero, alright?"
"Never wanted to, never will." The two left the tent. Rain splashing down from the
sky, big drops drumming against Asher's hood.
Outside, soldiers were streaming from the tents and making their way to the
battlefield, towards the rising sun. It wouldn't be good for the soldiers to fight against
the sunlight, but heavy clouds were shrouding the Enlightening's eye enough to not
blind any men. An and Asher made their way to the medic's tent where they would
meet up with their partners. Soldiers hurrying along side them, jogging in full armor
and with their weapons.
The armor of the soldiers was made out of hard leather. Big tiles were overlapping
each other and protecting most of the body, only down under the knee the armor
made room for some simple cloth under garment. Most of the armor was painted red,
like the Highlord's tent with cold insigia on them. They wore traditional helmets
made of leather as well, covering their hats and keeping the rain from their eyes. The
helmets were a circular shape abover their heads, slanted to the left side. Their face
half covered by wodden masks, leavin only the eyes free to see during battle. Armed
with spears, or some wealthier soldiers, with real sords, they looked fierced and well
trained. Asher was again, strangely impressed by the accuracy and familiarity the
soldiers acted around the camp.
The two split up to meet up with their respective partners for the remaining time of
the war. San was waiting in front of the medic's tent, An had to go look for his. Asher
wished him good luck again and An walked inside the tent.
"I have three rules," San said. His clothes in dark blue, like Asher's own. Only the red
belt and his hair distingiushed him from the others. "Number one, you do not touch
anything or anyone without me telling you to. Number two, you do not talk back to
me about the best cource of action when taking care of a wounded soldier. I heard
you had some training, but this is the real world and I won't see my soldiers die
because you want to show of some of your shady knowledge." The man talked like
the soldiers were his own children it seemed to Asher. "Number three, don't get
killed. Am I understood?"
"I think number three is kind of irrelevant, don't you think? It's not like I want to get
killed on the battlefield."
"Alright, number two will be extended. Do not talk back to me ever. I am here to do
my job and if this ridiculous plan of Highlord Rember will work, we need to do our
best."
They started walking towards the formation of soldiers. Thousands of them lining up
outisde the camp, the noise was immense. "What would you normally do without my
help?" Asher asked curiously.
"Your help, hilarious." San didn't laugh. "Normally we would get the soldiers from
the battlefield as quickly as possible, but a lot of them would die of bloodloss before
we could get them back. Our job will be to get to serious injured soldiers and stitch
them up as quickly as possible. So I will wash their wounds quickly with some fresh
water and then you will give me a heated blade to close the wound. Is that possible
for you?"
Asher began to like this guy. Eventhough he seemed to not like Asher at all, the man
seemed to care about his work a lot and Asher respected that. "I can do that." He
wasn't sure how long it would take to heat up the blades everytime, but he would try
his hardest to keep the soldiers alive. After all, they had human lives in their hands. A
responsibility he never had to endure. He felt nervous.
San and Asher were watching the battle unfold from behind the lines of the soldiers.
The army was divided into smaller divisons, every division held a dozen soldiers,
who would fight together as a unit and hold each others backs free. Whenever any of
the smaller divisions raised a blue flag, the team of medics were to dash over to them
and aid them as best as possible. Asher and San would take care of the badly
wounded, the rest would be brought back to the medic's tent in the warcamp for their
medical attention. Smaller cuts, brusies, broken bones and therelike would be treated
with less urgency. The deep cuts and high blood loss was what Asher had to deal
with.
"Keep calm," San said, noticing how nervous Asher was. "You're not gonna die today,
I promise. And something else, if you need to vomit, do it away from anybody. Don't
want any of your vomit on my soldiers, right?"
"Understood," he answered, tipping from one foot to the other. The lines were
forming up against each other. Highlord Rember standing with his personal guard and
his bannermen in the center of his army. From there he was going to give orders,
however that would work, Asher wasn't quite sure. All of the banners looked so alike,
but slightly different. To tell the difference in the battle must need years of training
and trust from everybody.
It started slow, but the armies rolled against each other with an inevitability Asher
found frightening. Finally metal clashed with metal, spears were thrusted, but Asher
could barely see what was going on. The rain made the armies recede in the
background.
"Left flank," San cried out and was already dashing towards it with high speed. His
clothes rattling through the wind, a bad of tools in his hand. Asher tried to keep up
but was falling behind. The muddy ground made him almost slip and fall.
A man was getting carried back through the ranks from the front line. His face
smeared with mud, his dark hair clinging to his head, the helmet gone completely. He
had deep cuts on his legs, the armor didn't hold back the blow completely. San was
already doing his work. Removing the part of the armor with a knife, cutting the
leather straps, which held the armor together. The piece fell down.
"Asher, sterilize the knife, now." And Asher did as told. He got out the knife from his
little bad and held it between his hands. San trying to stop the blood flow in the
soldier's leg. He wasn't concious anymore. The velvet blood flowing like little rivers
from the wound, the rain washing it away within seconds. The wound was filled with
pieces of grass and mud.
Asher commanded the flames within in to heat the blade in his hand. It was harder to
see that anything happened, a faint glow, which was not visibile in this weather was
all somebody could have seen. The metal slowly warming and getting some color.
Asher normally took more than half a minute to heat the blade enough to use it for
closing a wound with burning.
"Are you done yet?" San cried out again, looking at him. He held his hand out for the
blade, and after a few more seconds he gave San the blade. It glowed faintly red, it
must have been hot enough. Asher was sure of it, when he heard the sizzling of the
falling rain of the blade and when he saw San burn out the wound, to stop it from
bleeding. He worked fast and within seconds, he handed the blade back to Asher.
Asher felt himself recede into the background. This was suppose to be his time to
shine. He was suppose to help theses soldiers, he had learned so much. Was it all for
nothing?
"Keep it hot at all time," San shouted to him over the battlfield noise. Horns were
sounding now, which probably meant some formation changes, because the men in
front of Asher shuffled away. "I don't want to wait for it next time, we could lose the
next one. He is barely going to make it if we are lucky." The man was ash white in his
face, some other blue medics carrying him back to the warcamp.
Asher looked around him, still catching his breath from running before. Blue flags
raised from every side of the battlefield. They were too many. The battle most have
been going badly for the Highlord. San was already on the move, shouting back to
Asher to follow him. Asher tried to keep up, but again the nible medic was gaining
distance arriving at the next wounded soldier far before Asher would. He tried to gain
speed, but it was his downfall. He slipped on the muddy ground, losing grip of the
heated blade in his hands. It flew across and Asher lost track of where it landed. He
cursed but kept on walking.
Finally, when he met up with San he cried out for the knife already. "Are you rainin'
serious? You dropped it? Then get a new one quickly. Here." San gave Asher a new
blade from his bag, but the blade was cold. He commanded the flames again, but it
was too slow. He saw the men in front of him die in San's arms. He was frantically
trying to stop the bloodflow to the soldier's right arm. A deep cut watering the ground
with crimson liquid.
Asher saw it happen before him. The man slowly dying, with him trying to heat up
this blade in his hands. Was this really all he could do? Was this what he was
prepared for? Why learn all these medical skills, when he would never use them. It
made no sense. But he knew one thing he could do. One thing he was trying to forget,
so he didn't think of it. He remembered that day in Balin's barn with Ameline. He
heard her scream again. This time would be different though. This time he knew what
he was doing, and he knew what it was for.
Asher crouched down next to San and told him to step aside. San only shouted at him
to finally give him the blade, he was losing him. But Asher didn't listen. He held out
his hand and touched the wound with his bare hand. Just a quick touch not more, but
the flesh immidiately burned away, giving off that scent he had smelled before. It
made him sick, but he didn't back away. He again touched the wound with the side of
his hand, following the course of the wound. His hands were shaky and he burned
more than he needed too, but the wound closed. San staring at him. The other medics
getting the body from them and carrying the soldier away from the battlefield.
San shot Asher a look. He didn't look happy about the situation, but there was no
time. They had to go. They were needed somewhere else. Blue flags against the
heavy rain clouds guiding them towards the wounded. Asher finally found something
he could do with his curse.
