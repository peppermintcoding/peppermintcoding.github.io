Chapter 6


With thorny bushes gnawing at Malina's skin, she tried to make her way through the
thicket. Tears streaming down her cheeks like the rain from above. They were silent
tears, not a single whimper leaving her mouth. The clouds seemingly weeping with
her. She thought about making her way through these woods with Dago. Did they
only head north or stray from their path east or west? She didn't remember. She didn't
thought it would be important one day. But now it was.
She still felt his hands on her body. These rough hands of a stranger. Even after hours
of scrubbing her body with burning hot water the filth did not wash off her body. The
smell. Everything. She felt vomit coming up from her stomach but forcing it down.
She needed to push that memory away. As far away as possible in the deep, deep
dungeons of her mind. There they would be in good company, she supposed.
Where was the little tree village? She knew this wasn't a good plan, but where else
would she turn too? Where do you go when your home because not safe anymore, she
asked herself. She would probably die in these woods. Devoured by a sinbu, a giant
cat creature as dark as th night, left behind to starve with a broken leg or just
wandering around until her legs couldn't keep walking anymore. She would starve,
right here in these cloudy woods. For a moment she asked herself what might be the
best way to go. Starvation or devoured by a sinbu
Those hands. The images bubbled up to her consciousness again, but she repelled
them violently. Again she tried to force them down, this time deeper than before. She
errected walls between them, as high and thick as she could imagine them to be.
She kept on forcing her way further, leaving her old home behind with nothing but a
small spark of hope. Something she clung to like moths to the moons. Trees became
shorter, the way steeper with more rocks underneath the forest ground. She needed to
take extra care not to trip over fallen branches or stumble into holes in the ground.
Her legs beginning to burn and her lungs gasping for air. How much longer could she
take this?
And then there was this feeling again. The feeling she had when travelling with Dago.
Invisible eyes following her, hiding from her sight. Malina looked around her, turned
from side to side, searching for the origin of this feeling. She couldn't find it then and
she didn't find it now. She scanned the branches high up in the trees, nothing.
"Hello", she shouted into the empty forest. The rain a soft background noise. No
answer. It was probably just her fear lashing out. What a stupid idea it was. Why
would these savages treat her any different? She had nothing but the words of Dago, a
drug merchant, to go on. How pathetic. It didn't make any sense. She was about to
turn around, leave her fate in someone else's hands. Abandon the idea altogether. This
was no place for a Scarra after all. Her family was as old as the Highlord's, once
prestigious enough to mingle with the lords and ladies. Not anymore though. Who's
fault was that?
A body popped into existence from behind a tree, only a couple of metres in front of
Malina. It was woman with bow and arrows in her left hand. Her posture up right
with strong arms, much stronger than Malina's father ever looked. Her blonde hair
was unusual for one of the forest people. She didn't have one of her breast exposed
neither. Her clothing simple leather with the fur of a Sinba on her right shoulder.
"Lost your way little bird?", the stranger asked.
Malina mustered up all her confidence and strength, "I am looking for Karman. Is he
of your tribe? I wish to talk with him. It is important"
"About what matter would you like to speak to Karman, little bird?"
"The matters are between him and me. I wish not to speak about them with someone I
don't know."
"I see." The stranger waited. "I think you should turn back to where you belong. This
is no place for people like you."
Turn back? She couldn't and she wouldn't. He either accepted her or she would die
trying. This sense of finality gave her strength. When there is no way back, it is easier
to keep on going. "I won't turn back. You will lead me to Karman or you have to
shoot me down right here." She was surprised herself hearing the confidence in her
voice. A slight tremble was there for the ride as well.
The stranger smiled. "I see. You may follow me if you can. If not I won't have to
waste a good arrow on your fragile body." And with that the stranger walked on,
quickly Malina tried to catch up but the tempo was fast. Her legs weak and her breath
short.
"I am Malina", she shouted at the strangers back. "What is yours, if I may ask?"
"You may not."
"What should I call you then when I want to get your attention?"
"Persistence. I wouldn't have guessed to find a quality like that in you. You seem like
the type who gets their way on the first try at everything. I feel surprised."
"You should get to know me a bit more before jumping to conclusions then."
"Oh I know enough about you." Crushing through a thorny thicket, Malina found a
narrow trodden path before her. From here on walking was easier, and they quickened
their pace.
"And what would that be?"
"I know I want to save my breath and not waste it on you. So do us both a favor and
shut up before you draw the attention of something you wouldn't want to meet."
Malina bit her lip. The way the stranger woman talked reminded her of her father.
That dismissive tone, condescending. He brought that out whenever he was angry at
her for something, which happened more and more often.
After walking silently Malina and her unfriendly companion made it to the camp
safe. The woods cleared out in front of her and the busy small camp appeared in front
of her. But a fw people the camp was deserted. Had they moved again? The blond
stranger didn't stop like Malina had and went on straight towards on of the huts in the
trees. The last she was here Malina didn't notice how makeshift everything was. The
huts seemed to have been build in a rush, most of the unfinished, the conncetions
betweent mere branches to balance on to get from one hut to the next.
"Wait", Malina cried out.
The stranger stopped, "What?"
"I don't see Karman. Where is he?" A short moment of doubt, of anxiety. Could it be
that he had died?
"He is out hunting like everybody else. Life isn't as simple here. You will see that
soon enough. That over there is his hut." She pointed to one of the bigger ones,
almost in the centre of the whole village. Malina wasn't sure if it even was considered
a village with so few people. The only settlement she knew was Nigita. A city with
thousands of people and big buildings. Here she was surrounded by buildings fit to
hold maybe a couple of dozen people. "You may wait there if it pleases you, but don't
get too comfortable, you won't stay long I am sure."
A ladder leaned on the thick tree trunk leading up to the hut. Should she wait in his
home? Not without an invitition she shouldn't. Or should she? Malina didn't know the
customs of how to act around these people. She only learned how to do so in Nigita,
not with these people.
Malina decided to wait under the trees in one of the seating areas. It was the same
Dago and her had sat some time ago. How long had it been? A few people were
walking around the camp with baskets full of things Malina couldn't make out.
Nobody bothered her though. She was sheltered by a few giant plant leaves so the
rain wouldn't soak the logs she sat on.
After waiting for hours, the sun close to hitting the horizon, Malina heard commotion
all around the camp. The hunters were coming back from their day's work. Men as
well as women made their way to the clearing in the middle of the camp. Some of
them carrying game bount to thick branches. They carried their food on their
shoulders. Again, Malina was uncomfortable looking at the specific nude spots. It
was no sin to show skin for woman of class in Nigita, but to show a whole breast or
even their necks was unheard of. Feeding children in public was allowed if the breast
was covered from stranger's eyes, but fully exposed with rings and rods through the
nipple. It made her blush.
At the front of the group Karman walked proudly with head held high, the nose ring
reflecting the shallow sun rays. Some of the people looked at Malina funny, but most
did not care it seemed. Karman made his way towards her, putting his bow to the side
and sitting down.
"I see you have returned", he said. His black hair clutching to his forehead. "How did
you find us here? I hope you didn't stumble upon our camp by accident."
"No. I was following a woman, blond hair, Sinba fur on her arm. Sadly, she never
gave me her name."
"Ah, it is Aelana you speak of. What a funny conincidence it is for her to guide you to
our camp."
"Why is that?"
"You see", he leaned forward, "she is like you in a way. She came from the lowlands
wanting to make a living out here. We accepted her into our tribe, almost with no
difficulty. She has been with us ever since. I was just a little boy when she came. She
keeps to herself a lot but she is the best hunter I have ever seen in my life."
A woman with black braids along her head and green eyes came over to the two. She
wore several rings on her ears and left nipple. Her face cold and fixed on Malina.
"What is the meaning of this, Karman", she asked.
"This is Malina, our guest." His voice was calm but his body uneasy.
"Look at her, she can barely walk by herself. Your five year old is more capable than
her. You can't be seriously considering to accept her?"
"Tara, that is enough", Kalman with raised voice. "You do not decide in these matters.
Responsibility falls on the chieftain, have you forgotten who that is?"
"No", Tara conceded and looked down. "Apologies. Do as you see fit." With one
poisonous glance to Malina she left the two alone.
"I have to apologize for her behaviour. Times are tough for us and we have barely
enough to feed our own. But tell me, what brings you to us?"
Malina hesitated. Should she tell him everything? No, he wouldn't want to hear her
sob story. She needed to show him what she needed. "I want to stay with you. I have
turned my back on the lowlands and I don't plan to go back."
"I see", Karman said and stroked his beard. "I guess Dago put the idea in your head
then, eh?"
Malina's stomach turned. This didn't sound good at all. "He mentioned it, yes." Her
mouth as dry as sand.
"We do not discriminate in who can join our tribe as a lot of the others do, but we do
have rules to follow. One is, everybody needs to pull some of the weight for the
group. We have children and old people to care about. Aelana came to us and
promised she could bring something back every day. And she did. The question is,
what can you do for us?"
The question echoed in Malina's head. What could she do? She learned how to smile
at men to attract their interest but that wouldn't help her here. She knew seventeen
different dances, at least two for every occasion you could think of. Four of the just
for the harvest festivals. Then she knew something.
"I can write", she said.
"Write?", Karman looked skeptical. "You mean you can draw sounds?"
"Yes, I can draw sounds. If there is a word I know the symbol for it. My father made
me practice since I have been four years old." And failure was punished severely. She
rubbed her hands.
"It means you can also read the symbols if I were to give you some arrangement?"
His blue eyes lit up. "We treasure old stone tables with for us unknown symbols
engraved on them. They are said to be from when lowlanders and we were of the
same people. United not divided. I wish to know what is written so we can
understand better. But all I asked didn't know what to make of them. Well, we only
had men like Dago to ask, but now this could change."
"I will gladly take a look and decypher the stone tablets. If that means I can stay?"
She was unsure if this was a good idea. Malina knew all the symbols, she learned
them for years, but if Karman was not exaggerating, these stone tablets were old.
Ancient even. What if the symbols had changed since then? She would need a way to
figure it out anyway. This was her only way in.
"Splendid", Karman grinned. His whole face seemed to be flooded with joy. It was
important to him. "Then there is only the question of shelter for the night. Hm.
Aelana is the only one living alone and we don't have time to build a new hut. So I
guess we should go and ask her."
"Aelana. No, no. She didn't seem to like me at all. She will never say yes to this."
Malina was mistaken. Aelana accepted with a shrug, she was there only for sleeping
anyway. She didn't seem to care at all.
The hut was more than simple. Hanging up in the trees there wasn't much space at all.
The two women had just enough place to fit too beds in. But they couldn't be called
beds. Back at her father's house, she had a worm and soft feather bed with thick wool
blankets and pillows. Lots and lots of pillows. Some small some bigger, all with
different stitchings. Here in the forest, all she had was some animal fur to roll around
her body. They slept on the wooden floor with nothing between them.
On the wall, Aelana had a hung her weapons. A short bow for hunting with several
arrows and two swords. Their bend blades sheathed in black leather. One was almost
as short as a normal knife, Malina had never seen a blade that short, but she didn't
know about warfare to begin with.
Aelana caught Malina's eyes glancing over the blades, "If they go missing, I promise
to make you die a slow death. Don't touch them and we are fine."
Malina nodded, terrified to live with her without any walls between them.
The only things she brought with her out here were a golden bracelet from her mother
with a tiny ruby and a thin blanket she snatched from the servants quaters. At least
she had something to keep a bit warmer at night. Her blue raincoat hung at the
entrance, water dropping from the sleeves.
"Come on little bird", Aelana said. "It's time for evemeal." And so the two women
climbed down form the tree, which was quite the task for Malina. The height wasn't
the problem, but the small ladder swayed a bit and creaked like it would break every
minute. But it didn't.
Over a big crackling fire, flames fighting against the soft rain from above, the tribe's
people cooked their evemeal. There were berries of all colors, most Malina had never
seen in her life. Meat from a big deer and a stew made from roots and all sorts of
other foraged goods blubbered in a big cauldron. The atmosphere was light and some
were singing. Back with her father's evemeals were very different. They were silent,
awkward and devoid of life. Servants bringing fancy meals, which weren't special at
all. It was all about appearances. The cook was instructed to make the simple food
look special.
"Malina", Karman shouted over the singing, "come sit with us." He pointed towards
an empty place next to him. A little boy sitting on his lap, who looked like his son.
Two other women were sitting to his left and a giant men on the other side. He had a
braided beard and little iron rings at the end of them. His head shaven to the naked
skin. Malina still wearing her own clothes, hiding her neck from view. Looking very
out of place.
She sat down between the shaven giant and Karman, food on wooden plates in their
laps. The stew smelled delicious.
"You have aquainted yourself with Aelana a bit more, I hope", Karman said.
"I wouldn't say that", Malina said. "She seems to me like someone who doesn't like
company much."
The giant on her right laughed, "Did she threaten you yet?", he asked. His voice twice
as loud as any others. He was almost shouting.
"In fact she already did. I am not to touch her belongings."
"Don't worry too much about it. That woman scares the rainin' woods out of me." It
made Malina laugh. This giant of a man, arms twice as big as her thighs almost was
afraid of that woman? Aelana was strongly build but she didn't compare to this man.
"But where are my manners, we have a real lowlander lady among us. Let me
introduce myself, my name is Choco." He took Malina's hand and gave her a soft kiss
on her fingers. She blushed, trying to smile but she felt uncomfortable shifting in her
seat. "Here take some." He gave her some of the meat with his knife. Malina took it
and put it in her mouth. She was starving and the food was delicious. She got her own
plate, filled with berries and nuts.
The other people started talking again, almost forgetting Malina was there at all. It
was fine for her. Fading into the background was good. She didn't need any special
attention.
Out of the corner of her eye she saw Choco raise his arm and Malina flinched and
gave off a high pitched shout. She held up her arms in defence in front of her face.
The other four people falling silent within a heartbeat.
Malina's heart beating fast she forced herself out of the defensive posture. She felt
ashamed, it was only a reflex. Choco looking at her with disbelief.
"Did you think I would hit you?", he asked, eyes wide with, what was it, horror?
"My apologies", she said. "It was only a reflex. I wasn't thinking at all."
"I don't know how your life has been", Karman chipped in, "but around here, noone
raises their hands towards someone. We do not accept violence without justification."
"My apologies if I have scared you", Choco said honestly.
"It is me who has to apologise", Malina said. "I wasn't thinking ill of you. Apologies."
The rest of the evemeal went quieter with less gesturing around and Malina felt at
fault for it. As the night grew darker she excused herself from the others and went
towards Aelana's hut. Half way there, shrouded in darkness, too far from the fires to
see much, a shadow materialized in front of her.
"You might have Karman wrapped around your little weak fingers", the shadow said
with familiar voice. "Lowlanders always bring trouble. I will be watching you." It
was Tara the woman who spoke up to Karman. Without anything else she vanished
silently.
