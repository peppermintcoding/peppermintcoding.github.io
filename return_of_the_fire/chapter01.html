Prologue


"Can I trust you?", she asked.
"You don't have to", the man said. His wet, black leather armor shone with the light
of hundreds of candles spread across the room. His face shrouded by a hood.
"Everybody is in place. We never misses our target. You know that. Everybody will
know it before long. We will look after everything when you are gone."
The woman walked over to the middle of the throne room, her red hair swinging from
side to side. It was a circular room, with a high hanging ceiling, where twenty men
could comfortably stand on each others shoulders. The everlasting fire nurtured by
the woman herself feeding of the wood that has been constantly supplied by her
servants for twenty-five years. Raindrops falling from a hole in the ceiling onto the
fire and turned into steam with a sizzling sound. She reached with her hand into the
fire, her back to the man in black.
It didn't hurt. It calmed her. This decision was the right one, or was it? Would it really
make the world a better place or was it all in her mind? Would it even work? She
knew of what they were capable off but still, a drop of doubt remained.
"How do you want it to end?", he asked. They were alone. The words hanging in the
room like smoke. She turned around facing him straight on, her eyes covered by a red
strip of cloth. Underneath her eyes were empty, like her mind was. Did she want it all
to end? Could she throw all the planning away, the sacrifices she had to make and not
go through with it? Could she betray the Onkari and everyone else just like she
betrayed the others?
No. This was for the better of everyone. This was bigger than her or her sisters and
brothers.
"Tell me, do you know the story of Hardulan and Siphone?", she asked. The fire
behind her fading further and further to darkness.
"A love story if I recall correctly", he answered. "Like Siphone then?"
A monent of doubt washed across her. So intense, so strong she held her breath. Her
mind walked throught the reasons over and over again. A second passed. She was
sure. She was calm. It would be for the best. It was her legacy she could offer the
world.
"Like Siphone then."
