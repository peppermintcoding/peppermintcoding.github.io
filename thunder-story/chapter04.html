Chapter 3


The air was still cold and wet when the Professor was loading up the wagons. In the distance the
thunderstorm was still raging on sending thunder through his bones, the sun about to crest the
horizon and paint the world in a warmer color. Lightning lanterns illuminating the caravan and
street. They would soon have to give up on lightning power when they went over the border.
Hopefully the few men he paid to follow his instructions of documenting the thunderstorms would
carry out their task with a certain seriousness, but he doubted it. The sapphire cube around his neck
was freshly charged with lightning and the current was dancing in seemingly random patterns
within the gem. It was so mesmerizing that the Professor often spent hours watching it circling
inside the gem. It always seemed to him, the lightning had a mind of its own. Wanting to escape it's
blue prison and be reunited with its brethren in nature. When lightning hit the earth it vanished
instantly without leaving any trace for the most part. It might char some wood or set a tree on fire
and he even saw it strike sand and turn it into dirty glass. But where would it go after that?
Somehow it was unthinkable that this pure energy was just vanishing, but when channeled into a
sapphire it was trapped inside ready for use. So many mysteries they had no answer too and he had
to go on this useless almost suicidal trip around the grasslands to find something he knew was
impossible to find out there.
The city felt empty out here by the wall. Down near the harbor the streets were already being
plucked up by the sheer amount of traders and workers running around. Trade by ship was way
more lucrative, fast and safe than trading with the tribes of the grassland. So close to the outer wall
he felt a strong loneliness emanating from the city itself. Looking around at his new companions he
was even more disappointed, no to put it into the right words, crushed. The Professor knew of the
importance of every craft and he didn't see his own profession as something higher or more
important. But he couldn't shake the feeling, he was unwelcome between these men and women. It
was rather obvious how they eyed him from the side, had to help him with basic tasks like securing
the cargo onto the wagons or making sure everything was covered by a tarp against the rain. They
were shipping all sorts of cargo, from crafted tools and jewelry to dried meat, rice or other food,
which could be conserved for a longer period of time. But these surely wasn't his people. The
conversation were bland and revolving around their uninteresting work. So little actual planning or
optimization went into their trips it was almost comical if it wasn't so serious. And if he wasn't a
part of them now. How for lightning's sake could he survive this for half a year?
It now pained him even more he had to leave Clair behind. He knew it was selfish to think about
this part of the whole situation, but he couldn't help it. He wasn't sure how she would fair in Shima
without the income and shelter from him, but he knew she would manage somehow. She was way
smarter than anyone would guess when they saw her first, especially given her background. What a
wasted talent, he thought sometime. She could have even surpassed him, if she would have gotten
the proper education he had gotten when he was a child. He was concerned anyways though.
Without her comments and talks to keep his mind occupied he felt lonely, like he hadn't felt for
quite some time. But there was no more he could do for her and he had to look forward to his
journey. He fastened the last rope on his wagon and turned towards his fellow merchants. Their
whole caravan was waiting just outside the North Gate of Shima. It was towering over them with
the huge walls to either side. It was build for over ten years by thousands or people. Sadly, in that
time there were still slaves who had to build it. There was great literature written by former slaves
working on the walls. Full of hardships and abuse, but also strangely positive. But he was dreaming
away again. Focus Cy, he told himself.
For the moment he only knew the head merchant Aratos and his wife Calandi, who were the ones
giving orders and seeing over the whole process. Aratos was a small rather heavy guy with dark
locks and a long beard. Like all merchants he was wearing the typical small golden nose rings to
identify as one of the three great merchant guilds. His wife Calandi had long, open raven hair and
was much taller than her husband. The only other feature the Professor could really distinguish from
her was her rather big nose. It was unusually long. The other two dozen men were either lower
merchants or soldiers, armed and ready to protect the caravan if necessary. But if push came to
shove the Professor was sure they wouldn't hold out for long. Most weapons had rust on them, their
shields had cracks and dents. At one point he heard basically all their names, but he didn't bother to
memorize any of them. He wouldn't have to engage with them much anyways so why bother, he
thought to himself.
The Professor walked up to Calandi to get something else to do. Standing around idly was not
something he was fond of doing. "Is there anything else I can help you with, Calandi?" he asked
politely.
Calandi quickly turned around to him seeming a little flustered. "Oh Professor, er I mean Cy. I sure
hope I won't mess it up for too much longer, you have to forgive me I am not the fastest thinker."
You don't say. "Oh that is quite alright. You are doing a fine job", he reassured her. "But is there
nothing I can lend you my aid for? I feel rather useless. Everybody is doing at least something."
"Well", she looked around when the Professor noticed a little clipboard in her right hand. She
followed his gaze and looked down at the papers on her board. "Ah yes, I was checking up on our
inventory and was doing some overhead calculating of what we still need to buy for supplies in the
outer cities. You could check my numbers if you are interested. I presume you are even more trained
with numbers than I am." Was she starting to blush, the Professor asked himself. He wasn't sure
why.
"I would be delighted to give my mind something to do. If I may?" Calandi offered him the
clipboard and a refined charcoal pencil. It didn't need more than a quick glance to see what kind of
mess this woman had made on the paper. Nothing was lined up, a big table with croaked lines,
comments everywhere and half of the words were messily crossed out because she made a mistake
or thought of something else. He let out a short burst of amused laughter, which he regretted
instantly.
"Is something the matter?" Calandi asked him. Strangely enough she didn't sound offended in the
slightest and when the Professor looked up from the messy paper he saw no trace of hurt feelings,
but something he could only interpret as fear. "I know it's probably not up to your standards and my
apologies, but it was a last minute sketch up. I normally don't do things this way, but we had some
last minute changes coming up with your arrival and your companion and then-"
"My what?" the Professor stuttered.
"Your companion?" she asked again. The Professor looked at her puzzled. "Oh I was informed by a
royal messenger, a very impressive woman I have to say, that we will be joined by a companion of
yours when we get near the border. Some sort of helper? I don't really remember, she was quite
intimidating and I thought it was all cleared up with you before hand."
"Yes, yes", he said. "I remember now. I am sorry to have startled you. I wasn't thinking right. Must
have slipped my mind for a minute." Were they giving him a babysitter or was this some different
plot he had no knowledge of yet?
"Oh no problem, Professor, er Cy. Oh I am such a slow bird sometimes. You are not at fault here."
Getting back to the papers the Professor made some changes of certain numbers and totals. She had
definitely problems keeping track of the carry with additions and she sometimes forgot to add
proper estimates. He was no merchant, but a hundred gold pieces per sack of rice was at least ten
times as expensive as it should be. Even in these times where the agriculture wasn't exactly
flourishing. He handed it back to her with a smile, "I made some changes. Just a couple of little
mistakes with the numbers, but nothing neck breaking."
Calandi looked back at him with big eyes. Looked down at the paper and back up. "You went
through all of that in a few seconds?" she asked sounding like a child almost. "I have to apologize
again, but what we heard of you wasn't putting you in a good light at all. But this is beyond
amazing. I have to.. wait here for a moment, I will be right back." She left the Professor standing
there puzzled and amused. Amazed by simple addition, subtraction and multiplication. What would
their evening conversations be about, he asked himself. Repeating old tales and legends? A lot of
silence? Talking about their faiths? Pathetic.
And then it got into his mind. What would he do for the whole day marching through empty country
side? Watching the vegetation and animal life wouldn't satisfy him for more than a few minutes or
an hour a day. Would he succumb to making small talk with the other merchants or soldiers?
"Clair..", the Professor sighed looking from one faceless soldier to the next. They all looked and felt
the same, like empty vessels. But rather than them, vessel were at least useful for something.
"How often did I tell you, I hate it when men sigh my name like that." Turning around the Professor
couldn't even contain the grin if he wanted to. But he absolutely didn't. Clair was walking up to him,
wearing a thick coat with the hood drawn back exposing her short, pitch black hair and caramel
colored skin.
"Do you come here to torture me with fake hope or are you actually considering joining me?" He
sounded a bit too hopeful.
"Even if that could be rather funny, I am here to join you. Can't let you wander around on your own
out there, can I? And who will be the mediator between these guys", she gestured towards the other
men and women surrounding the caravan, "and you. I sure hope you didn't insult all of them
already, did you?"
"I actually think the merchants wife, Calandi, has found quite the liking to me actually. But Clair",
he got serious again, "what about-"
"I have thought about it this last week. And what do you always say, facts don't lie. I put it all
together like you always do. I can't say it yet out loud, but we both know the truth. It's just easier to
hold on to hope, even when there is none left for the rational mind. I have not forsaken him though
and I will continue my search, but only after we have returned." The Professor could see how much
it took out of her to say this, it must have been even more to come to this conclusion. Her brother
San was all she could think about, every single day. But she was right, they both knew it for a long
time. She picked up her gaze from the ground and looked at him with a scheming smile, "Won't you
say anything to stop me, Professor? I mean we are venturing through dangerous territory. Don't you
want to tell me to stay here where it's safe?"
"Don't be ridiculous. This city is probably twice as dangerous as the grassland if we are being
honest. And if I die on this trip I sure hope I don't have to do it alone." He smiled. She smiled back.
"But what are we going to do about the virus?" she asked.
"I have taken all the instruments and supplies we need to do some basic experiments. But I doubt
we will be successful without a lot of help from the tribe's healers. And we can't count on that at all.
Understandably they hold a little grudge against us people."
"So we are fucked."
"Nicely put. To be more precise, the nation is fucked, if we don't find a miraculous way of making
this work."
"Well, if anyone can do it, then the great Professor himself, isn't that right?"
Instead of answering, the Professor looked over the city sprouting over the several hills it was built
upon. The sun was coming up and reflecting off the colored roofs. He didn't have a strong
relationship with this city, the only friend he had was standing next to him about to leave. But the
thought of seeing it fall into chaos let knots grow in his stomach. It was the closest thing he had to a
home and even the closest Clair had to a home. A pathetic thought, that they had nothing more than
this Gods forsaken city.

The wagons rattled over the bumpy cobblestone road next to Clair. The air felt unusually clean and
fresh. It was good to be outside the confines of the giant capital. The wind was brushing her short
hair and cheeks. To her disliking, they had to walk the whole way, because all of the wagons were
reserved for cargo and the few bulls they had were already at their maximum capacity, heaving
under the immense weight they had to pull. She hoped they would make it back and not break a leg
somewhere around the road and end up being made into food for the travelers. Right next to her a
black bull was slowly walking forward. She put out her hand caressing his head earning a satisfied
him from the giant animal. She checked the straps running all around his body, seeing if they didn't
cut into his skin at any point.
Looking back at the city, Clair felt a big emptiness in her heart. Searching the wall she wasn't
disappointed to see a little figure standing on top the battlements. Even from the distance she saw
ice blue eyes piercing her gaze. She felt tears running down her cheeks without noticing they had
been starting to form. Slowly the figure started walking forward closer to the edge. She could her
his voice inside her head, calling for her, begging her to help him. To safe him. I am hurt big sister,
he cried out, please. I need you.
You aren't real, she told herself trying to close her eyes, but not succeeding. She couldn't help but
watch her brother get closer to the edge. This was all her fault. She should have been more careful,
should have been less naive. Why did she trust that slug Sakara. Her sweet words were nothing but
poison. No wonder they called her, the Viper.
"Is he there?" the Professor asked, touching her lightly on the shoulder and bringing her back to
herself.
"Yes", she whispered back.
He put his arm around her shoulders and pulled her gently to the side, so she couldn't look back
anymore, but was forced to see ahead onto the road. "The best thing we can do for him is go
forward with our research. The plague will hit the poorer districts first, where the rats roam free."
"I know", she said trying to compose herself again. "But my mind keeps telling me otherwise. I can
still hear his voice", her voice broke abruptly. At least the nightmares were getting less extreme and
there were nights she would sleep more than two hours.
"What would you think if we started some drama around here?" he asked all of the sudden.
She looked at him sideways. Weirdly enough his crude manners were always helping her in these
situations. Everything occupying her mind instead of this was something she couldn't say no to.
"What kind of drama do you want to start? You know we have to travel with these people for quite a
while. It wouldn't be wise to upset them and make enemies, would it?"
"Simple minded Clair. Have you forgotten my unbelievable genius. My plans never fail, do they?"
"One time you were sent to prison. Then once you almost lost your job and your students had to beg
their parents to buy you back your job. Oh, and then there was the time you-"
"Alright, alright", he laughed, but his eyes were sparkling with enthusiasm. "Only this time we are
in a different position. These people aren't, let's say, the brightest sapphires in the world, are they? I
am sure we can get away with a lot. Especially because they all think I am a renowned scientist and
professor. Who would dare to put the blame on someone like me?"
"Great. Then they will just blame me. It was hard enough to get them to accept me coming with
you. We even have to buy some extra supplies still to have enough. I don't want to press Calandi
even more. Your charm isn't strong enough to make her stand against her own husband."
"You already gave me a great idea, my friend. Indeed my charm normally isn't on par with most
other men, but with her it seems different. Not only is she impressed by my intelligence, but I have
the impression she is also interested in more than just my brain. I noticed her eyes keep wandering
when she thinks I am not looking."
"Come on", Clair exclaimed all too loud. "You can't be serious. What are you seeing already again. I
thought I am the only one with delusions and hallucinations." Big sister, a whisper riding on the
edges of the wind to her ears. It sounded like the rustling of leaves and not like a voice, but it was
unmistakably San's.
"Watch. Closely." He started walking faster to catch up with Calandi, who was walking at the top of
the caravan. In total Clair counted about three dozen wagons rattling over the royal road. The
surrounding people were walking in little groups of not more than three, talking or walking in
silence. The bulls were breathing heavily. Clair went past the little groups to catch up with the
Professor slowly. She wanted to stay out of sight, but still hear what he would say to her.
"Is it alright if I join you for a bit, Calandi?" the Professor asked when he finally caught up with her.
She was rather tall for a woman and wasn't looking like a child next to her, in contrary to Clair
herself.
"Oh, it's you Professor, I mean Cy of course. I will get it down, don't you worry one bit." She did
seemed flustered already.
"I have no doubt you will get it down in no time." Clair could hear the smile in his voice. Where did
he take that confidence to talk to a stranger like this? "I was only wondering, what you were
thinking about?" Clair's eyes rolled so far back she must have looked like a demon. "Walking
through this empty countryside is beginning to bore me. My company isn't a great help either. So I
kept wondering what you might be thinking about just now?" When he finished I will punch him in
the stomach, she thought. She would go for the face, but it sadly was out of reach for her little arms.
"Oh, you were wondering about me?" Her voice went unusually high. "But isn't she like your
student? Wouldn't that make her a way better person to talk to then the simple minded merchant
wife I am?"
"On the contrary", the Professor reassured her, "she might be my disciple, but that is the problem.
She is still learning and growing up, a mere child if we are being honest for a moment. I love her
with all my heart. She is like family to me and family is so, so important, wouldn't you agree?"
She nodded frantically. "But I often long for a more adult conversation, with proper topics. I don't
want to be the teacher all the time. It gets so exhausting."
"I totally understand. I have two children myself and it gets really annoying and troublesome to be
the parent all the time. So but, oh, what was I thinking about? I don't really know." She knew very
well, Clair was sure, but she was trying to think of something more interesting to impress the
Professor. "I was just thinking about random things, you know? Like, what is the purpose of our
lives? I often get these kind of thoughts when I am walking through the country on our trips, you
know?"
"A discussion about the very nature of our existence, how fascinating you would think about
something like this in this empty landscape. I knew my gut feeling about you was right. You know
what they say about us scholars, as real truth-seekers we can't ignore our gut feeling. And am I
allowed to hear the conclusion you came to regarding the purpose of our existence? I am dying to
hear."
"Oh, but isn't it in the nature of the question", she began her pathetic pseudo intellectual answer.
Clair didn't like how she was changing things about herself to impress someone else. You shouldn't
play the part for someone else and forsake yourself. It never works out anyways, so why bother. She
had seen it everywhere, but especially when working for Sakara, the Viper. "That you can't really
find an answer to the question. I think only the Gods know the answer to that question. They are the
ones who created everything and therefore also the purpose of us. But I am interested to hear your
own thoughts of course. They are probably way more thoughtful and not as simple as mine."
"It actually hurts me physically how you put yourself down here. Don't let anyone tell you, you are
too simple to think about these kind of questions. Even if someone doesn't agree with you, it's no
reason to think your view of the world is any less sophisticated than theirs. I for my part-" Sadly the
Professor was interrupted by Aratos, Calandis husband and head merchant of the caravan. Busted,
Clair thought. This didn't look good. He was scowling at the Professor.
"What are you two talking about?" he grunted at his wife.
"Oh, Aratos. Nothing really. Just some small talk for passing the time." She was casting her gaze
down towards the ground. So the Professor actually was right, she felt exposed. Clair couldn't
refrain from smiling, only barely keeping herself from laughing out loud.
Another grunt from Aratos. "I need your help with something. One of the ledgers is wrong, I want
you to look over it again before we arrive in Jorda."
"And I will keep you no longer. It was a pleasure to talk with you, Calandi. Until next time." The
Professor slowed down and starting walking next to Clair again.
"Have I promised too much?" he asked. No he hadn't. "The bait is thrown out."

It was almost nightfall when they finally arrived in the smaller outskirt town of Jorda. The Professor
had made a few more attempts of conversation with Calandi and was shut down by her husband
every time, but he could already feel the tension growing. It was getting interesting definitely.
"You seem more tense than usual", Clair said from the side. She was having her hood over her head,
the air was getting cold and even the Professor was feeling a bit chilly. "Are you afraid you have to
go further than just talk with Calandi after you led her on so much?"
"On the contrary, I think I won't be able to go through with it at all. It's getting more and more
difficult to keep myself contain."
"Can you elaborate?"
"Did you notice how often she says 'oh'?" he asked.
"Oh, I did notice", Clair laughed. The laughter was making the Professor relax for a moment. He
knew it was wrong to play with Calandi's feelings like this, but to cheer Clair up even a bit he
would do way worse things without hesitating for a second.
"I thought it would be fun to talk with her, but in actuality, it gets my insides boiling. I can't take her
pseudo talk anymore. It's not even that I find her dumb or bland, but if you don't have the capacity
or experience with certain things, why fake it for some stranger? I mean I fake interest in her
thoughts, alright, but at leasts I don't make up stuff."
"And here I thought you finally found the woman of your dreams. Snatching her away from a sad
and unfulfilled marriage, riding into the sunset on your bull led caravan. Staring into each other's
eyes and confessing your undying love for each other."
The Professor scowled at her. "I rather die alone than settle for her."
The little city of Jorda was building up before them. The road was still in top shape out here, but the
houses were tiny in comparison to the capital. The wall was made out of mud and wood and barely
high enough to provide safety from people with basic climbing skills. A couple watch towers were
sprinkled around the perimeter with lightning sapphire lamps giving up their cool pulsating light.
"Did you know I was here once before?"
"Really? How come?"
"My parents took me here when I was, hm, maybe about five or so. I remember because there was a
spring festival here and my father had some contractor work he needed to take care of while my
mother, my and baby San were enjoying the festival. In my memory it was so much bigger, even
though I knew the capital well by then."
The Professor would have liked to stroll around the little city and see what she remembered from
her childhood. But he was too afraid of ripping open some old wounds. This was a precious
moment all together, her speaking of her parents at all was so rare he was delighted it happened at
all. But with not knowing what to answer he stayed quiet.
They walked on next to the caravan seeing light in the surrounding houses, mostly pulsating
sapphires. The Professor looked around and saw on a small elevation further towards the heart of
Jordan a high tower gleaming in the last sun rays. Their lightning rod was nothing compared to the
ones inside the capital, but it was a beauty nonetheless. A perfectly straight iron rod stretching far
into the sky, about thirty meters the Professor guessed, ending probably in a building at the bottom.
He couldn't see it from here, but it sure was there. It was where the sapphires and lightning rod were
connected via some more cables. It was a fascinating process watching the gems charge during a
thunderstorm. With every lightning hitting the rod the bunch of sapphires connected to it would
light up tremendously, several times brighter than they would be when fully charged even, and then
sort of cool of slowly and stabilizing until they were charged fully. Without any use they could last
for weeks giving off their cold, blue light.
"Calandi isn't the reason you are so tense, is it?" Clair's voice cut him out of his thoughts. He almost
forgot she was there for a moment and jumped at her sound. He couldn't see her face with the hood
over her head.
"No it isn't", he admitted. The merchant's wife was a welcome distraction not only for Clair, but for
him as well. He still was unsure who this mystery companion would be, he was supposedly meeting
here in Jordan. He didn't understand the need for a bodyguard especially, because the Professor was
quite sure, Zalan was loving the idea of him dying somewhere out in the grasslands. There were
enough dangers besides raids and bandit tribes, but he was actually having to look for some form of
antidote he would need to befriend some of the tribe's healers. Getting close to them wasn't easy and
he didn't have much knowledge at all about their culture or customs. It was dangerous, but a single
man wouldn't turn the tides in any of these cases. Maybe he was there to keep taps on the Professor
and Clair? Was he there to stab him in the back somewhere way out in the no man's land? It was
unsettling, and not in a good way.
"We will be getting some company, send from the royal treasury, or to be more exact, from Zalan. I
think we will meet him in the inn we are staying tonight. It is unnerving me."
"Do you think they send him with us to kill you?" she asked bluntly. He was impressed, the same
thought came to her, too. "Or us both maybe?"
"Well, I wouldn't worry about your life, if I were you. He will be struck by your unbelievable
beauty and intellect. He won't even be able to lift a finger to hurt you, without a doubt. I think the
biggest danger you are facing from him, is him not being able to contain his love."
"If it's only love I won't mind so much, but with most men it's lust they have a problem to contain",
she said with a bitter undertone.
Aratos came up to them walking along the caravan. It looked like they were coming up on the
market place in the center of the settlement. "The wagons, animals and a few men for protections
will camp on the south side of the city. We will be staying at the Sapphire Rose tonight. You can go
ahead with most of the men and let them know we are coming. I don't need you to make the caravan
safe for the night." He almost said the last remark as an insult it appeared to the Professor.
"Very well", he said trying to sound as nice as possible, "we will try to get you a place close to the
fire with some warm stew or whatever they are best at."
He earned a grunt from Aratos, who was already leaving only listening with half an ear probably. It
was almost comical how he started hating his guts.
"I guess you two won't become friends any time soon", Clair said.
"What a shame. Let's go and see what this inn is like. I am dying for something to eat, aren't you."
They followed the rest of the men down towards a bigger building made from mostly a rich brown
wood with a cobblestone base. The windows were a dark blue tint, which where the inn probably
got it's name from. It made the sapphire lanterns shining from inside even more blue. Entering
through the front door, the air smelled of smoke, good food and sweat. Just like any other inn worth
its salt, the Professor thought. The inn would barely fit all of the caravan's people inside, but luckily
for them not many from the town folk were inside anyways. Must have cleared up for them
beforehand.
"Are you alright, Clair?"
"I don't like the air", she answered removing her hood and unbuttoning her coat. The Professor did
it just the same. "But I will be alright, I think. I would prefer a table on the edges though, if that is
possible."
"Certainly." The Professor walked through the open dining room, two fires crackling at the far side
of the room giving off a nice warm feeling. Behind the counter the innkeeper was already filling up
mugs with ale. He was a burly old man with a long, but well kept beard. It went down far beyond
his chest and was cut off by the counter. How far did it go down?
"Greetings, what are you serving tonight for some hungry travelers?" the Professor asked leaning
onto the counter.
"Welcome to our humble inn. My name is Harlo, the gentle Bear. Please make yourself at home in
the renowned Shining Sapphire. You are in luck, my wife has been preparing our famous sheep stew
with fresh vegetables and as dessert some sweet pancakes with butter. How does that sound to you
two?" Humble, but renowned? the Professor asked himself.
"Sounds delicious. We will be seated at the corner over there."
"My daughters will be right there with your food. Relax and enjoy the fire and some good ale." He
offered them two mugs, which Clair happily took from him without another blink.
Suddenly, the other men from their caravan started a commotion over to his left. The Professor and
Clair were trying to look what was going on when two small young women emerges from the
cluster of weary men. Both had long braided black hair hanging down way beyond their waist even.
They must be the daughters the innkeeper had been speaking of. They both were wearing tight
fitting traditional hanuka dresses. A form of dress where a high slit was on the right side of the
woman giving sight to undergarment, most commonly blue stockings. To balance the symmetry the
left arm had a long sleeve going half over the hand and the right arm was barely covered half way
towards the elbow.
"Would you look at that", Clair exclaimed, "twins." The Professor had to look again. They were
looking exactly identically. He hadn't even noticed. Same eyes, nose, stature, even hairstyle. He was
so hung up on their clothing and entrance, that he hadn't noticed the obvious. Fascinating to find
identical twins out here in this little town indeed. "Sakara would weep with joy finding them here.
Let's go and let them have their fun." She went off towards the table they had observed before.
The Professor followed, but not after taking another good look at the twins trying to entertain the
new visitors. It was going to rain tips tonight, he was sure of it. Quite the rare find it was indeed.
What kind of knowledge one could gain from perfectly identical people, the asked himself. Hard to
say.
Clair sat down at their little table, facing the stone wall with one of the blue stained windows at her
left side. She liked it more to not have to see all the people in the room and given the tavern was
filled to the brim already, awaiting even more when Aratos and his helping crew arrived. To the
Professor it was a mystery how it felt better to have them all in her back rather than in front of her
and being able to see what they were up to. But everyone was indeed different, he thought.
The food arrived shortly after, brought by both of the beautiful twins. Now that he had a better look,
he was sure, they were not only beautiful, but stunning. The hanuka dress fit perfectly down to
every single curve of their bodies. Their heavy braids in perfect shape and hazel eyes unusually
large. One of the talents the Professor prided himself on, was his skill to get the overall intellect
from a person by just a quick glance at how they act or talk. But these twins were a complete

The mystery men looked around and for a moment the two locked eyes and the man stopped,

was happening. He looked at Clair's eyes and saw giant dilated pupils, sweat forming on her
forehead, breathing heavily and her tiny hand clasping the knife so tight her knuckles were snow
white, was shaking immensely. Rising to his full height, he stood next to her facing this man, who
seemed to have sparked some deep rooted panic in Clair.
"Long time no see, little Clair", the raspy voice said with a grin on his face, "I see you have grown
up quite a bit. Sakara sends her regards. She will be delighted to hear you are well and in good
shape with your new.. friend." The Professor's heart dropped, he felt a chill go over his body and
was suddenly aware everybody was staring at them. He stepped in front of Clair covering her with
his body. The lightning sapphire around his neck started tingling, when he felt his rage rise. So they
had finally come to take her back.
He had swore to never let that happen and by the Gods he had no intention of breaking that promise.
