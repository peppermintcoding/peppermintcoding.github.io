Chapter 10


Clair was nervous. It wasn't like her anymore to get nervous when a day of work began. She knew
what was expected of her, most of her customers were regulars and she had no reason to be nervous
about it. Other emotions were far more common like disgust or anger, but being nervous was not
one of them usually. But today was a rather special day for her and everything needed to go as
planned to be successful. A lot depended on it.
She brushed her long hair and then braided it into a long braid she would lay over her left shoulder.
The Lord was quite specific on how she had to look for him every time. Clair wasn't absolutely
sure, but it wasn't hard to guess who she was impersonating. As long as she was the only object of
his pleasure, she was happy with that. Better her than his own daughter. At least it was her hope that
made this weekly endeavor a little bit easier to stomach.
Inside the flock of hair she braided three strands of wool, two red and one blue. Clair opened the
upper drawer of her desk and got out the incredible sapphire necklace the Lord had gifted her, well
gifted in a sense that she was supposed to wear it when he was around, but never at any other point
in time. He was indeed a generous man. The freshly charged gems were pulsating and felt cold on
her bare skin. She watched herself in the mirror and had to admit, they looked beautiful. It gave her
face a certain glow and made her look wealthy and not like a street kid at all. She then quickly
changed into the uniform he had bought for her. It was the traditional girls school uniform of the
royal schools around Shima. A dark blue skirt, which went over the knees and a white blouse with
one long sleeve and one short sleeve. On the right side of her chest was the school emblem, a red
rabbit with long ears looking up.
One last look into the mirror she checked if everything was in order. Necklace, braid, uniform, no
make-up and an innocent smile. Everything as ordered. She checked if she smelled bad and decided
to put on another whiff of perfume just to be safe. Being nervous always made her sweat quite a bit
and she couldn't afford to put the Lord of in the slightest. He was a vital part to her plan and if he
wasn't happy she wouldn't be able to persuade him.
Closing the door behind her, she walked along the hallway towards the staircase, when she heard
soft sobbing from on the doors to her right. She counted the doors from hers and was surprised,
because normally this room was empty. This could only mean one thing. Clair knocked softly on the
door and the sobbing stopped immediately. "Can I come in for a moment?" she asked softly, but
there was no answer. So she simply opened the door and went inside, there was no reason to wait.
She wouldn't get an answer most likely.
Entering the room, it looked just like any of the others. A small little space with a table and mirror
to get ready, a small wardrobe for costumes and other work outfits, a space for washing and hygiene
and a bed to sleep in. Not much had room in here anyway, but most girls had decorated their rooms
at least a little bit. Clair for example, had quite the collection of books to show for by now. She first
thought some of the customers were actually just generous, getting her books as presents, but she
quickly understood it wasn't like that at all.
On the bed lay a small ball of blankets, but a little head poked out the end to see who had entered
the room.
"Hi", Clair said and slowly approached the bed. "I am Clair, we are kind of neighbors. I live just a
couple of doors down the hall. What's your name?"
The little girl pushed away a bit more of her blankets and exposed her face completely. She had
obviously been crying for some time now, her eyes were all red and her cheeks wet. She had short,
matted hair, a beautiful face and looked about eleven. It still wasn't normal for Clair to see girls this
young within these walls. She herself was barely fifteen and had been working for Sakara about two
years now. But eleven? She was just a child.
"Don't worry, I am not going to hurt you. I am your friend actually, well as close to that as possible.
Don't you want to tell me your name?" Her eyes were wide and she was hiding in the furthest corner
of her bed.
"Carai", she whispered and wiped away some of the tears. Clair slowly walked to the bed and sat
down on the opposite side of the bed as Carai. She wanted to be as far away as possible, but still as
near as possible to give her at least a little feeling of safety.
"Did you arrive today?" Clair asked. The little girl nodded. "How old are you, Carai?"
"Ten", she answered and snuffled. Ten years, by the Gods, Clair thought. In Shima you were
normally allowed to marry with fifteen and even that was changing. People weren't so desperate
anymore to marry off their children as quickly as possible, but were waiting longer and longer for
them to have an opinion in the decision as well.
"Are you hungry?" The little girl shook her head. Clair wasn't sure what to say to her. What does
one say to a ten year old child beginning her life in Sakara's house? There simply was no way of
preparing her and no way of taking away any of the fear she was obviously experiencing. And Clair
was running out of time. The Lord was strict about punctuality. "What did Sakara say you have do
today?"
"Is she the old lady? The one with the fake smile?" Clair had trouble holding back a loud laugh, but
had to chuckle at least. She had almost forgot how direct children could be.
"Yes, that's Sakara. She must have gave you some things you need to do for the day."
"She told me a few things, but I don't know how to do any of them. She told me to go downstairs
and take a long bath to wash up all the dirt and filth. But it is scary down there, with all the drunk
people. I don't like it. And they probably have to cut off my hair, she said. I don't want them to cut
off my hair. I know it's a little messy, but I like it this way. What will I look without any hair?"
Clair was close to crying, "I have an idea. Why don't we go down together. You don't need to worry,
there is a different way to get to the basement, without having to go past the lobby. We girls use it
all the time, but I guess they failed to tell you about that. But that's what I am here for, alright?"
Carai slowly nodded, but wasn't convinced obviously.
"And about your hair. I think you will look good even without your hair. It will grow back in no
time."
"But what about in the mean time? I will look like an old man." It was probably a good thing for her
to get rid of the hair entirely. Maybe she would have very few customers because of it. But then
again, who would want to spend the night with a ten year old child in the first place? Maybe a bald
head wasn't enough to deter these kind of people.
"You will see it's going to be alright. You can wear a hat for the time until your hair has grown back.
They have lots of beautiful hats. What is your favorite color?"
The girl though about it for a moment, "Black", she said.
"They definitely have a black one, I am sure of it because I wore lots of black hats already." Time to
get going, Clair though. "Come one", she stood up from the bed and extended her hand towards the
frightened girl. "It will be quick and you will feel a lot better after a warm bath."
Slowly the child crawled out from under her blankets and took Clair's hand. Hers was tiny and cold.
Her clothes were tattered and Clair saw bruises on her body. "We have to hurry a little though. I am
running late."
"I am sorry", the girl said. "You don't have to show me. I think I can do it myself now."
"Don't worry. We always make time for each other. It's something you can count on." Clair smiled
and for a moment she thought Carai would smile back, but her mouth just twitched a little.
Clair lead the girl around the house down the second stairway to the basement, where she dropped
her off to one of the maids. But the child wouldn't let go of her hand and looked even more
frightened now than before. Clair went down to one knee and took both of her hands in her own.
"You have to be very strong now, alright? Just do what the people here tell you to do. They won't
expect anything bad from you, just that you wash yourself right and scrub everything off. And when
you are ready you can go back to your room. And tonight, when I come back, I will come to your
room, alright? I will bring another girl with me, Oria she is called, and we will talk about everything
you have to know and you can ask us everything you want, alright? It's going to be okay, you just
have to be brave right now. Can you do that?"
Carai nodded slowly and Clair released her hands. She pushed her gently to go into the washroom.
The little girl looked back once and Clair smiled at her. She hoped the girl could handle what was
coming towards her.
She then dashed towards the stairway again trying to make up at least a little of her lost time.
Hopefully the Lord was in a good mood today, or her plan was already doomed.

Outside of Sakara's establishment a small carriage was waiting for Clair. In front, six men were
waiting and ready to pull them around the city. The sky was clouded and a slight breeze was going
through Shima at this day. Good for the men, she thought. Going up and down the hills would make
them sweat quickly and the late spring sun would only make it harder on them. Especially since
Clair was planning to go through the whole city into the northern district. She only needed to
convince the Lord is was in his interest as well. Hopefully he was in a good mood today or the
carriage ride would fall quite short and they would simply go around a few houses and end up here
again. She breathed in deeply and went on.
She stepped towards the door and opened it to get inside. It was something the Lord hated
profusely, not being able to help her inside the carriage, but he was reliant on secrecy. It wouldn't be
good for him, if his family knew what he was doing when he was for business in Shima. So they
had to stay inside the whole time going about the city. The slight possibility of getting caught was
exhilarating for the Lord though.
"I thought we have talked about punctuality already, my dear", he remarked when Clair stepped into
the carriage. His voice was cool, but welcoming. He wasn't in a bad mood today. There was still
hope. The doors of the carriage had little windows, hung with curtains so nobody would be able to
identify them, but they had at least the possibility of looking outside during the ride.
"I apologize deeply, my Lord", Clair said looking down towards her hands. He loved it, when she
was showing remorse. Or faking remorse. "I was helping another student with some of the
homework she didn't understand and forgot the time a little bit."
"I guess that is alright", he said. Clair closed the door and sat down next to him. The carriage was so
small, that there was barely any space between her and the Lord. He wasn't a big man, but rather
slender with long brown hair and green eyes. He wasn't even looking half bad for the clients she
was used to normally, but he was old enough to her father. "Where do you want to go today?" he
asked and opened a little window at the front to speak to the pulling men.
Be vague, but not too vague. "I was thinking we could maybe go north today? Last time we were
down at the harbor and that was fun, but I never seen the northern part of Shima myself."
"The northern district?" he pondered. "I suppose the northern part isn't that interesting at all and
rather far away from where we are. It will probably take us around an hour to get there if we are
lucky, probably more. How did you get the idea?"
"Well, today in school we were learning about the city and its various districts. From the harbor and
warehouse districts to the industrial ones and noble districts. We also learned, that the northern
district is reserved for the church with temples, monasteries and even schools of the church. And it
isn't so populated, so we would have lots of privacy up there." He had planned this for weeks now,
trying to pack the real reason into their role play. She wasn't expecting for him to just agree out
right, but for that case she had phase two.
"I don't know, my dear. It's quite a long ride and I would rather spend some time alone with you and
not traveling through the dirty streets of Shima. I was thinking of making a little detour to the sea
for a bit and then leave through the southern gate to take a stroll through the woods." He loved the
woods, nobody around to see or hear what was going on.
But Clair wasn't going to budge so easily. She still had a trump card in her deck. "My Lord, please I
am begging you", she pleaded and places her hands on his thigh. He loved it when she begged for
something. "I was looking forward to it the whole week, since I last saw you. And I told all my
friends we would go there and I don't want to have tell them we weren't there after all. Please,
please, I am begging you."
The Lord looked at her, but Clair could see he wasn't persuaded at all. "I really want to fulfill your
wish, I do, but I think it's more important for us to spend some quality time together. I haven't seen
you all week, because my schedule has been so difficult altely and I don't want you to think I am
neglecting you. How can we do that going around the capital in this carriage? You know we have to
preserve some amount of secrecy, sadly enough."
Yes, so sad, she thought. Time to go for it. "My Lord, I wasn't suggesting anything less than what
you want. I, too, was looking forward to some alone time with you, so I was thinking we can do it
like this: we drive up to the northern district and have lots of time to talk and take in the bustling
city around us. I think you have lots to teach me still about the city I don't know, it's always so
interesting." He also loved flattery even if it sounded as fake as Clair did right now. "And then on
the way back I thought we could close up the windows completely and have some privacy that way.
I will promise, I will be as quiet as a mouse. And if you can't trust me with that, I have brought
something with me to ensure it." Clair turned to the side and grabbed the gag inside her little bag
she had brought just for this instance. It was Oria's idea really. She had told Clair, that a lot of men
like the Lord would go crazy if she suggested something they normally used herself, like it was her
idea or that she actually enjoyed it. And Oria was not mistaken. As soon as the Lord had gotten his
eyes on the little black gag, his favorite at that, he tensed up and his look changed completely. She
dangled the gag in front of her face with one hand and with the other she played with her long braid.
The way the Lord looked at her was so familiar to Clair by now it frightened her. She saw the
desire, the lust in his eyes. She could even smell it, if that was even possible. The whole air in the
carriage seemed to have changed into something thicker, it wasn't the fresh air she wanted to
breathe, but something that made her want to vomit with every breath she took. It was invading her
body and she couldn't do anything about it, she couldn't stop breathing until it was over. No, she had
to sit and endure it every time.
The Lord turned towards the little window again and shouted orders towards the men. They were
going to the northern district. He couldn't resist the new fantasy she had provided him. He was keen
on not being found out by his family, but he also liked the close line they were walking and he
wasn't afraid to push it. He quickly took the gag from Clair and stashed it in his own pocket, it
wasn't time yet. The carriage started to move forward and Clair was watching the houses slowly
move by. The Lord started to strike up a conversation, but Clair was only listening with half an ear.
Her thoughts were circling only around one thing now, San.
The conversation was most of the time very alike. The Lord would either ask Clair about school or
how she was getting along with her friends. Clair had become quite adept at creating little stories,
weaving the girls at the house into her stories without giving it away to him. Helping Carai through
the first hours of her first time became helping her with homework and so it happened with a lot of
things. Only real things, like her family and her brother were things she never shared with anyone,
not even packed into a story. They didn't deserve any of her personal life. Besides that the Lord
would often show her buildings around the city, some he owned himself, some he helped build or
simply new the architect and was always sure to dump all the details he knew onto Clair. Sometimes
it was rather interesting to hear how a theater or some of the big gateways were built, but today she
had no interest.
Clair was getting nervous again. Now that she had persuaded him to go to the northern district,
things were actually getting serious. Would she be able to see her brother today? She didn't know
exactly where he was or in which school Sakara had shipped him off, but something wasn't quite
right to her. The letters she had received were getting inconsistent and she was simply afraid that
things were going as they seemed. By now, San should be able to write the letters himself, but she
still received the one page every two weeks written by one of the priests and not him. Something
wasn't right.
I am coming, San, she thought.
