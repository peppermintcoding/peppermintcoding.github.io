Chapter 6


"I do miss the fighting", the Emperor said. "Not the war, not the slaughter of men, but the simple
and incomparable feeling you get when the lightning is running through your veins. This feeling of
power, of life. I don't know anything that makes you feel quite like it."
"No, there really isn't", his brother, Haru, said. Su was standing a bit to the side watching over the
sparring grounds below. The arena was big enough to host multiple thousands of people, but today
they were only a small group to watch some of the members of the Royal Academy spar. Four
guards, two servants, who were constantly bringing refreshments and little bits to eat, Su himself
and the two brothers.
"A great hit", the Emperor exclaimed hitting the armrest with his fist. He was wearing a rather
boring military inspired uniform like he always did. Of course, it was way more sophisticated and
ornamented than normal uniforms of generals, but the lack of anything original was disappointing.
A few sapphire buttons here and there sparkling and crackling or the golden family crest, a growling
wolf head, was everything that really distinguished him from a random general. Even his hairstyle
was nothing special, short all around. How could you waste the freedom and money you get as an
Emperor and not do something more forward with your clothing? It was beyond Su's understanding.
"Brother", Haru began, "we aren't here to watch the sparring, are we?"
The Emperor stoically watched the pairing below. Kurana seemed to have the upper hand, but she
wasn't conserving any of her lightning charge so it wasn't yet decided. The crackling was audible
even so far away and the occasional rumble of thunder washed over them when the two Charged
exchanged powerful blows. "Su, tell me, what is our biggest problem right now?"
"In what regards, my Emperor?"
"In regards to the whole nation of course. If you look at the complete picture here."
Su thought about it for a moment, he didn't want to disappoint Haru with a thoughtless answer. "I
suppose our biggest problem is the repercussions of the plague. Because the birds were a vital
carrier of the virus a lot of farmers were infected from the birds seeking shelter from thunderstorms
in their barns and so on. We will be facing quite a shortage of food if we can't cultivate our fields."
He had kept his voice even and without shaking this time. Even after years of being in the company
of the two brothers, it was still making him nervous.
Haru nodded slowly and the Emperor said, "You taught him well brother. Keep it up boy and you
will surpass us old guys in no time."
"I would never dream of-"
"Yes, yes", the Emperor waved his hand at nothing. "I know of your convictions, but save them for
someone who wants to hear them. I have had people swear on their family's lives they wouldn't
betray us, but those bastards did so anyways. Words are nothing than empty shells, actions are the
only truth. And that's what we have to do, take action."
"We are already taking action", Haru said taking a rice ball with a meat filling. "Our soldiers are
moving out and working the fields before the population recovers. Wasn't that your genius plan just
a few months ago." It weakens us though, Su thought but didn't want to speak the words out loud.
"A great idea in a perfect world, brother. Sadly enough, our world is far from perfect. I know you
aren't interested in politics and that's fine, but I need you to understand the seriousness of our
situation. We are losing power and we are losing it fast."
"What do you mean?" Haru asked with a full mouth. "Nobody would dare to stand against us, not
after the War of Unity. We still have the biggest army and the most talented Charged. How are we in
any danger?"
"I have to apologize Su. Apparently my brother isn't fit to be a teacher at all. Our army is barely
enough to fend off two of the four kingdoms. The North is still loyal to us and that won't change
any time soon. The rest is another thing entirely. We gave them too much freedom. A tax break here,
more control over a certain trade and and and. Now our treasury is running dry."
"You say we, but I didn't sign off on these laws. I would have gone to war like we should have done
back then. You can buy their loyalty, you have to take it by force."
"And put the nation through another war? No, not if I can prevent it."
"Apparently you couldn't. You have just postponed the inevitable."
The Emperor sighed. Suddenly everybody turned their attention to the entrance. Little high pitched
bells sounded through the hallway leading to their lounge. Out of the shadows emerged a tall
woman with long, braided hair. Su was always struck by her appearance. Even the Gods must envy
her beauty, he was sure of it. It wasn't only her obvious beauty, but also the way she demanded the
attention of a whole room as soon as she was walking in. She was wearing a dark blue dress with
impeccable seams and embroidered flowers in a thin gold yarn. Her shoulders were left uncovered
and the dress went over into a loop around her flawless, long neck. She wore a deep red lipstick and
blue eyeshadow matching the dress perfectly. Around her ankles were the little bells she always
wore without fail. He still didn't understand quite why. Small charged sapphire earrings were the
only jewelry she wore to round out the composition.
"I hope I haven't come too late", the Empress said with a voice that left goosebumps on Su's skin.
She glided over to the empty seat next to the Emperor. Haru was staring a little too long at her
walking through the room, the guards knew better than to stare, but the Emperor's brother wasn't
subject to these normal rules.
"No my dear, I was just trying to explain the situation to my brother", the Emperor answered, leaned
over and kissed her on the cheek, "but he seems to not get the point yet."
"Well, not very surprising I suppose, is it?"
"It's always nice to have you around, Ridani", Haru answered dryly. "I hoped you were still busy
ordering around the servants to fetch you another dress."
The Empress ignored the spiteful tone of Haru and looked over to Su, who in turn began to turn red.
"It seems the fight is already over", she exclaimed disappointed. "I was looking forward to see a
good sparring match. It has been quite some time. Su, why don't you show us what it means to be
Haru's prodigy? I would love to see what progress you have made since I last saw you."
"I doubt Kurana or Liu would be satisfying opponents for him", Haru said. Was there a little pride in
his voice?
"What about a two against one sparring match then?" the Emperor said.
Ridani seemed delighted. "Forgive us, Su", she said, "but you really don't have a choice in this,
because I got you a little present. And you can only wear it in a fight. Your garments always look so
fashionable, it pains me to see you fight with these bland beige vests all the time." She clapped
twice with her hands and a servant appeared in the doorway.
"A present for me, Empress?" Su exclaimed shocked. Did she just make him a compliment for his
style? A warm feeling expanded in his stomach giving him the feeling of being half the weight he
actually was a second ago. It felt like floating, blood rushing to his face. "I don't think I can accept a
gift from you." He bowed deeply.
"Do you want to deny me this simple pleasure of making you a small present?" she asked outraged,
but then smiled quickly again. "Please, just try it on once. Karon, would you be so kind and inform
Kurana and Liu they should prepare to fight Su." Karon, commander of the guards, bowed and went
down the stairs.
The servant brought over the present to Su. It was packaged in black satin with a red ribbon around
it. He slowly pulled the ribbon off and unraveled the satin. Underneath a sparring vest appeared.
But it wasn't in the normal bland beige color, no, it had a rich blue color, the Empress favorite, with
purple pockets for the charged sapphires. It probably didn't meet the taste of most people, especially
Charged, but for Su it was gorgeous. He had been begging Haru to let him wear a different outfit for
sparring was always shut down again and again. Tradition was important to him, and he though
color would simply distract from the duel. Not that it made much sense, because if you were
distracted from a bit of color, how could you fare well in a real battle with so many things going on?
Also, the lightning cloak was tinting the outfit underneath even more.
But this was really bad. What was he to do now? He couldn't not take the present, it was from the
Empress herself of course. Then again, taking it would mean taking sides against Haru. The joy he
felt tipped over and he started sweating.
"Ridani", Haru exclaimed with repressed anger in his voice and made Su jump a bit, "you know
quite well, why I have forbid him from using anything else than the traditional garments for a duel.
We all know why you did this."
"Excuse me", she answered as calm as a puddle after a storm, "but I made Su a present because I
feel he deserves it. What is all this power we have proclaimed, if we don't use it to make the world a
little bit more beautiful. It's just a vest, Haru. What's the big deal?"
"The big deal is, that you did it to spite me. The worst part is, that you have put him in a horrible
position. Whom will he listen to now? His teacher or the Empress? He can't say no to either one."
"It was never my intention to any such a thing. I was simply trying to make him feel welcome to the
family and with that comes some special perks. Like not being subject to any dress code, even if it
is in the holy duel arena."
Haru grunted and the Emperor breathed out deep, "Can you two get along for once?" Su wanted to
say something, but his tongue felt like sand in his mouth, no way to actually make a sound.
Luckily, in that moment, Karon appeared again, "Emperor, Empress, the two opponents are ready
and waiting for Su."
The Empress thanked him and looked at Haru.
"Master?" Su cawed. Hopefully all this sweating wasn't going to ruin his shirt. It was made by his
new tailor.
"Go ahead and take the fucking vest", Haru finally said. "I guess the Empress is right, it's just a vest.
I hope though, that you won't take other rules and principles with the same leisure." Su could hear
the teeth gnashing with every word. This would have repercussions.
"Thank you, Master", he said, taking the vest and going down to the arena. When he left through the
doorway he winced, when Haru and the Empress started arguing intensely.
#
Waking into the arena the sand felt soft underneath Su's feet. Two servants had filled every pocket
of his vest with fully charged sapphires. A talented Charged could fight with a vest like this for
about an hour on full power, but he didn't expect the fight to go that long. Light streamed in through
the glass dome overhead of Su. The thunderstorm had just left, waltzing towards the west before
finally becoming a mere drizzle.
"There he is", Kurana said with her sly smile. Both her and Liu wore the traditional beige vest, so
basic.
"So is this going to be a two against one?" Liu asked.
"Yes it is." Su made some warm up movements. Even though the lightning was giving the body
extraordinary power, the baseline for everything was your own physical muscles and joints. If you
weren't in top shape, all of the power couldn't be used in an optimal way.
"Nice vest by the way", Liu said, "I guess it has its perks to be kissing the asses of the royal family,
doesn't it."
Su shot her a vile glare, but didn't say anything. She was clearly jealous.
"How about we make this a little bit more interesting than just sparring it out?" Kurana asked trying
to change the topic and avoid further insults.
"And what are you thinking about?" he asked, but knew the answer already. She had been plaguing
him to go on a date for weeks now.
"If we win, Kurana has a wish free from you", Liu said when she saw Kurana was hesitating. If Su
had the choice he would rather go out with Liu, not that he actually would though. At least she was
putting in some effort with how she dressed. Now, she was wearing the boring training clothes, but
normally she also didn't always adhere to the strict fashion rules everybody else was following like
Kurana.
"And what will I get if I win?" he asked instead of engaging further. He stretched his arms and legs,
bending down touching his ankles.
"You get to choose both our outfits for the next ball", Kurana said.
Su jumped up from his stretch suddenly feeling way more excited. "I get to choose the outfit,
makeup and accessories?"
"Everything", Kurana laughed. "You know, you might be the only guy in this world, who would be
excited because of something like that."
"We can't be all unique, can we Kurana?" This was actually getting more interesting now. He
especially had so many ideas for Kurana to finally show her how much she could do if she only put
in some effort. Her body lent itself to a form fitting dress, or even a combination of pants and
something else. So many possibilities.
"I accept", he said. "What are the rules?"
"No weapons", Liu said, "you are out when you are outside the inner ring or you give up
voluntarily."
"That won't happen."
"Let's see about that", Kurana said and her lightning cloak formed around her. The familiar
crackling filled the air and her eyes turned to white. Everything on her body defied the the ground
and moved like she was submerged in water. Her hair flowing as if wind was softly blowing on
them with every movement she did. Liu followed her lead, hands meeting in front of her chest and
the crackling cloak formed around her, hugging her body perfectly. Her aura was a bit brighter than
Kurana's, meaning she was able to use the sapphires a bit better.
Su breathed in deep and put his hands together in front of his heart. He opened himself up to the
outside and felt the sapphires in his vest start to tingle and vibrate slightly. It was almost like they
were crying out to be used and the lightning to be released from inside. When the lightning entered
his body, it felt like a cold liquid running through every vein. His head cleared up and his whole
body felt light as a feather.
He entered water stance, a stance fitted best for quick dodges and light attacks. He first needed to
feel out his opponents and see how they would react to certain attack patterns. Fighting a charged
was all about timing and openings. The cloak made direct injuries impossible without a weapon, but
the impact from a well placed punch to the chest could make you go flying dozens of meters
through the air. And that was his plan.
Liu and Kurana wasn't going to give him the opportunity though and dashed towards him with
superhuman speed. Their attacks were slightly staggered so he dodged Kurana's fist going for his
chest first by dodging to the left and then jumped with a backward somersault over Liu's kick
towards his legs. The key to fighting was not to ever stand still and give the other person time to
form a precise attack plan. So he dashed towards Liu trying to land a low blow against her side. She
blocked and retaliated with a series of quick punches. Su blocked them all with one hand while
using the other to block the incoming attack from behind. Kurana tried to sneak behind, but wasn't
quick enough to surprise him. Fighting was exhilarating even after all these years. The
weightlessness he felt was incomparable to anything else, he jumped higher than normally possible
making it seem like he could fly.
Whenever a blow was blocked a little rumble of thunder was released, like a angry animal getting
restless to overwhelm its prey. To Su fighting another Charged was more like a dance than a fight. It
was about quick exchanged and fluid motion. The lightning made it possible to move almost twice
as fast as normal humans and every blow was strong enough to shatter stone pillars. Fighting two
enemies was normally quite unfair, but Su was playing in a different league than the two girls.
Somehow the Gods favored some over the others and gave them greater ability to use the lightning
power. Nonetheless he needed the fight to end quickly, being faster and stronger than them wouldn't
protect him from making mistakes.
The next hit from Kurana he blocked with dodging close to her body, his cloak brushing hers and
releasing a loud crackling sound. He then pushed his shoulder against her chest with a satisfying
rumble and made Kurana fly through the air and land in the sand a dozen or so meter away from
him. A big cloud of sand shrouded her before him. She was close to the line, but not over it, he was
sure of it. But it would give him one or two seconds, enough to get rid of Liu.
Liu tried to use the opening to swipe his feet from under him and topple him over, a perfect trap was
unraveling. He dodged the kick with a high jump, normally an amateur move and Liu thought so
too because she was smiling seeing him rise in the air. Instead of waiting for the arch to send him
back to the ground, Su held out his right hand towards the ceiling of the domed arena and
discharged a large quantity of lightning through his hand. He had to stabilize his hand with all the
strength he could muster up when he felt the recoil. He immediately felt two sapphires lose their
charge and stop vibrating in his chest, but this move was worth the cost. Liu was caught of guard
when Su was catapulted towards the ground with an immense velocity instead of following the slow
arch he had predicted and wanted to take advantage of. He had trained this move so often with
Haru, the time was impeccable. The discharge shot his hand right against Liu's head and punched
her into the ground. A shock wave of air and sand spread from the blow accompanied by a deep
growl of thunder. He didn't skip a single of his fastened heartbeats, grabbed her by the legs, feeling
her lightning cloak tingle his hands and not allowing him to touch her actual skin. With one fluid
spinning motion he flung the stunned Liu around once and released her making the poor girl soar
through the air towards the elimination line.
One down, he though and making himself ready to block the incoming attack of Kurana, but she
wasn't coming at him at all. Instead he watched her whiz through half the arena, intercepting the
flying Liu, grabbing her leg and spinning her around to slow down her velocity. They were rotating
so fast, a normal person would have ripped apart probably or lost track of where they were, even
with lightning running through your veins it was impressive. Kurana released Liu shooting her right
towards him. He just saw her topple over the line before Liu crashed into him. There was no time to
dodge her, so he tried to block with another discharge of lightning, reducing his sapphire charges by
another complete three. Another rumble of thunder crashed through the arena. Even so, the first
punch from Liu's fist cracked his forearm for blocking a powerful direct hit like this with an audible
sound. She then followed it up with a spinning kick towards his chest, catapulting him away from
her towards the elimination line. Su tried to grab hold of anything, but there was only sand he
couldn't hold on to. He looked behind him and saw the red line approach quickly, giving him little
time to react. He had not enough to use another discharge, because it would leave him with not
enough lightning charge to continue fighting so he would lose anyways. Instead he pooled his cloak
into his still intact left forearm and hand, thrusting them into the ground. The lightning cloak cut
through the ground like it was nothing turning the sand to glass in an instant. The screeching sound
was horrible cutting through all that newly formed glass, but it gave him enough resistance to stop
just shy of the ominous line. He couldn't lose, not only wanted he his price, but he also really, really
didn't want to give Kurana a personal wish.
When he finally stopped, he stood up straight and saw Liu watch him intently. She also suffered
some injury from the punch waiting for the lightning to heal the bones. It was a weird how the bone
regrew inside his arm. By now he was accustomed to it, every strong punch would leave you with
broken bones, but they always healed quickly. Only the pain was irritating. Not right now, while the
cloak protected him, but after wards it would hurt and swell a little.
"Nice move", Liu said with her distorted voice.
"You too, I didn't expect that kind of teamwork from you two. But it's time to finish this." He put his
hands in front of his chest again, preparing for the second stage when Liu's cloak disappeared and
she held up her hand.
"I give up", she said, put her hands before her chest and bowed a little. Su did the same, ending the
sparring duel and releasing his cloak. He immediately felt pain shooting through his right forearm.
He would need to get some sort of ointment from the doctors.
Kurana came running, supporting Liu who had trouble putting weight on the leg she used to kick Su
out of the ring. "Well", Kurana said quietly, "I guess you will be putting us in embarrassing clothes
then."
"On the contrary", he answered, "it will be the first time you won't embarrass yourself at the dance.
You will see, nobody will be able to take their eyes off of you." The words turned her head red and
he regretted saying the words. She probably hoped he would be one of the people. "Well, I will be
going then", he said quickly. "There is still some business to be discussed and I would like to catch
the end of the conversation."
"Is something happening?" Kurana asked.
"I will tell you if I know something for sure."
#
Returning to the lounge Su was greeted by applause from everybody. "What a splendid fight", the
Empress proclaimed enthusiastically. "I hope the vest was up to your normal standards?" Su
nodded. Even Haru acknowledged his well used discharges during the match, a move he invented
himself.
"Fetch a doctor quickly, the Emperor ordered one of the guards. "Sit down, Su, we have still some
important things to discuss and I want you to be part of the discussion."
"Listen closely, Haru", Ridani said with a disarming smile. "It would be a shame if we need to
repeat ourselves again and again."
"Anyways, where were we?" the Emperor said ignoring the cold air around. "Yes, we need to
strengthen our position. We need to get our soldiers out of the fields again so nobody is getting any
ideas while we are weakened. And we also need to weaken the armies of the four kings themselves.
They have been growing rapidly, not only in numbers of normal soldiers, but in equipment and
training experience."
"What do you think we should do, Su?" the Empress asked suddenly.
Su felt his mouth dry up completely. She asked him a direct question. Calm down or you will
embarrass yourself, he thought. "I am sorry, Empress, but I am not sure there is a solution to this
problem. I doubt we can get any of the kings to use their own soldiers to cultivate the lands after the
Emperor had already agreed to do so himself. And we can't just create thousands of people, who
would be able to relieve our soldiers, from nothing, can we?"
Ridani smiled amused, "We actually can, with some ingenuity of course."
"Please enlighten us with your world saving plan", Haru said taking another rice ball and biting
down on it.
She ignore the spite in his voice like a true empress, "You might be in a grumpy mood now, but I
am sure you won't be after you hear what I have to say. We have been planning something nobody
has dared in hundreds of years. We are going to invade the grasslands."
Haru began choking on his rice ball, spitting it out onto the floor. A servant hurried quickly to clean
it up before the last piece had fallen to the ground. Then Haru continued laughing. "And how will
that solve any of our problems? There is nothing out there Ridani, if you have forgotten already. We
don't need more land to feed our people, so what are we looking for out there?"
"We are looking for the people themselves", the Emperor said.
"Wait, wait", Haru said gesturing heavily with his hands. "You can't be serious, can you? You want
them as slaves?" Su was as confused as Haru was. The empire was built upon certain laws, which
couldn't be changed, not even by the emperor himself. And the outlawing of slavery was one of the
cornerstones the empire was built upon. "Brother, you of all the people wouldn't dare to change
these laws, would you? This isn't what father died for."
"We are not going to change any of the laws, we are also not going to break any of these laws", the
Empress interrupted. "We are going to make use of the exact words, the laws have been written
down."
"No citizen of the empire shall be property to another citizen", Su recited.
"Exactly", the Emperor said, "but the nomads of the grasslands aren't citizens of the empire and will
never be citizens. So they aren't subject to our laws at all. Don't you see the opportunity we have
found here, brother?"
"I always thought you were the smarter one of us, brother. But this plan of yours is a fools one. Let's
ignore the fact how the population will react to basically reinstating slavery, even if it's not with our
own people. But of all the times we could have tried invading the grasslands, you are picking the
worst of times. The four kings aren't as obedient as we want them to be and starting a war will be an
opportunity if nothing else for them. And also what about the grasslands? Have you forgotten how
they went completely dark in the last months? No merchant, who has any desire to live or see his
wares sold is making trips around anymore, our spies are all disappearing before they can send back
any information. Something strange is going on out there and we have no idea about any of it. And
you want to invade them now, without any preparation?" Haru had clenched his fists and was
getting quite angry.
"So what will you have us do?" the Empress asked not faced by his anger one bit. How is it possible
for her to stay this calm? Su thought. He was getting antsy just sitting in the vicinity of him. Would
his anger be projected on him he would turn into a pile of dust and try to disappear in the cracks of
the floor.
"I thought you would relish the idea for a good fight", his brother said. "This is the only opportunity
I can give you in your lifetime probably. I won't see another war happen that divides this nation. But
this war has the possibility to stabilize this land. We don't have many other options. Either it's this or
we will see our people starve. What do you think they will do then?"
Silence fell like a heavy blanket on top of everybody. Su wasn't sure if there was anything he could
say in this situation. He didn't know if the Emperor was right with his assessment, but he probably
was. One of the arena's doctors came into the room, relinquishing the silence for a moment. He
bowed low and treated Su's wounded arm. Everybody was waiting for an answer from Haru. He
was after all the commander of the royal army and their strongest warrior. Without him the
endeavor was sure to fail.
"What if we fail to bring back any slaves?"
"We won't fail."
"You can't be serious brother.."
"Do you actually think I would consider this plan if there was another option?" the Emperor said.
He was just as calm as his wife. They were quite sure of the outcome of the conversation. They
must be having some form of information still holding back, that will bring this all together.
"What about the charged sapphires?" Su asked, immediately taking a sip of wine when he felt his
mouth to be dry as sand again.
"I was thinking the same thing", Haru said. "Our powers are useless out there. We can't preserve the
lightning for more than a few weeks in sapphires and beyond the border there are no storms
anymore. They will simply see us coming and ride away. We aren't fast enough without Charged
cutting off their retreat and forcing them to fight."
"The council has been working on something special", Ridana began, "for especially this kind of
purpose. I think it was Soruma, who lead this research making it possible for sapphires to contain
their charged for months, if not years. He couldn't test it thoroughly yet, because it is a fairly new
discovery. We have thought about this plan extensively, Haru. We haven't pulled this from nothing
just before today. We have been working on it for months now."
"So, I would actually get to fight for real again?" Haru asked.
"The white flame will burn again, brighter than ever before and lead us out of this darkness", the
Emperor exclaimed loudly.
"Too dramatic, sweetheart", Ridani said, but Ru could see the light in Haru's eyes. He wanted to
burn again for so long and they finally gave him a reason to do so. This meant war, and Su would be
on the front lines if he wanted or not.
