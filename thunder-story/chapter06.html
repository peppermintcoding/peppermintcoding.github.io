Chapter 5


Clair's feet were already hurting from the constant walking through out the day. They had left the
small village of Jorda a couple of weeks back and were approaching the border to the endless
grasslands. She caressed one of the bulls walking next to her, this one particularly liked it if you
touched his face. He liked it between his eyes, too.
"Clair, we have to do something", Cy said, who had been walking silently beside her this whole
time. He wasn't in a good mood ever since Deko, the former right hand of Sakara, had made his
appearance and was now walking peacefully two wagons ahead of them.
"And what do you suppose we do?" she asked. "He has the seal of the Emperor. He was sent by the
Royal Treasury, we can't just kill him and think nothing will happen to us."
"Nobody said anything about killing him, but good to know where your head is at." There was
genuine concern in his voice, "You can't go on like this. How much do you sleep each night? One
hour, maybe two when the exhaustion gets the better of you? You can't even sleep if I stay up. It's
ruining you beauty."
"Would you be able to sleep if the man who brought you into Sakara's hands was sleeping just a few
steps away from you? Do you really think he is genuine about what he said? That he is not working
for her anymore and isn't going to do anything to me, but actually was chosen to protect the both of
us. How can I trust this piece of shit after all that has happened?"
"I was thinking about a couple of things", Cy began, ignoring her words apparently, "we have to
stage something and get him into a position, where the others are forced to deal with him. Some
form of crime maybe, but I don't know what that would be. He doesn't seem to be the reckless and
stupid type. We have to be careful about this."
"Even if we get him to steal something or I don't know, hit you in the face, it wouldn't change a
thing. Nobody will raise a hand against someone with a royal seal."
"So something more serious. A capital crime then."
"Sure, let's make him kill a random stranger here and see what happens. Maybe next time you could
think for at least a second before spewing out that crap at me."
Cy looked over at her, but she avoided his glance. "You are quite short-tempered when you are
suffering from sleep deprivation. Not very surprising, but still interesting. Let's channel that energy
into something productive though. Think, Clair."
He didn't deserve the way she spoke with him, but she couldn't help it. He was right, the constant
sleepless nights had made her be on edge the whole time. Everything was enraging her, even feeling
a bigger pebble underneath her boots would bring her close to erupting and punching something.
Calandi came strolling towards them, talking a little to every group she passed before moving
further down the caravan. When she finally approached them, she had a smile on her face as wide as
her nose was long. Her long hair was playing in the wind, "How are you doing today, Clair", she
asked. "I hope you slept a little better this night."
"Yes, a bit better", she answered trying to smile.
"Oh, lying again, are we?" She was shaking her head. "Cy, why aren't you making her something to
let her sleep. Some sort of brew or, oh I don't know, there must be a plant around here to help her
sleep."
"I tried everything, Calandi", he sighed, "but she wouldn't take anything. I really am out of my
wits."
"Well, you two are in luck. I will make this right. You will lie down right now, Clair, and I will not
have another discussion. These bulls are strong enough to carry your small body for a couple of
hours."
"I told you, I don't-"
"And I told you to not discuss this with me!" she exclaimed playfully angry. "Cy and I will be
walking just next to you, within touching distance. Oh and I will make sure this man with the scar,
won't come anywhere close to you without waking you, don't you worry."
"How did you know?" the Professor asked.
"Oh, everybody can see the way she looks at that man and I heard what happened in the inn. I might
be not as fast with numbers as you, but with people I can run circles around you, my dear
professor." Clair laughed, but actually climbed onto the wagon right next to them. She mentally
apologized to the bulls having to pull even more weight now. "I got you a little blanket here as
well." The blanket she pulled out from underneath her arm was a clean white with little yellow
flowers on it. It wasn't a very thick blanket, but the weather was getting milder the longer they
journey went. Summer was arriving.
"And now you will sleep in no time." Calandi brushed through Clair's hair before tucking her in a
little lighter. "You have nothing to fear around here", she whispered in her ear. "This is my caravan.
Here I am the empress." She smiled even wider and walked over to the Professor, who also smiled a
little at her.
The warm, comfortable blanket and the unsteady wagon was making her sleepy in seconds. Weeks
of no sleep had been taking its toil on her. She heard Cy and Calandi start up a conversation, but
couldn't understand a single word before finally falling asleep.

"I hope you don't mind me asking", said the Professor, "but why do you keep on going out of your
way to care for Clair?"
"Well", Calandi answered, "because apparently you are doing a horrible job yourself."
"I", he stuttered, but was interrupted by laughter.
"Don't worry, I didn't mean it that way. A little joke, that's all it was." She paused for a moment. "I
don't really understand what it is, but when I look at her I feel my heart feel very heavy. I want to
protect her, even if that makes no sense. She is not my child."
"She is no child at all, I forget that myself quite often. She is a grown woman by now, almost
nineteen years old. Sometimes she seems to be older than me, just by what she says, but on other
occasions, she seems not much older than a child. You should have known her when I met her. The
most interesting mind I have ever met, not a scholar from the council or a professor at the
university. No, a girl from the streets with anxious eyes."
They walked in silence for a bit, the rubble underneath the Professor's boots crunched with every
step. The land around them had changed significantly, from fields of rice and other grain as far as
the eye could see to a deserted grassland. No farmer dared to cultivate land so far out, even though
the border was only approaching. He didn't know if the raids were still common out near the border
or if that had changed in the last years. He wasn't much interested in these affairs.
"Can I ask something personal?" Calandi said all of the sudden.
"Always, but I don't know if I can give you the answer you want."
"So you already guessed it. Oh, of course you did. But still, what happened to her?"
It was a simple question really. He knew the story, well most of it at least he thought. Clair wasn't
very talkative about her past and he wasn't either, but somehow they managed to get pieces of each
other every now and again. Most of the time when they were drunk. "I am sorry, but I don't feel
comfortable speaking about that without her knowledge or without asking her beforehand. She is a
very secretive person and I know she is beginning to like you, but not enough to converse with you
about these kinds of topics. Please don't take it personal, I myself had to piece a lot together without
her telling me. But some things get very obvious when you get to know some details."
"Then I am glad she found you. I think you two make quite the lovely little family."
Family. Was this what they were to each other? "I guess we just both have nobody else, so it's only
natural to stay together if you can tolerate the other person."
"Oh", she said a little hesitant, "I didn't know you had not family either. I am so sorry to hear that."
"You really don't have to. It wasn't quite so bad. I even remember the Emperor's father visiting our
orphanage from time to time. I learned how to fight with a sword from him personally."
"You fighting with a sword?" She laughed, holding her hand in front of her mouth to cover it. "I
never imagined you have had any experience with the sword or warfare at all. You don't strike me
as that type of person."
"Well, you would be surprised of what talents I hold."
A sudden commotion was going through the caravan. Men were running along the line of wagons,
talking to every one of the groups and pointing back towards Shima. Calandi and the Professor
turned around and they both startled for a second. How didn't they notice an approaching
thunderstorm. This wasn't good. The weather had been so stable for the last weeks. Quite
predictable, so they had always time to find shelter along the roads. But now so far to the west the
thunderstorms normally were far less frequent and less volatile, but this one looked angry and dark.
"I am sorry", Calandi said, "but I have to get to my husband and see what we are to do with this
storm." She left quickly, hurrying towards the front of the caravan.
The Professor looked over to Clair, still lying on the wagon tugged in with the blanket from
Calandi. "Time to wake you again", he sighed.

"Stop it!" a voice cried out. Clair was running as fast as she could, but San was still infinitely far
away. "I didn't do anything!" he shouted again, but another slap hit him in the face. It was all her
fault, like always. She tried to shout something towards them, but the words were sucked from her
mouth before they left her lips. Then she saw another slap slowly approaching San's cheek. She saw
every detail of it, the tears in his blue eyes, the giant, rough hand raining down and she was trying
so hard to get to him and help him. But as always she wasn't strong enough to do anything.
Then everything happened at once. The slap hit her brother, someone touched her on the shoulder
and growling thunder brought her back into the real world. She blinked and looked into the
apologetic eyes of the Professor. "I hope the nightmares didn't bother you the whole time", he said.
"Did I shout or cry again?" she asked trying to orient herself.
"Not this time", he reassured her. "But I am sorry for waking you, we have a little problem." He
gestured to the right, if she wasn't turned around it was the direction where Shima was. A black
curtain was trying to swallow up the land underneath it. The thunder was already reaching them, it
was still faint, but a constant growling. They said the thunderstorms were a lot less strong out here
so far away from the mountains, but even so, hundreds of lightnings were raining down to the earth
every time she blinked. And they had no shelter near them.
"What are we going to do?"
"It seems, that Aratos is trying to get everybody together to answer just that question. Come one
let's go, they might need the help of these two brilliant minds." He winked at her and helped her
down from the wagon.
Aratos was already standing on top of one of the wagons and a crowd of people had been gathering
in front. Calandi was nowhere to be seen, but Clair had trouble seeing anything from behind all
these men. The Professor probably had more luck.
"We have studied the maps and turning back is not an option", Aratos began to talk, "and the next
shelter ahead is the border garrison, still a couple of hours away. We can only guess, but I suppose
the storm will reach us in less than an hour." The crowd began to chatter anxiously. Clair looked
around and saw Deko standing a little apart from the other men, even he looked uneasy watching
the lightning in the distance. I hope one of them has your name on it, she thought.
"The ones who are fit enough can try their luck and walk ahead to the garrison outpost. I doubt any
of the bulls will make it in time, so the rest of us will have to stay behind and hope we can survive
this."
"And how do you suppose we do that?" one of the soldiers shouted towards Aratos. "It's not like
these are only a few bolts, we are talking about hundreds and we have no shelter."
"We do have some shelter", Aratos said nervously gesturing with his hands, "we will hide
underneath the wagons. It will give us enough protection." What was he talking about? Clair
thought.
"Not only did they send us into exile", the Professor growled with closed teeth, "they also gave us a
moron merchant to take us through the grasslands. Is this his first expedition or what? How can you
be so reckless and not think about a thunderstorm before planning your route? Now all the messed
up ledgers make sense. They don't have a fucking clue what they are doing at all."
"So what are we doing?" Clair asked. "Dig a hole for all of us and hope it's deep enough?"
"Interesting thought, though not manageable in the short amount of time we have. To dig a hole
deep enough for a human to survive this you have to dig quite deep. No, that's not feasible." She
meant is as a joke, but apparently he missed that again. Somehow the Professor was staying quite
calm about all this. She would have thought him being a bit more anxious about the cloud of death
approaching them. When she looked at him, she saw nothing, but curiosity. Meanwhile, Aratos was
bombarded with accusations and insults from the other men, and rightly so. "What we need is
something that can shield us during the storm."
"We can't build a tall lightning rod to deflect the lightnings and conduct them into the ground."
"No, sadly not. But can you imagine how amazing that would be?" he grinned. "Build a makeshift
lightning rod with only the supplied found in this caravan. No, let's focus. We actually do need
something, or we are getting burned to a crisp."
The crowd was getting quite angry and the shouting was annoying. It didn't help anybody if they
shouted. It also made Clair quite uncomfortable, she could taste the anger on her tongue. If there
was anything worse than a man, it was an angry man. She put the Professor between herself and the
crowd and tried to focus again, when the people parted and the nose of Calandi appeared gloriously
out of the mass of faceless strangers.
"There you are", she sighed, "please you must know something, that can help us. Cy, you are our
only hope. I have to admit, we aren't prepared for something like this."
"Yes, no shit you are", the Professor answered. "You had no business going on this journey and it
has become quite clear you are amateurs at best, but it's beside the point. I am thinking, but this
crowd of angry morons isn't making this any easier. Find a way to shut them up and I will think of
something."
"Oh, but how am I suppose to do that?" She seemed not only frightened, but completely distraught.
She probably understood, that it was her husbands and her fault they all might die in a few hours.
The Professor looked at her with that face, the face he did when he was trying to hold back so hard.
He was indeed an arrogant ass and if Clair wouldn't say anything quickly he was going to make this
a lot more uncomfortable. "Give the men something to do", letting the words stumble out of her
mouth making it up as she went along, "collect every piece of metal you have. All of their armor,
weapons and cargo. Put it in equals piles around a center point, far enough away so we can all fit in
a circle with some extra space between the piles and us. It will attract as much as the lightning as
possible and will deflect it from us."
Calandi looked from Clair to the Professor and back. But he just nodded. "Alright, what else."
"Put all the wagons close together so the bulls have space in the middle. Make it hard for them to
escape. They will probably get spooked when the lightnings go down near them, they normally are
inside of course."
"Got it. She really is a quick thinker."
Calandi quickly went towards her husband and whispered it in his ear. Aratos looked at them for a
moment, contemplating if he wanted to give this a shot. In his eyes was clear wounded pride, but
his fear was greater so he tried to get the crowd something to do. And to Clair's surprise they
listened. Even Deko started to unbuckle his sword belt.
"I hope you know the piles of iron won't get us anything", the Professor said quietly.
"I know that, but they don't, do they? We just need to think of something now." She looked over
towards the horizon and saw the black and white chaos rush towards them.
"Sapphires!" the Professor exclaimed suddenly.
"What?"
"Yes, yes. It will work. We just need as many sapphires as we can get. Alright, you go around and
collect them from everybody. If they don't want to give them to you, tell them if they rather want to
die. I will get the rest done."
"The rest for what?" she asked, but the Professor was already off actually running towards the
wagon they had stashed their own stuff. Mostly his stuff.
Without having any more answers Clair ran over to Calandi and told her about what she needed.
"But we don't have any sapphires with us", she stuttered. "Why do you need them?"
"What does it mean you don't have any? This is a trade caravan. How can you not have any
sapphires around here?"
"Oh, but out here in the grasslands, sapphires are worthless, the thunderstorms won't reach much
further and so nobody wants to buy them as high a price you could get back at Shima for example."
"What about the men? Some most have a few as trinkets and for good luck, won't they?"
"Let's try. I will help you. But what is Cy doing with them."
"Don't know, but it seems to be our only chance." She hesitated for a bit, but added: "Can you
please ask Deko for me. I want to avoid his company if possible."
"Of course, darling", she said and smiled, which looked more scary than reassuring.
Convincing people to give away their sapphires was easier than she first thought. Most of them
were fine after she told them it was for their survival and they would get it back and the ones who
needed a bit more persuading she told they could explain to everybody then, why they all needed to
die because of them. In sleeping thief inside her was devastated at how easy to get their sapphires.
She had worked so hard back then to get even a few gold chips.
After a bit Clair and Calandi met up with the Professor, who was going through the cargo wagons
looking for something. "We got the sapphires", Clair exclaimed and he stopped, looked over to
them and climbed down.
"How many?" he asked.
"I got", Calandi began counting the blue stones in her bag, "five."
The Professor's eyes widened in shock. "I got about ten I think", Clair stumbled, "but some of them
aren't that big to be honest. Most of them are indeed just little trinkets."
The Professor put his face in his hands, "We need at the very least about thirty for the plan to work."
