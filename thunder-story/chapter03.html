Chapter 2


Clair was leaning against the house wall with her hood up hiding her face. She was waiting
patiently for Karan and Zaran, but her patience was running thin. Their plan was highly dependent
on timing and they couldn't afford to be late. The boys were late though. She crouched down when
she saw one of the street dogs approaching through the little alley way she was waiting. The houses
here were standing close and blocked out much of the sun. A chilly wind was blowing through the
city and it wasn't stopped by the houses at all. The dog wailed his tail when noticing Clair and ran
up to her. It was the one with black fur and white dots. He was looking strong and well fed, she was
glad.
"Come here, good boy", she said and petted his ears with both her hands. She would have given him
or any of the other dogs names, but it was harder when they stopped appearing in the streets and
were named. So she stopped quite some time ago to give them too much weight. It wasn't keeping
her from caring for them, so she reached into her satchel dangling from her side and took out a
piece of dried meat. The dog sniffed the air and turned around to see what she had in her hand.
Hastily and without much regards for her fingers, he bit down on it. "Careful, careful", but it was in
vain, it always was. You couldn't teach someone raised on the streets any manners. It went for dogs
just as for humans.
"What is taking them so long?" She had no clock on her and even if she had the big and clunky
things would have just been in her way. But the sun was already high up in the sky and their target
was quite a few blocks away from where they had meant to meet up.
Quick steps echoing from the walls came from down the alley way. If it's not them, we are fucked,
she thought. But as she saw it was Karan turning around the corner with his brother in tow she
relaxed a little for the first time today. At least the first step went well, even though they had to
make up some time.
"It took you long enough", she said with an angry undertone. She stood up and the dog hid behind
her legs. Apparently the two boys were scaring the little guy, how adorable.
"Sorry, Clair", Zaran apologized, breathing heavily, "but we were having some trouble snatching the
uniforms. It's like someone is trying to tell us it's a bad plan. They started washing the uniforms at
least half an hour later than we normally observed in the last week. Isn't that a sign, Clair?"
Why were they all so superstitious? She wasn't sure of many things in life, but one thing was clear:
the Gods weren't interesting in little street rats as themselves and definitely wouldn't send them
convoluted signs like making them wait for the uniforms like this to tell them some secret message
of how stupid their plan was. Or how stupid her plan was, maybe she didn't want to admit that. "But
you all three of them?" They both nodded. Both weren't the luckiest boys in Shima. Lots of tiny red
pimples scarred their faces and both their noses were broken so often, that is was a miracle they
could even still smell something. It made them horribly croaked though. "And you got the right
sizes, right? It will look overly suspicious if our uniforms don't fit right. This plan hinges on the
details, guys."
"We got it all, trust us", Karan said with an annoyed looked on his face. He didn't like getting
bossed around by her. Which was not too surprising. She was barely half their weight and a couple
years younger. But even if he didn't like it, he was smart enough to follow her, because that's how
they got their money.
"Let's got then", she said and they moved quickly down the alley to do the second step in her plan.
They were going to rob a nobles household today, and nothing could go wrong or it would be more
than just a beating and some kicks in the stomach from Morlan this time.
#
Luck was back on their side and Clair hoped it would give Zaran and Karan a reason to stop
worrying about their superstitions. Entering one of the nobles districts was forbidden for any people
not part of the upper class, but today the patrols of soldiers were nowhere to be seen. She had
picked the Azumaja manor for a variety of reasons, but one was, it was near the districts edge,
which would give them a perfect exit route and hopefully let them find cover.
The lavish upper districts were not only different because of their giant manors and personal
soldiers, but there were dozens of little forests with lakes, creeks and ponds. "Let's hide behind this
boulder over here", Clair said to the brothers and they followed her. It wasn't long now before the
service maids form Mother Kanoshi's cleaning service would cross through this little forest on their
way from another noble man's manor to the Azumaja family. Clair was getting giddy again and was
playing with her hair tips. She needed to wash it again soon, it was matted and oily. Zaran often
suggested cutting it short for it being more convenient and easier to take care of, but she loved her
long hair. Even if it was taking more time and effort to keep in shape, she wasn't giving up her
mother's beautiful black hair. She hadn't much to remember her by.
"Did you get the lockpicks?" Karan asked.
"Of course", Zaran answered annoyed, "you know I packed them in. Right in-" This wasn't a good
sign. He was frantically going through his pockets, turning them inside out, but nothing dropped out
other than some food, crumbs and dirt.
Karan hit his brother on the back of his head, "You idiot. How are we supposed to open anything
without lockpicks?"
"It's not my fault. Why did I have to bring them with me in the first place? I was in charge of getting
the uniforms? What have you done until now?"
"Keep your voices down", Clair cautioned them. "Do you want a patrol to find us and throw us into
a prison for a week?" Clair opened her satchel and took out two complete lockpick sets for both of
them. Somehow she knew they would forget. "Put them somewhere, where you can get to them
quickly, but also somewhere, where from the outside they are not visible. And let's put on the
uniforms before they get here."
"You are a life save as usual, Clair", Zaran said with a smile and even Karan thanked her. They
changed into the uniforms, stashing their old clothes under some yellow and red leaves, which were
plenty in autumn. The boys peeked at her periodically while changing, but Clair didn't mind. It
wasn't anything special by now. Living in a lair with dozens of young thieves, privacy wasn't
something one had.
The clothes fit alright, but her pants were a bit too short. The boots she were wearing should cover
it up well enough she hoped. But if everything went to plan they only had to fool some overworked
guards at the main gate.
"They are coming", Karan whispered ducking down behind the boulder. Two boys and one girl were
coming closer wearing the same uniform as they were now, pulling a little wagon behind them.
Within they had stashed all sorts of cleaning utensils like mobs, special liquid to polish all sorts of
surfaces, oils for the wood and other random bits and pieces. It was where they would hide their
stolen goods, walking right out the front door with nobody noticing.
Clair got out a little bottle with a yellow liquid inside. She also took out three pieces of cloth and
put some of the liquid on it. "Don't breathe it in", she explained them again. "Press it against their
noses quickly and hold it there until you can feel their bodies go limp. It will only take a few
seconds."
"Do I want to ask where you got this stuff?" Karan asked with raised eyebrows, but Clair didn't
answer and gave them both one of the cloths. The three targets were getting close enough now, so
she gave the boys a nod and they jumped from behind the boulder. It was a quick struggle, but they
were no match. Zaran and Karan were big boys for their age and had no problem overwhelming
their counterparts and the girl was no match for a street bred thief like Clair. The three employees
from Mother Kanoshi were barely any older than them. Clair punched the girl right in the stomach
and heard the air escape her body. After that she put the cloth on her nose and the body went limp in
seconds. They quickly stashed the bodies behind the boulder but only before going through all of
their pockets until finally finding theri scroll of passage. They carefully covered them with more of
the leaves, trying to make it look as natural as possible. But hopefully they were gone as soon as
someone noticed them or they woke up from their slumber.
"And this will keep them down for how long?" Karan asked.
"Long enough hopefully", Clair answered and started walking towards the Asumaja manor. "We
have no time for smalltalk, let's get going guys. Stage three of the plan, infiltration."
#
After a couple of minutes the forest was getting lighter and the manor rose before them out from the
ground like a giant castle. It had strong stone walls all around with spiked on top to prevent people
from easily climbing them. The main gate was just ahead, guarded by four private guards wearing
the family crest of the Azumaja family, a red ship with two masts, on their armored chests. Long red
capes were flapping in the wind and with their short swords at their side, they were quite
intimidating.
"Stay calm and remember your lines", she encouraged the two boys. And herself a bit as well.
"They have no reason to suspect anything from us. It's not unusual for Kanoshi to send different
people to clean different houses."
"Finally", one of the guards exclaimed overly loud. "We were awaiting you almost an hour ago."
"Our apologies", Clair answered quickly, looking towards the ground, "we were held up by our last
employer. It took a little bit longer this time. We are also doing some restructuring at our business. It
won't happen another time, I can assure your Master."
"Look, it's a Maru girl", one of the other guards exclaimed. He had a sly grin, messy and oily hair
and big bags under his eyes. "Maybe you can come to my house and do some cleaning there too, if
you know what I mean?" It got a big laugh from the other three guards.
"If you that's all we would like to get to work", Karan intercepted. "As you said, we are already late
and I don't think Master Azumaja will be happy when he comes back from the theater and we are
still roaming inside his house. I sure don't want to be punished for something, that isn't my fault."
"Right, right", the first guard said, probably the captain. "Utensils are in the basement, just left after
the entrance. You got all your instructions of what rooms are off limit and which are to be cleaned?"
Off limit rooms? This was new information. "Of course we are", Karan answered with confidence.
"What kind of business do you think Mother Kanoshi runs? We are professionals and not some
second grade cleaning crew." Don't overdo it, Clair begged him in her thoughts and shot him a
glance, but he didn't look at her.
"Alright, little man", the first guard said. "Go ahead." They made way and the two guards, who
hadn't spoken yet, opened the big iron, spiky gate. They were in. They didn't even want to see the
scroll of passage, which was needed to enter the district without being a member of the nobles.
Everything went according to her plan, but it was too early to celebrate. As soon as they had some
distance between themselves and the group of guards, Clair began to go through the plan again. The
main entrance of the house itself was still a hundred meters or so away from the main gate. So much
unused space, but down at the harbor people were desperate to get some place to sleep.
Zaran was pulling the little wagon and Clair cautioned them about some details of the plan. Don't
steal anything what would be noticed by random guards walking around the manor on their patrols.
Nothing too personal, because it would arise suspicion when selling it or was too dangerous to even
bring to the shadiest merchants. Nothing with charged sapphires. Even though the Azumaja family
probably had quite the delicate collection of large pieces of sapphires who could hold more
lightning charge, the risk was too high. If you were silent you could hear them make a crackling
sound and the light they gave off could also reveal them. Everything had to be perfect. The guards
would not have mercy with them.
The boys nodded towards Clair, and they arrived at the front door. They carried the wagon up the
couple of stairs, two guards opening up the front doors, which swung open and revealed a
miraculous inside. The floor was sparkling with tiny blue charged sapphires covering the whole
lobby. Glass was covering the sapphires and gave light to a white marble, which was used all over
the manor. The stairs had thick carpets, in the main color of the house, red, and the railings were
depictions of animals fighting against each other. There were even animals from Clair's homeland:
mighty lions ripping apart gazelles, rhinos charging ahead or elephants fleeing. Most impressive of
all this luxury, was the giant chandelier hanging from the high ceiling in the middle of the lobby. It
must contain dozens if not hundreds of charged sapphires. All perfectly round spheres hanging from
golden chains. It was buzzing and crackling in an intensity Clair had never heard before from any
charged sapphire. It must be worth more than anything she had ever put her eyes on before. Sadly,
they couldn't steal it so she refocused herself and cleared her mind.
The two brothers weren't any different, looking at the inside with open mouths. "Stop looking like
street kids seeing a manor for the first time", Clair said to them.
"You look just the same to me to be honest", Karan countered.
"Let's not fight", his brother said, "we have a job to do and I want this done as quickly as possible. I
have a bad feeling all of the sudden. This is way too dangerous. What have you gotten us into
Clair?"
"I have gotten us into one of the richest houses of Shima. Do you think we can clear our debt with
Morlan by stealing from some random stranger? Not anymore."
"Alright", he conceded.
"Let's start with the dining room. There should be a lot of silver and golden forks and knives for us
to grab", Clair grinned. It was satisfying when a plan was working out. They would not only clear
their debt, but could live for a bit without having to risk their lives on these heists. Maybe it would
even give them some safety from Morlan's fury. You are not there yet, Clair reminded herself.
Zaran pulled the little wagon with them into the dining room, which was located in the right wing of
the building. Clair had done some work to uncover the overall structure of the manor. They would
also hit the upper floor where the Lady Azumaja and her daughters had their bedrooms. Lots of
expensive jewelry was waiting to fill their pockets.
The hallways were decorated with oil paintings of the lineage of the house's history and prior
Masters, or so she guessed. Of whom else would you hang paintings of in your hallway? But to be
honest, Clair didn't understand it anyways. All it does was feel empty in here. The only thing she
heard were their foot steps and her pounding heart. What a waste.
When she opened the double door towards the dining room she felt her heart stop for a couple of
beats. On the head of the giant table a boy was sitting eating some desserts. What was he doing
here? The boys stumbled against here because Clair had frozen right in her tracks. The boy turned
around looking right at her.
"Hello", he said. He had short brown hair, warm brown eyes and was wearing a simple black shirt
with golden buttons. A fire crackled on the far end of the room in a enormous fireplace, burning
through a days worth of wood for a normal family in half an hour.
"Apologies, Master", Karan said, bowing and his brother did the same. He had to gently hit her
before she finally bowed down herself. She had to focus. They had to change a bit about their plan
and be more careful, but this could still work. Maybe one of them had to distract the boy so the
others had free roam.
He stood up from his place and strolled over. Only a noble man's son could exude this amount of
confidence with only his style of walking. Every step was taking like he owned the world himself.
Nobody owed him anything and he could do everything. He stood for everything Clair was
disgusted with. He was the reason they were in this trouble. If she just would have been fine with
robbing random families or merchants. But every time they vetted some potential targets she got
cold feet and blew it off for some idiotic reason, covering up for the fact, that she couldn't bring
herself to taking from the people who had just enough for themselves. Because of the high taxes,
most of the money went to great houses of the nobles. It went to him.
"We didn't know anybody was home", Clair said getting her composure back. "If you excuse us, we
will start in a different room if that is alright with you?"
"Tell me", he said instead, "where are you from?" He was standing now just in front of Clair.
"Sorry?"
"I want to know where you are from?"
"I am Maru", she replied staring him right into the eyes. You weren't suppose to look a superior into
the eyes, but she couldn't help herself. Who does he think he is?
"You two", he pointed at Zaran and Karan. "You can start upstairs with the bed rooms. My sisters
made quite a mess out of the bathrooms up there, they need some extra attention. Would you like to
sit for a minute or two", he said turning back towards Clair.
"Sit? Master I am sorry, but I don't understand. I came here to work and not to entertain your
afternoon. Mother Kanoshi won't be happy if she hears I am not doing my job."
"Well, you can either sit with me and risk the fury of this Kanushi person you are speaking of", he
strolled over to the table again. This arrogant walk. "Or you can go upstairs with the other two and I
will have to tell my father, what a horrible job you did today. Also, being rude to the Master of the
house will not sit right with my father. He is very, let's say, conservative in that regard."
Clair hesitated for a moment trying to find a way out of this situation. But this was the best option
they had. She would be able to distract him and the other two were busy robbing the upstairs clean.
They just needed to somehow get out without this boy noticing anything. She walked over, and the
boy held back a chair for her to sit on. She sat down and he brought her a plate with a sweet piece of
cake. It looked like chocolate, something she had never tasted before, but heard enough about. It
was reserved for the upper class, like so many things.
"It's alright. You can eat", he said laughing, "I won't tell. It can be our little secret, alright?"
Without another though she grabbed the cake with her hand and took a big bite. The taste was so
sweet she instantly felt sick. It was so moist and sweet, but also a little bitter. This was what
everybody was getting crazy over?
The boy laughed, "You normally take one of these", holding out a fork for her. It was made from
silver with gold embellishments, probably worth enough for Clair to feast for a week. She took it, a
little ashamed for being so crude eating with her hands like she normally did. "What's your name?"
"Why does it interest you?"
He grinned. "You know, normally people don't talk back like this to me. If I ask, you answer. Let's
try this again, what's your name?"
Little arrogant bastard. "Clair", she said trying another piece of the cake. It was still bad.
"That's not a Maru name is it?"
"You are weirdly interested in one of your cleaning ladies, aren't you? My mother used to say, it's
not very polite to pelt a woman with questions, if you haven't even told her your name first."
That stupid giant grin on his face, he seemed to enjoy himself splendidly, "Alright, Clair", he
pronounced her name overly dramatic, "my name is Sali Azumaja, heir of the Azumaja family and
in a few months I will come of age and will have control over half the wealth of our family, which
will make me one of the richest young men in the whole nation."
"You say it like it's something impressive you should be proud of", Clair said and froze when she
noticed she said it out loud and not just in her head. Right now, he was enjoying himself and nice to
her, but it could change quite quickly with these nobles. They don't do well with disrespect. Clair
looked up from her plate, chewing on the last bite of her cake. It was getting worse with every bite.
To her surprise he didn't look angry, a little confused maybe, but also interested.
"Can you explain what you mean?" he said and the confidence aura was beginning to crumble.
Be careful Clair, she cautioned herself. "Please forgive me, I shouldn't have said any of this. My
mother always told me I shouldn't run my mouth so much, but sometimes I can't control myself. It
won't happen again."
"Don't do that. You have nothing to fear from me. I really want to know what you mean with what
you said. Why wouldn't it be impressive to be one of the richest people in the world?"
Clair looked up into his warm eyes, "I just don't understand why you boast about something you did
nothing to earn. I mean what did you really do for the wealth of your family? Be born? It's not
something I would be overly proud of."
"So you think I don't deserve it?" he asked looking puzzled.
Clair breathed in deep, "It's just something my father used to say a lot. That the nobles made their
wealth on the back of slaves back in the days and now they are making it because of the wealth they
accumulated from them. They earn money from taxes, they own businesses, they give out loan.
Everything they can do is only because they have a large bag of money in their houses somewhere.
I think he was just angry about the fact, that he had to work for everything and you, well, you
inherited more than he could have ever made in a hundred life times."
The following silence swallowed everything around them. For saying something like this he could
put her into custody and hold her for some period of time. Long enough for the robbery to be
noticed and she would have to take the fall for it. At least she hoped Zaran and Karan could escape
somehow before all that happened, with some loot to show for. Why, on this day of all the possible
days, was he at home and not with his family? They had watched the Azumaja family closely the
last few weeks and they always went together to the theater. Always. Sali's eyes were fixated on
Clair's. She wasn't sure what would happen now. Her heartbeat was thundering in her chest and her
hands were sweaty.
"What do you think?" he asked instead. Hasn't she told him already?
"What do you mean? Haven't I told you?"
"No, actually you told me what your father said about it and it's quite interesting I have to admit.
We never talk like that about anything and especially not about our own wealth and family, but I am
getting curious now. Do you agree with every word your father said or do you have a different point
of few? Or even more interesting, what would you do in my position? Relinquish the whole wealth
and live as a, yes, as a what?"
Clair thought about it for a moment before answering and Sali cut her another piece of this
disgusting chocolate cake and put it on her empty plate. She dug in immediately. "Yes, I would give
almost all the money away, only keeping enough to feed me and my family for the rest of our lives.
I would change things around the city. No person should be starving on the streets, nobody should
have to sleep together with eight other people in one room, just because you can't afford anything
better, while the noble families occupy this huge space inside the city. You have more bedrooms
than your parents could ever have kids. It's not fair and I wouldn't feel comfortable with it. How can
you?"
Behind his warm brown eyes something was moving. It wasn't his pupils or eyes as a whole, and it
wasn't even a change in color or something you could really put your finger on, but Clair was sure
something was happening behind these warm eyes. She took a closer look at his appearance and
was intrigued by his hands. They were absolutely clean. Not one grain of sand or dirt underneath his
fingernails, no scar running along his long fingers, no burn marks, they were flawless and beautiful.
This was what a lifetime of wealth would buy you besides a giant manor and business empire:
impeccable hands.
The soldier barging in through the double doors ripped her from her thoughts and even Sali seemed
startled like his thoughts were something he wasn't allowed to think about. "Forgive for the
intrusion, Master", the guard said hastily and bowed before the heir of the Azumaja family. He
wasn't one of the guards at the gate and nor one of the ones standing guard at the main entrance.
Clair hadn't seen him before. "We have a situation, Master." He eyed Clair with a grim expression
and put a hand on the sword hanging from his side.
