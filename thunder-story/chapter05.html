Chapter4


Clair rushed through the hallways checking every room. Bathroom. Bedroom. Bedroom. Bedroom.
Bathroom. All furnished fit for a king, or so she thought of them. She never was inside the
Emperors quarters, but she couldn't imagine anything more luxurious and redundant than this. So
many carpets, paintings, vases and plants. But no sight of Zaran and Karan. She needed to find them
quickly. This was their chance to escape with whatever the two had gathered. Luck was back on
their side as Sali had to investigate something that happened on the other side of the manor, in one
of the gardens. She couldn't hear what was going on exactly, because the guard whispered it in Sali's
ear, but it was enough to get the gist that they were not looking for the brothers and so they had a
small window to escape. He left her with the words, "I hope we meet again, Clair, and can finish
our discussion. I look forward to it", and then vanished with the guard through another door on the
other side of the dining room.
Where were they? She darted around a corner and finally stumbled upon the cleaning wagon and
two open doors. She barged through one of the open doors and found Karan going through some of
the drawers. "Karan, put it all back and help me get the wagon out of here. We have to leave, now!"
Her voice startled him so much he almost fell over.
"You could have knocked or something and not scare the Gods out of me", he said angrily, but did
as he was told and closed all the drawers. "While you were busy entertaining the rich boy, we were
hard at work. I hope we can even lift the wagon down the stairs again. We got some good stuff,
Clair. Real good stuff."
"Let's talk about it when we left the noble's district. Sali is distracted right now, but I don't know
when he will come back. Get your brother and let's go."
"You already know his name, how romantic", he teased and went to get his brother. Zaran was deep
inside one of the wardrobes. This one was like another room inside the giant bedroom, probably
belonging to one of the four daughters. The room was bigger than the house Clair grew up in. So
many shoes, dresses and coats. Even pelts and jewelry was plenty inside. They all helped together
to get everything back to normal and Clair was bombarded with remarks about her dining
experience with the little rich boy, as they called him.
The stairs were their last obstacle and then they only had to walk out the door and never look back.
It was too heavy to just carry it, like they did to get it upstairs. They had to heave it one step at a
time, letting the heavy wagon thump on every step along the way. Halfway down Clair saw a guard
walk by through the lobby.
He frowned and stopped in his tracks, "Do you guys need some help?"
"Oh no, you are too kind, but we got it", Clair replied quickly, trying to make her voice sound as
normal as possible. Her hands were shaking. "The two are just tired from the long day. We are up
since before sunrise, I think you can relate. But we will get through it just like we always do, we
don't want to trouble you with any of our business."
He watched them for a second, then shrugged and went his way leaving the three thieves to breathe
again.
"That was close", Zaran said while breathing heavily.
"Come on", Karan said with a red face, "this wagon is getting really, really heavy. No time for a
break before we aren't out."
"He is right", Clair said and tried to help the boys, awkwardly grabbing one of the sides of the
wooden vehicle.
Approaching the door, they opened it themselves before the guards outside helped them. Outside
night had almost fallen on Shima and the two moons were starting to employ their dominance on
the sky again. The three got out their coats from the wagon, being careful not to show any of their
loot. The air was cold, but all too refreshing for Clair. She felt like she could finally breathe
normally again. Only a few more meters of walking and they would be in safety.
When they were out of range of any guards Clair asked, "Where did you put all the cleaning stuff to
make room by the way?"
The brothers laughed, "We emptied all the bottles everywhere and stashed the remaining stuff in
every little nook and cranny we could find. They will find memories of this robbery for weeks."
#
The way back to the thieves' lair was trivial. They avoided the main streets and intersections. It was
more tedious, but what were a few more hours of walking with a treasure right beside them, a
treasure they had because of Clair.
"What will you do with your share, Clair?" Zaran asked.
"Hm", she thought about it for a moment, "probably get me and my brother a room alone and the
rest will be spend on new clothes, good food and some books. I want to be able to teach my brother
to read and write."
"I will buy myself the biggest piece of chocolate I can find", Zaran said, Clair laughed. "If you want
a taste, you can try a nibble." He winked at her.
"I guessed that means things will change though", Karan said. He was right. Hopefully for the
better. At the moment they were at the bottom of the hierarchy, but with this robbery Morlan would
be forced to reward them. No, he would want to reward them, because they were finally bringing in
the big money. Maybe he would even promote Clair to have a bigger crew. A fresh wind was always
good, it was necessary.
Going around another corner they finally reached the lair, their safe house and origin of the whole
operation Morlan was running. It was in the outer ring of Shima, far beyond the inner walls, and far
enough away from the harbor and merchant districts to have it nice and quiet. Perfectly incognito.
Ruki was on look out greeting them and opening the door. "I see you were successful", she smiled.
"Even better, Morlan is in a great mood today. He had some visitor and something is going down
tonight. I guessed it's your lucky day."
The lair was very quiet at this time of the day, normal crews were working at night and so the whole
building was eerily empty. "Leave the wagon here and let's go up to Morlan", Karan said who was
sweating through the cleaning uniform underneath his coat. The wooden steps were creaking
underneath their boots, nobody said another word. It was somehow different than Clair had
imagined it. She thought they would be more celebrating and optimism, but somehow everybody
was in their own thoughts. They were probably thinking about what to do with their share of the
robbery. Morlan was taking the biggest cut, but with the amount they had looted it was enough to
get by for quite some time. It meant safety and privileges, something nobody of them had often. Too
many failed jobs.
Morlan's office was at the very top of the building right underneath the roof. The air was warm and
candles were burning on the walls for lighting. The flames danced on their little black wicks.
Contrary to many others, Clair always thought flames to be more alive than charged sapphires. To
many the lightning within the sapphire was almost like living in thing, but flames were so much
more fascinating and unique than the lightning. They not only had no money to keep sapphires for
lighting, they also would vanish quickly, being sold somewhere to get some money for food or a
drink or two. At the end, this was a lair of thieves and not a noble's house.
Opening the door, there was a sharp smell of smoke. The boss doesn't smoke, Clair thought. Morlan
was sitting behind his desk with piles of papers, gold pieces and always an open bottle of liquor. He
was a large man, in height as well as in width. Weighing probably four times as much as Clair he
was feared for a reason. His temper wasn't making it any easier to be comfortable around him. His
harsh face and red nose made him appear so ugly it was only because of his strength he wasn't a
target for ridicule.
But today he was smiling like a child. On the other side of the table sat a stranger Clair had never
seen before. He was the one smoking a thick, round cigar. He wasn't turning around but blew
another thick cloud of smoke into the air. It swirled up towards the ceiling and disappeared slowly.
Where does it go? Clair thought.
"You are late", he grunted at them.
"Yes, but we have a good reason", Clair replied, trying to decipher the meaning of his smile. He
didn't seem more drunk than usual. Maybe it really was because of this mysterious visitor. "We did
it. We have enough to pay our debts and then some. You will be very happy with our loot. It's
waiting right down stairs." Clair was proud of herself. This would get her finally some more
responsibility and credit with the boss.
"And where did you get it from, if I might ask?"
This was the point she was waiting for. Now he would be utterly impressive with her. "We robbed
the manor of the Azumaja family. Got all the stuff from right under their stupid noses."
Morlan walked around the table towards the little group. Karan and Zaran stepped back a bit
leaving Clair in front. It was more a reflex than anything else. Normally he would rain down
punches, if they fucked up again. But she wasn't scared, not today. Why would she? That's why the
slap caught her off guard. "Who the fuck allowed you to go into a noble family's house to rob
them?" Another slap. Clair was so surprised, she didn't let out any sound. "Do you even know how
stupid that was? What do you think will happen now? They will just let it slide and not look for you
three? I don't think you were so smart to hide your faces, were you?"
"Well no", Clair tried to explain, but another punch to her stomach wouldn't let her finish. She went
down to her knees and tried to moan, but the sound wouldn't come out.
"I knew this was a horrible idea, to let you roam free. I thought you three couldn't fuck anything up
anymore than you usually do. Maybe get at least something for the whole crew, but what do I get?
Only fucking problems." Another kick to her side, she winced. What was going on? She didn't
understand. The plan was flawless, they got back with so much loot. With hundreds of thousand of
people living in Shima, how would they ever find them? He wasn't making sense. But before she
could even voice any concern, another hard kick hit her. She felt the familiar taste of blood in her
mouth.
"That's quite enough", the mystery man said. A voice so deep it sounded unnatural, like something
from an old legend. Something from the realm of Gods.
"Have a promised too much?" Morlan asked turning around to the stranger. Clair was trying to pick
herself up, touching her sides looking for serious injuries. She couldn't find any and spat out some
blood. She didn't need to look back, to know Zaran and Karan were cowering as far back as
possible. It was how things always went. She would take the brunt of Morlan's hits, but she could
take it. They couldn't.
"It is indeed impressive", the stranger walked over to Clair. In the candle light a long, vertical scar
ran over his cheek. "I have to apologize for his behavior. Robbing the Azumaja family is overly
impressive, but in the future you won't have to do anything so dangerous anymore. How would you
like a change in profession?"
"She has-", but Morlan was cut short by the raised hand of the stranger.
He waited for an answer. "I don't understand. What kind of change?"
"You will be working for Lady Sakara from now on. Your old boss and I have done a deal, you
won't be having to endure any more from him. Your new job will be quite different. But don't worry,
Sakara will explain everything in great detail with you personally. You have ten minutes to pack
your bags and meet me downstairs. Don't be late, it's something Sakara will make you remember
quickly."
Everything was happening too fast. "What about my brother? What about-"
"We have already discussed your brother. What was his name? Ah yes, San. He will be joining you
and live with you in your new home. You two will be having your own room, separate beds, daily
baths, two warm meals a day and many more luxuries. Of course, under the condition of you
working up to Sakara's standards."
Away from this flee hole? There must be something wrong about this. Nobody gives out free meals
and baths for total strangers. Clair couldn't get head around it. "What will be my work about?" she
asked shyly.
Morlan laughed behind the stranger, "We will clear up any questions, when we get there. Now, pack
your bags. I don't want to have to repeat myself again, or we will have some problems."
Clair wanted to ask something else, but she didn't dare. The eyes of the stranger left no more room
for questions. She had no other choice and turned around. The two brothers looked at her with
anxious and puzzled faces and she thought Zaran had wet eyes when she walked passed them out
the door into her unknown life.
