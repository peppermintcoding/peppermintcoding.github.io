Prologue


Looking down onto the battlefield, Dericho was shaking. This was his first battle, his first war he
was ever a part of. He had just come of age and finished his basic training program. He had been
one of the best at the military academy and so the King had picked him personally to join the Giran,
the special unit adept in dealing with enemy Charged. By now the enemy hadn't fielded his Charged
and so Dericho and his squad were still on stand by. They were standing on a small hilltop with the
commanders giving out orders with errand boys overlooking the whole battlefield. In the stories
battles and wars had been made out to be something heroic, something where legends were forged,
but this wasn't what he had expected. It was a lot slower than he had expected. The lines of soldiers
crashing together and barely moving back of forward. Men poking with their spears and hiding
behind their shields. But already the cried of the wounded were sounding louder than the clanking
of iron on iron and wood. Medics were running through the lines trying to salvage whatever they
could. Most would probably die being trampled or executed by the enemy.
"Where are the brothers?", Dericho's captain asked. "Why don't they join the battle yet? Their army
is losing." Dericho couldn't see it. It looked like nobody was losing nor winning. The lines of men
barely moved in the last half an hour.
"I won't complain", Paro said, Dericho's best friend he knew since they had started at the academy.
They had lasted through the whole program together and had made it into the Giran. "I am not so
keen on fighting the White Flames of the North, if I am being totally honest Captain."
"They will come", the Captain said. "They have to, or lose what they have gained up to this point.
And they don't strike me as the men, who will surrender after conquering two kingdoms already.
But they won't do the same to ours, I will see to it personally."
Dericho checked his equipment again. It always calmed him down, going through the mundane
check list. It was something he knew and was accustomed to. Something to occupy his head. On his
back he had fastened a heavy wood club with iron plating around, used to crush through the
lightning cloak of a Charged. He had no use for swords or knives, they wouldn't fight any normal
soldiers, they had an extra squad protecting them against those. The Giran's only objective was to
kill or injure a Charged to the point of making them redraw from the battlefield. On his side hung
multiple, thing iron cords with a small iron ball at the end as a weight so it could be thrown and
would wrap around the targets arms and legs.
It felt like their whole army held their breath when they saw two pure white silhouettes crash into
their ranks. They had flown in through a high arch, higher than any of their Charged could ever
manage. Their entry was already devastating. Under the cheering of their own army the two
brothers cut through the ranks like it was nothing. Highly training soldiers in full formation were
being decimated, long swipes of their giant gold swords splitting men in half with their armor on.
This was the power of a charged, beyond anything a normal mortal was able to imagine. But they
had a weakness and the Giran were ready to exploit it.
"Let's move out", the Captain cried out. In total they had four squads of Giran ready for
deployment, two for every brother. They didn't have more Charged anyway. To think they had
conquered two whole kingdoms with their inferior numbers and only two Charged was still
puzzling to most. It had come out of nothing.
Lord Morogan had joined Dericho's squad and would lead them as their Charged to draw attention
of one the brothers. While the Lord was trying to keep him busy, Dericho and his squad would try
and go in for the kill.
"Are you ready?" Paro asked him.
"I am shitting my pants", he answered and got a laugh.
"We all are, brother", Paro said and slapped him on the back.
"Unit A and B are moving to the right flank", the Captain's voice bellowed. "The rest are with me
and Lord Morogan, we take the left. Let's show them whose land this is." The men cheered and they
rushed towards the two enemy brothers.
When their unit hit the back rows of the army, the soldiers quickly parted to make a small alley for
them to advance quickly further into the battlefield. The men looked worried and anxious, seeing
how many of their own men were being slaughtered in front of them. They were trying to hold of
the onslaught, but the Charged ripped through squad after squad, splintering shields, breaking
spears and crushing bones. The one brother they had to fight had left a giant hole in their ranks,
making it easy for the enemy army to advance and attack from multiple sides. If they didn't do
something quickly, the battle could actually be lost.
The Lord activated his lightning cloak, creating a protective layer of pure lightning around his
whole body. His eyes turned to a haunting white and his long black hair started to rise into the air
like he was submerged in water. The familiar crackling of a Charged was mixed into the cries of the
dying. Next to the enemy brother, Lord Morogan looked rather pathetic. His cloak was barely half
the intensity and didn't extend as far beyond his body, but it was all they had right now. But that's
why they had brought two units of Giran with them, hadn't they?
Morogan jumped towards the enemy and landed with a audible thump on the ground dozens of
meters ahead of Dericho and his squad. They started running and getting their weapons ready. He
unbuckled one of the iron chords, hooked it into his sapphire satchel and rushed towards the duel
site. He still didn't understand enough about the science of how the sapphires worked and held
lightning energy, but it worked and that was all that counted for Dericho.
The two Charged exchanged blows so fast and intense it was hard to follow them at all. Sometimes
they were so fast, everything got blurry, only lines of white flashes and thundering blows. The two
units of Giran swarmed out to surround the two Charged. Paro gave Dericho a last look of good
luck and nodded towards him before running off to his right. The captain shouted orders all around,
but they were mostly for the normal soldiers trying to built a secure perimeter around them, so they
could get to work. Dericho and his unit knew what was to be done.
He swung his iron chord, slowly picking up speed. The familiar buzzing of the chord was music to
his ears. He must be careful now though, hitting their own Charged could mean the end, he was
already highly outmatched. The Lord was in cloud stance, a stance only used for blocking and
evading attacks of a Charged. He was the distraction and without him the two units of Giran could
be easily slaughtered just like all the other men around Dericho on the ground. He tried not to look
down, but the smell gave it away anyway. It was a mixture of blood and shit, something he never
thought he would smell on the battlefield of all places. This wasn't the place where heroes were
made.
Dericho tried to get closer to the dueling Charged, but it was fruitless. The long sweeps of their
giant, lightning empowered swords were too dangerous. Then the Lord dodged a lunge attack from
the enemy and it left a short opening for the Giran. Almost in unison all of their men, who stood at
the left side of the circle released their chords, humming through the air and trying to hit their
target. It was a hard move to pull off, the small bead at the end of the chord was suppose to wrap
around the enemy's arm or legs and would then suck some of the lightning power into the empty
sapphires waiting in their satchels at the side.
Dericho's chord cut through the air and missed completely. "Shit", he muttered. The Charged didn't
get dodged, he wanted it to happen. He ducked underneath the incoming chords and was catapulted
towards the Giran around him. He crushed into two of them cutting them down immediately, their
bodies cut into two halves. Blood and intestines sprayed through the air and the smell of burned
flesh drifted over to him. Dericho retracted his chord quickly to get ready for another round and the
Lord crashed into the brother to give them another try.
"Formation 42", the Captain shouted over the thundering and battle noise. So it was all or nothing
then. Formation 42 meant the enemy had to be stopped under all circumstances, even if it meant
sacrificing men of their own.
The Lord understood himself as well, somehow hearing what the Captain had shouted and threw
himself at the brother trying to engulf him with his own body. The brother was taken so by surprise,
the Lord was able to wrap his arms around him for just a second. But it was enough because
Dericho and the rest of the two units already released their chords, wrapping around both of the
charged and taking away their lightning cloak. The blinding white light emitted by the two dimmed
considerably, until they heard the cries of pain. The Lord's cloak had vanished completely while the
enemy's was still somewhat intact. It was burning him alive, they had to move quickly.
Dericho unlatched his big iron plated club from his back and charged towards the two. He was there
barely in time, the Lord already unconscious or dead, he wasn't sure and it wasn't important right
now. Dericho was the first on sight and swung his club over his head for the maximum force behind
it. Breaking through a lightning cloak was impossible, but it didn't protect against the impact force
completely. So a club could bruise the skin and bones underneath the cloak. Repeating it multiple
times could actually impair the user so much he could lose the ability to draw lightning from his
sapphires.
The club hit with a satisfying thump, the lightning licking around the club sending shivers down
Dericho's spine. He hit again and again, always trying to hit the same point, his knee cap. The joints
were the most vulnerable, which could actually break with enough impact. Paro joined to his right
and two other guys to Dericho's left, all hacking away on the Charged on the ground. It wasn't
heroic, it wasn't beautiful. It was barbaric and felt wrong. They were beating on a human laying on
the ground unable to move. The lightning cloak flickering more and more, receding with every hit
he took. They were close and Dericho was happy it was over soon. He grinned over to his friend,
who was grinning back. Bringing down one of the White Flames of the North must be getting them
all the girls when they would return.
But they shouldn't have celebrated so early.
The ground shook when the second brother landed right next to them, blazing white. His crackling
cloak drowned all of the other noises, but he didn't leave Dericho any time to think. With a quick
sweep of his long blade he cut the heads off the men on Dericho's left. The tip grazed his face,
cutting deep into his flesh. He lost control over his club and let it fall to the ground holding both of
his hands against the wound on his face. He smelled his own burned flesh and felt the searing pain.
The cut must have been as long as half his face, even his ear seemed to be cut off completely, he
didn't hear anything on his left side anymore. He took a step back away from the Charged, but
toppled over something on the ground falling down on his back.
He saw Paro try to run away as well, but the Charged was faster, wielding his sword in an elegant
turn around and cutting Dericho's best friend in half. The blade didn't even slow down when
meeting his flesh. It cut through like it was nothing. The two halves of his best friend fell onto him,
burying him underneath them. He tried to cry out or shout for help, but he couldn't move. He was
completely paralyzed feeling his friends warm body on top of him. The blood was seeping out of
the long wound onto his face. It was so much he had to close his eyes letting the gore cover his face
completely, some getting into his mouth making him gag, some clogging up his nose making it
impossible to breathe. His stomach revolted and released its insides, before Dericho's vision closed
in on him and left him with only darkness.
