Chapter 9


It was hard for the Professor to see her like this. Clair was given a spot on a caravan wagon to lie
and recover since the lightning storm. They had gotten some ointments from the border base they
passed through into the grasslands. These were of little use, generic mixtures of plants and oils the
Professor had seen thousands of times. Their potency was low, but he had no way of getting
anything better out here. Even in Shima it was hard to get anything against lightning wounds. For
starters, they were so rare, there simply wasn't a market for them. The few people who actually had
something to help against the burns and shakes were selling it at an astronomical price, but he
would have given every last gold piece he had. Seeing Clair like this broke his heart.
Her left arm was completely covered with burn marks and bruises form the impact. The bolt had hit
her hand first, which you could see quite clearly from the sprawling pattern it drew on her skin. If it
wasn't so serious, the Professor would have called it quite beautiful, but there was no beauty in a
wound like this. The skin was either burned to a deep red or a mixture of purple and yellow.
Through all of that was a sprawling network of little lines and swirls, so fine in detail it was hard
not admire the intricacy of it. The Professor had never seen a wound like this before, the only
people he had seen, who had been hit by lightning bolts, were completely unrecognizable from their
burns. He wouldn't even go so far and call them people anymore, because they had to collect the
different limbs and pieces of their bodies, which were scattered all over the place. The lightnings
were unforgiving.
So it wasn't surprising the other travelers had a different opinion towards Clair now. For the
Professor it was a miracle she was still alive and breathing, even though her body was still fighting
to stay that way. For the others it was unnatural. Nobody survived the lightnings if they weren't a
Charged and then it didn't face them at all. A survivor of a lightning impact was unheard of,
impossible for all and beyond anything good. They were shunning them, scowling at them, some
even going so far to leave them behind to walk on their own without any supplies. So easily they
forgot who had saved their lives during the thunderstorm. If it hadn't for him, they would all look
far worse than Clair.
She turned around and opened her eyes. She looked weak and had lost a lot of weight already. The
Professor quickly grabbed a ladle of water, which he had put in some sugar to give her just a little
bit of energy. "Take a sip, please", he said. She did so, slowly but drank the full ladle.
"How late is it?" she asked.
"We are going to stop soon, I think. I should be around seven or eight, if I am not mistaken. How
are you feeling?"
"Stop asking me this, please. You know well enough I just feel weak and nauseous. I still don't feel
any pain nor will I ever do so. Stop worrying about it. It looks worse than it is."
"It is way more serious and worse than you think it is. If you stay like this and can't keep food
down, because your body is revolting, you will simply starve to death."
She waved for another ladle of water, "I can imagine worse deaths than this. In any case, it's either
here and now or later from the plague, so what's the big difference."
"I see you are being quite the optimist today."
"When am I not?" she tried to smile, but failed miserably. Her eyes were sunken deep into their
sockets and the color of her skin and changed from a beautiful brown into an ugly mix of brown,
yellow and green even.
Aratos, who was at the front of the caravan only a couple of wagons away from them, signaled for
them to halt and the whole succession came to a rolling, slow halt. He had picked a flat piece of
land next to the road, well the dirt path really, to camp for the night. Preparing everything for the
night has become routine to everybody by this point. The wagons were rolled close together at the
eastern side of the camp to shield them against the winds, at least a little. Then everybody was
building up their tents for the night and would come together to prepare food. The Professor spotted
Calandi leading some bulls to their pen, she looked over to him and smile half-heartedly. It didn't
bother him much how everybody treated them, but somehow it hurt a little seeing her keeping her
distance as well. He understood of course, she couldn't stand against the whole group, too,
especially being one of the leaders, but still. The biggest surprise was Deko though. Not only did he
not care much about any curse, but he openly defended Clair, when the men tried to attack them
physically. Who would have guessed Deko would stand between them.
"Can you help me?" Clair asked from the side and he turned around quickly. He helped her down
from the wagon feeling her cold, small hand in his. "I got some optimism for you. Did you see me
sleep back then? Like a little baby, almost never waking up at all. Isn't that something?"
"It doesn't count if you are too exhausted to panic, Clair", he said instead. He grabbed their tent and
walked away from the group, who were setting up next to the wagons. It was a luxury they had to
give up, like so many others. Deko walked over to them, smoking another cigar. He must have some
secret stash somewhere on the wagons. If he could find them, there were so many possibilities. But
then again, it wouldn't be nice to prank someone, who was their only ally left in this caravan. Was
this really enough to excuse the things he had done before?
"I see you have finally stopped acting like a frightened child around me", he said looking at Clair.
"I am simply too exhausted to care anymore, Deko. But if you put a hand on me, you will see how
much strength I have still left when I tear the skin off your face."
He laughed and had to cough, "It's really a shame. I am beginning to actually like you, girl.
Anyways, I have come with rather bad news."
"It's hard to make things worse, but I am listening", the Professor said.
"Well, they can't let us starve out here or leave us behind, not to me at least and I am ordered to
keep you safe. As long as I am alive you are alright, but I had to make some compromises."
"This sounds promising", Clair snarked.
"From now on, you will be traveling on a wagon at the end of the caravan, with a little distance
between you and the next one. Be happy that I got you a wagon at all. Also, she is not allowed to
come near the others in the camp, nor the fires. We two are allowed to get food from there, but we
are also not allowed to stay. Quite a shame, I loved the warm atmosphere between those bums.
Anyways, keep your distance or it can get ugly. A royal seal will get you far, but a lot can happen
out here, lots of ways for me to die without raising any suspicion back in Shima, if you understand."
"And there was I, looking forward to something like that happening and now I have to hope it won't
happen at all?" Clair said. "You were right, these aren't good news."
"How do you do it, professor?" he asked laughing. "How do you get her to like you?"
"Well, for starters I didn't bring her into slavery or killed her brother." Clair tensed up next to him.
Did he went too far?
Deko's laugh died and he took a drag from his cigar. "The boy isn't dead."
"Are you sure about that?"
He paused and locked eyes with the Professor. The evening light made it hard to make out more
than shapes, but the embers from his cigar illuminated his face a little. "It wasn't my fault. I was
following-"
"Cut it out", Clair shouted. "Both of you. Deko, I know you have done good the last weeks for me,
but by the Gods I will rip you apart if you say another word." He looked at them, blew out some
smoke, but left without another word.
"Clair, I am sorry. I shouldn't-"
"I need some space", Clair snapped. "Don't follow me." And with that she went off into the darkness
away from the camp.
"Don't go too far", he shouted after her and felt an uneasy feeling in his stomach. He shouldn't have
said these things.

Clair was walking away from the busy camp into the darkness of the night. The sun had gone down
so quickly, it's almost like the night was stronger out here than in Shima. Having only seen a big
city and a few smaller towns with her family, traveling through the grasslands was a surreal
experience. They were so empty. Nothing was happening, it was all flat and overgrown with all
sorts of different grasses. Even a few bushes were something quite special out here and trees were
so rare she didn't know if she had actually seen one during the trip or if she hallucinated it. She had
heard from stories there were giant herds of wild cattle roaming the grasslands and the people were
living off them completely almost. But so far, only emptiness seemed to roam the grasslands.
"Clair", a quiet voice cried from the side. She had seen and especially heard much from San these
last weeks. The fever wasn't helping with it. Not only was she reliving so many memories in her
dreams, but even when she was awake he was always there now. Back in Shima living with the
Professor she had been thinking less and less about her brother. She hadn't given up on him, not in
the slightest, but at least she could sleep without constant dreaming about him. But as soon as she
left the high walls of Shima her head wasn't working right anymore. "Where are you?" he asked the
wind distorting his gentle voice.
"I am here, San", she whispered and walked on into the open wanting to be swallowed completely
by the darkness. The burn wound on her left arm was itching terribly, but she was fighting the urge.
The Professor had said it would make things worse and maybe even kill her. An infection out here
with no herbs and medicine could be fatal. It was almost unbearable though. Her skin was
screaming to be peeled away. It felt like something had crept underneath it, rummaging through her
insides. The Professor had also said, it felt quite hot, so she had to change dressings every now and
then to cool it down.
It felt like she had walked for ever, but when she turned around she could still see the flames of the
camp dance in the night and paint eerie shadows from the travelers. Her body felt weak and even
the small distance had brought her to a point of exhaustion she hadn't anticipated. Slowly she went
down to her knees and prodded the ground to see if it was safe to sit on. She flattened a small area
of grass and finally sat down with a satisfied exhale. Hundreds of stars were shining over her head
and the two moons, both barely illuminated, were almost touching. Clair always thought it was sad
how close the two moons came to each other every couple of weeks, but they never touched and
probably never will. Maybe they were simply too shy to make the last step towards each other, too
afraid of what might happen.
"I didn't know you liked me so much", a raspy voice said from her right. It made her jump, but she
recognized the deep, dark sound of Deko's voice immediately. It was hard to forget it.
"I didn't know you were here", she answered into the darkness. She could barely see her own hand
before her eyes, but his voice sounded so near.
"Don't expect me to move places, just because you happen to wander to the same place as me. If
you want to be alone, walk on."
It was hard not to want to move away from him, just leave him alone in the darkness and find
someplace else. But her body was screaming to her, she should stay where she was. She needed the
rest badly, her body felt barely strong enough to sit upright. Walking away would just mean more
exhaustion.
"It's fine I suppose", she said instead. "But can you show me where you are somehow?"
She heard a rustling from Deko's side and a blue light appeared only a couple of meters besides
Clair. He had pulled out a charged sapphire, barely holding any lightning anymore. The dim light
was enough though, to make out where he was. His hair seemed to have grayed with the sapphire
light and the scar on his cheek was somehow glowing slightly. "Still so afraid of me, little girl?"
"Wouldn't you?" she asked. "I guess it's not your fault entirely, but I associate a lot of shit that
happened to me with you. I guess you can understand that."
"We all have our demons, yours just happens to have my face." He put back the sapphire in one of
his bags, concealing the light completely and plunging them back into darkness. Somehow it wasn't
making her more scared, now that she couldn't see what he was doing anymore. If Clair was honest,
she was weirdly enough, not scared at all. Maybe too exhausted to be scared anymore.
"Can I ask you something?"
"I hate it when people ask that. Ask the question or don't. Stop poking around trying to be
considerate or something. Don't beat around the bush."
"Alright, why are you here, Deko? I mean really here. They don't want to protect the Professor from
anything out here. Soruma would be delighted if something would happen to him out here. So
what's the deal?"
"A man gotta eat", Deko muttered.
"Well I didn't actually expect an answer, but don't think I am not onto you."
"You can barely walk or eat anything solid and in a few weeks you will be dead, little girl. You are
good at talking big, but you have nothing behind it. If I wanted to, you two would already be dead. I
am not good at a lot of things, but killing and fighting is something I am decent enough to beat a
little girl and a scholar. Even if he is rather tall for a robed one."
Sadly, he was right. She was good at talking big, but had nothing, absolutely nothing to put against
him. Taking hits and punches was something she had been able to endure pretty damn well her
whole life and it was sometimes enough. Even that has brought her more pain than the actual hits
would have been though.
The two sat in silence for a while. The other travelers had begun to sing songs to pass the time and
get morale back up probably.
"Deko", Clair began slowly, "what happened to the others when I left?" It was a question she had
asked herself hundreds or thousands of times since that day. It had been a ruthless decision and on
some days she regretted it deeply, but it was necessary and she would do the same again if faced
with the same decision. It had still hurt these last two years.
"What do you think happened? It's not like you cared about it, did you?"
"Of course I cared what happened to them. I cared for them more than you will ever do. Don't talk
to me like that, you know nothing what we have been through because of people like Sakara. While
you were leaning on walls, watching us or running some errands we were forced to sell our bodies
and more. You will understand the bond we formed because of it. So, don't talk to me about caring.
I just want to know what happened. Can you at least be a decent person for once and tell me."
"A decent person wouldn't tell you about it, little girl. And it's not important now, is it? It has
happened, they moved on, you moved on and things are back to normal. Nothing you can do about
it now, so what's the big deal?"
If she had any strength left in her body or knew she could actually inflict any amount of pain to
him, she wouldn't have needed to think about it for a second. "Maybe I should have stayed", she
whispered more to herself than anybody else. Speaking so loudly has left her mouth dry and she felt
how little she had drunk today again.
"It wasn't that bad. You know she couldn't do anything to them, they are her capital. It couldn't be
something visible, so that rules out quite a lot of horrible things."
"Are you trying to make me feel better about it? Because you are doing a horrible job at him. I am
no naive child anymore. I know Sakara and I know the things she can do to people. She had
something on all of them, otherwise they would have left quicker than you can light a new cigar.
She knew things about every one of us, things we thought were our little deep, dark secrets. And I
left them behind with her." The words sounded bitter in her mouth. She felt tears forming in her
eyes and the area between her chest and stomach seemed to sink deeper inside.
"They are alright", he said and his voice sounded raspier than normal. "Don't stay out here for too
long, the air is getting cold and you are too weak to catch a cold as well." The grass rustled where
Deko said, he probably stood up and then she heard quiet foot steps leaving her alone with the stars
and moons.
It took her hours to get back to the camp. The fires had almost all died down and most of the
travelers were already sleeping in their tents. Only the night watch was still awake, some sitting
around a single fire, the others patrolling the perimeter. Luck for her, the Professor had built their
tent quite a way away from the camp. Even further than he had to do, so she didn't run into any of
them directly. She didn't like the idea of being alone in the dark with one of them. Lonely men, who
were angry were the most dangerous ones of them all. And these men were getting restless already.
She crawled into the tent, noticing the familiar snore of the Professor. For a moment she was hurt,
that he didn't stay up to see if she was alright and getting back to the tent at all, but then his
silhouette turned around and the snoring stopped instantly. He was faking it.
"Get under a blanket quickly, please", he said gently and handed her one of their wool blankets. She
wrapped herself in it and lay down beside him. "Clair", he began, but stopped.
"You don't have to say anything, I know."
"Do you want to come closer?" he asked.
Instead of answering him, she shuffled over, because she felt the tears coming already. He put his
arm around her and she put her head on his chest. As soon as she felt the familiar warmth and felt
the safety of his arm around her, she wasn't able to hold it back anymore. The tears came streaming
and she started sobbing uncontrollable.
