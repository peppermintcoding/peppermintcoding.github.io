Chapter 3


The white buildings towering over Asher shone in the faint sunlight. The white
quartz, of which most of the buildings in Asta were made, was wet from the rain and
gave the buildings an even more otherworldly appearance. Asher had never before
seen buildings out of giant stones, stacked on top of each other. In Yevum, most
buildings were either made from clay, cob, which was a mixture of clay, sand and
straw or simply wood. The whole trip through the white city, his mouth was as wide
open as his eyes were.
He journeyed for weeks with seven clerics making their way from Yevum to Asta.
The clerics ignored Asher for the most part and kept their distance out of fear. But
inside their faces he saw not only fear but pity. Eventhough the clerics believed it to
be a curse from the Enlightening cast upon people as severe punishment, a few
couldn't help themselves from feeling sorry. Ogerius, the cleric administering his
mothers medicine and giving Asher counsil, made sure he could escape from Yevum
with his brothers and sisters.
The whole way Asher could only think about his mother, brother and Ameline. He
had to leave in a rush, cleric Ogerius helping him escape from the angry mob
gathering in the small town of Yevum. He only could imagine what Ameline's and her
family were feeling. Eventhough, most of his thoughts were with his brother, who
would now be the one trying to take care of their ill mother and dealing with John.
His inbability to hug Elyas one last time or squeeze his mothers hand were weighing
hard on him. But this was part of the curse, or whatever it was, touching another
human would burn their skin to the bone and further.
"That's it", cleric Farita proclaimed. "I wish you the best of luck and", he hesitated,
"the blessing of the Enlightening." Farita was one of the few who spoke with Asher
over the course of their voyage. He was the only one seeing him off to his new home.
"Thanks", Asher said. "Thanks for everything." He held out his hand to shake Farita's
for a goodbye only to realize, that he couldn't. Farita nodded to him and went towards
the giant cathedral of Asta.
Three knocks on the big oak door. The building complex was part of the palace of
Highlord Rember. It was said to be the only place in all of the lands where Scorched
were welcome and could live without fear of retribution from the common folk. Most
Scorched did not survive long enough to even get here, but he did.
The door opened, a young man, maybe about Asher's age with a red handbead
holding his flaming hair away from his face. The broad smile on his face was
contagious. He wore cold grey garments, a simple long sleeve shirt and tight pants. A
red belt was holding up the pants.
"Welcome friend", he said with a deep voice, "to the most magical place in all of the
world. Let your heart be at ease, as for here you are finally safe."
"Greetings", Asher said offering his hand again before remembering.
"Oh Elightening", the young man jumped back. "Be careful with this around here.
Are you an assassin hired to end my life or do you like living on a knives edge. But
come in the weather is terrible as always."
"Sorry, I forgot. I am new to this. I am Asher by the way." He entered the building
and was greeted by a small entrance hall with pillars along the side and statues of old
heroes, lords and ladies along the walls.
The young man's face confused, "I am Anderius. But everyone calls me An with a
long 'a'. You say you are new to this. You mean the whole red hair thing? How long?"
How long had it been? The smell and cries of Ameline still replaying in his head.
How many nights has it been where he cried himself to sleep? "Three weeks I think.
How long since you have changed?"
"Three weeks?", An said trying to contain himself. "You are not joking with me here?
I wouldn't mind if you do, but please don't." Asher nodded unsure what to make of
this. "I don't know if I should laugh about it or envy you. But you will learn about this
duality soon enough. Let's go, I show you around."
Asher followed him out of the entrance hall through a short corridor into a little
garden area. White pillars holding up the roof above the walkways so they were safe
from the rain. It was building a perfect square with some smaller trees in the middle
with benches made entirely out of white quartz. Asher had never seen a building with
so little wood construction in them. It seemed they didn't use any wood at all to build.
Only white shining quartz.
"This is for when this hideous rain finally stopps. Here we can be free of the people
stares and whispers."
"You are not so fond of the rain are you?", Asher asked.
"Oh it's that obvious? Well, for my part the only curse upon these people isn't our,
well our talent, but this rain. Why else would the Enlightening send us these clouds if
not to rid himself of us. To cover his eye of the human filth?"
"Why does he give us sunny days then, if he hates us so much?" Another hallway
with bare walls lead them further into the buiding complex.
"Maybe he hasn't given up on us completely and wants to see if we have changed at
all. Either that or he might be mighty forgetful."
"You must be popular with the clerics I presume?"
"Oh Newby, I am popular with everybody. Who wouldn't love me? You'll see." That
smile was so genuine and full of life, it reminded him of Ameline. His heart sunk
down his knees, the smile on his face froze. She would never be able to smile like
that anymore. An didn't notice but lead him further into another room, which looked
like a dining room for about forty people. How many Scorched were here? And why
haven't they met any other?
"This is where we eat", An continue his tour. "The cook is amazing. His will let
anything else taste like ash to you." Bread. Elyas. His little brother alone at home
without him.
"Hey, you look a bit sad here", An said. "It'll all be fine, you'll see. Lord Amberic is
taking good care of his little project here."
"I thought this was part of Highlord Rember's palace."
"Well yeah, it is. But gathering all of us here was Lord Amberic's idea. You will meet
him soon enough. But let's get you to your room. You must be exhausted and in dire
need of a shower." An laughed at Asher's reddening face. It had been some time since
he washed himself with real soap.
After a few right and left turns and more of An's talk about the cook and Lord
Amberic they finally arrived at his new room. The whole time An left a generous
amount of space between the two. Asher had to always remind himself not to get too
close, but An seemed to be doing it out of habit.
"So this is it", An said. "I know nothing fancy or anything but depending from where
you come it might be bigger than you thought." It was way bigger. Back at home, he
had to share a tiny room with his brother. Enough space for two beds and a furniture
to harbour their clothes.
"I will come pick you up tomorrow morning for breakfast and introduce you to
everybody, or at least a few people. But you will see. Ah before I forget, the shower is
just down the corridor. Soap should be somewhere in here. Okay? Great, see you then
Newby."
"Thanks, until then", Asher replied. A giant closet made from dark wood was on the
right side of the room. It was beautifully made but without many ornaments. The bed
on the other side of the room was actually made from feathers instead of straw. When
Asher lied down he felt like he was floating, so soft. Exhaustion washing over him,
he felt the weeks travelling in his legs and body. He needed sleep. So he took of his
clothes lay down and tried to sleep. And for the first time in weeks, exhaustion won
the battle against his guilt and he just fell asleep.
"There are some ground rules everyone needs to follow without any compromises
here", An said while they were walking down the hallway towards the dining room
for breakfast. "First of all, keep your distance. We all would like to hug and cuddle up
in this kind of weather but we can't. We don't want any infernos burning the place
down, alright?"
"Infernos?", Asher asked with one brow up. Quite the exaggerating for burning
someone's flesh off.
"So I presume there aren't many travellars coming through your little hut village?"
"Well, clerics come and go sometimes. We have quite the large monastery but that's
about it. They don't talk about news with the commoners." It was weird to talk with
someone standing so far away from each other.
"A true newby, aren't you?. Scorched touching any other living being, makes a big
burn", An explained. Asher knew all to well about this interaction. Amleline.
"Scorched touching Scorched.. well they turn to a pile of ashes with a big inferno of
fire. Nice to look at, even nice to stand near but be one of the two parties doing the
touching, not so good. Understand?"
Asher nodded. "Splendid. That's actually all the rules but I will give you a little tip.
Don't talk much about your past with the others. You are in quite the unique position
and some would be offened by it."
Rustling of ceramic came from the dining room, soft chatter and the smell of fresh
bread, tea and other things Asher didn't know.
"You keep to me and all will be fine." There was that smile again, making Asher relax
a bit. His muscles were sore form travelling but tense at the same time. His stomach
growled like a giant cat.
The dining room flooded with red hair. Almost everybody had them. From small
children to adults sitting on the dark wood tables eating their meals. A generous
amount of space between them. For the smaller children there were some servants,
clothed in white with wodden rods at their waist, making sure they stayed away form
each other and ate their meals. Everyone wearing the cold blue clothing Asher had
put on himself. He found it lying in the big closet in his room. Red belts around the
waist.
An guided him towards the counter were all sort of food was presented. Asher picked
up some tea with sugar, which was a special treat in his family. Sugar was rare. The
sugarcane only growing in specific conditions. Then there was bread in different
brown hues, smelling delicious. Butter, marmelade and some meat and cheese were
offered as well. Asher picked some of everything feeling his stomach tightening.
"Leave something for the rest of us", An complained.
With rose cheeks Asher was about to put some of his stuff back, "It was a joke,
Newby. Man."
They hurried towards some bigger table with three other young people sitting there.
The table was in a simple dark brown, just like the others, no ornaments, not even
down the table legs. It was weird being surrounded by this magnificent white quartz
all around with statues and round pillars, but the wood furniture was as simple as it
could be. The three people all had fiery red hair.
"Good morning my people, this is the Newby I told you about, Asher." The three
stared at him with vile glances. Why were they so angry looking? Did he do anything
to offend them?
"Morning", Asher mumbled sitting down quickly trying to avoid their faces.
"So this is Lilith." An pointed towards one of the women, or were they still girls?
Asher couldn't tell their age. They seemed around the adolescent age, but they could
also be five years older than Asher himself. Her hair was shorter than Asher's and it
was a red mess. It looked like she didn't bother looking into the mirror in the morning
and brushing her hair into place. It even looked like she hadn't done for several days.
Her eyes a warm brown eventhough her expression was filled with anger and
resentment. Her nose was small.
"She is nicer when you get to know her more. Come on Lilith, be nice to him. He just
arrived. It's hard on him, alright?"
"Oh no the poor boy just arrived. I am sure everything must be very hard on him."
"Well", An said diplomatically, "the other two are Patric and Kathrine." The two
looked like from stories Asher heard about when he was younger. Like Hardulan and
Siphone, or how he imagined the two, just the red hair was not how he imagined
them. Their faces were flawless with a mystic to them to draw in your eyes. Patric
with shoulder long hair and Kathrine with hair reaching down beneath the table
where Asher couldn't see. Patric had black lines under his eyes as it was custom for
men from the upper class. On Kathrine's covered neck she wore a red lether band
with a tiny heart made of iron hanging down.
Asher began to cover his bread with some marmelade. He felt the glances of the
others on his. He began to sweat, his feeling of hunger receding.
"How was your journey here? Where are you from?", Patric asked. A bit of a smile,
forced for sure but still there, creeping up on his face.
"I come from Yevum", Asher said. "It took me a few weeks coming here, eventhough
I don't quite know why I am here. What are we suppose to do here?"
The three exchanged glances. "Lord Amberic", An said, "is training us. For whatever
purpose he and the Highlord feel fit for us. Eventhough we are to this day still quite
useless if I may say it out loud. And you won't change a thing I am afraid."
"Why do you keep saying that? What's so special about me?"
And then they laughed. For a moment the tense atmosphere disappeared and they
laughed. Not all genuienly.
"They all think they are special when they come here", Lilith said.
"Well none will beat you my dear. Our grand jewel, the bringer of fire, the-"
"Cut it out An, or else."
"You are not special", Kathrin said. Her voice soft as feathers and soothing like the
rain. "We all are Scorched, special to the common folk and maybe even compared to
the lords and Highlords of this world. But the thing that divides our group, is the time
of awakening." Time of awakening. The time he changed.
"And you with your three weeks are the least special of us all", Lilith spat. "I would
be surprised if you could light a dry piece of tinder." He could burn of someones face,
but touching anything else did do nothing at all? What was she talking about?
"I wouldn't know how to go about it in the first place", Asher said. Trying to bite a
piece of bread. His apetite gone with the conversation. His stomach still growling.
"Why are you being so mean about it anyway? Aren't we all going throught he same
rainin' shit? Aren't we all the same cursed ones, so why go after me like that?" Lilith
was beginning to get on his nerves.
"Going through the same? You can't even begin to understad any of our pain." Her
eyes glaring at Asher. She stood up without another word and leaft the dining room.
Couldn't understand pain? He had his fair share of pain even before this curse
happened.
"Don't think too much about it", An said, trying to smile but failing for the first time.
"Tell me, Asher", Kathrin asked, "it's been a few weeks since you awoke is that
right?"
Asher nodded. "Did you kiss someone before you awoke?", she asked again.
"Yes, her name is Ameline." Ameline. Her face. The smell, her cries, her fathers fury.
"Even on the mouth", she asked. Her face stiff and emotionless.
"A couple of times, yes. When we were alone we did it eventhough it's frowned upon
to do so before marriage." But he couldn't finish his sentence, Kathrin rose up and ran
out of the dining room. Patric right on her heels trying to catch up.
"Did I say something wrong?", Asher asked.
"Told you not to talk about your past", An said. "It doesn't bode well here. Finish your
breakfast, we need to get going. Cleric Saratsu doesn't like it when we are late. He
enjoys punishment with the rod."
