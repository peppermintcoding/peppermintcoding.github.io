Chapter 5


Asher stood before Lord Amberic, who was dressed in a fancy blue coat with golden
buttons and white pants. Beside him stood an old woman who introduces herself as
the headmistress Sarina of this so calIed school. In front of Asher was a table with an
unlit torch on top of it.
"I hope you you've had a warm welcome", Sarina said. "I am sorry I couldn't be there
when you arrived but I had important matters to attend to. An did is job, I presume?"
"Yes he did", Asher said.
"Let's not drag this on for much longer", Lord Amberic interrupted. His voice loud
and deep. "We have called you here to assess your skills, your talents if you want.
You awoke three weeks ago I heard?"
"That is correct, mylord."
"Tell me, how did it happen?"
"How did what happen, mylord?" Was he talking about Ameline? Did they know?
"Ah, my mistake. Do you know why you changed?"
"No, mylord."
"After quite some time of research we found out, to become a Scorched you had to
almost die. That's what all of you had in common. Some drowned, some almost died
of rainfever, some fell and so on. So what is your story?"
"I think I drowned. I fell into the river and was fished out. Must have been close
then."
"I see", Lord Amberic said with obvious interest. "So, let's get to the exciting part. We
will want to know how strong the gift is in you. Please light that torch in front of
you?"
"Mylord, I don't know how I could. I tried before in cleric Saratsu's class, but I
couldn't do. I wouldn't know how."
"He is too new to this, mylord", Sarina said. Her voice was soft and warm. "I told you
it would be a waste of time. He has awoken to late."
"Apologies, but what does that mean? Nobody around here seems to give me any
straight answer. Why is it so important when I awoke. My touch is death to others
just as theirs is."
"Well you are right, that all Scorched burn other living beings", Sarina began. "The
key difference is the power of their gift. The later it is you awake the weaker you are
with the flames. Scorched who awoke before they could remember have far greater
power than, well, people like you. To be frank we never had one latebloomer like
you."
So that was the big thing everybody was tiptoeing around. He was the weakest. Even
weaker than the children he saw in the dining room before.
"Does that mean you won't keep me here?" He couldn't get back to Yevum. He
couldn't look Ameline in the eyes ever again.
"All Scorched", said Lord Amberic, "are welcome here. I make no difference. We will
find a way for you to be useful. The clerics will teach you medical procedures and
other things alike so you can help on the battlefield."
"I understand", Asher said. The battlefield? How was he supposed to help people if he
was unable to touch them without severe burning?
"You shouldn't miss any more of your class. You are dismissed."
Asher turned around and left the room. He felt their glances burning in his back.
Back in cleric Saratsu's class, the short men with dark brown hair, pointed towards a
table with An. On the table, a simple birch wood table, lay a giant pig on its back. A
tray with fine metal tools, some already shining with blood red some not used yet. An
was standing over the fat pig, making a long cut on its stomach.
"What the hell are we to do?", Asher asked.
"Practicing. They don't want us practicing on real people so they got us these." His
hands moving swiftly about. He obviously had some practice in this already. The
cleric was trying to help Patric and Kathrine out with their pig.
"Where is Lilith?"
"Miss her already", An grinned. "Have you already fallen for our princess? Wait,
don't tell her I called her that. She hates it. She basically hates everything but that's
beside the point."
"You haven't answer my question yet." An was taking a needle and thread from the
tray, holding the needle with wodden pincers.
"Oh have I not? My apologies. Lilith is not like us others. If our talent was to kill, we
would be blind without arms and she would be an Onkari. What I want to say is, she
doesn't compare to us. While we are learning to stich people up and use our hands to
burn off wounds, she is training to conquer kingdoms."
"But the Onkari are only a myth and this is real. What can she do?"
"So first of all, give me another thread, this one ripped again." Asher picked up some
of the thread and holding it for An to take, who raised an eyebrow. Asher quickly lay
the thread close to him, remembering to keep his distance. "Secondly the Onkari are
not a myth. Or how do you want to explain Highlord Karzan's death just a few
decades ago? He was surrounded by his guards during the full moon, but an Onkari
appear right in his own shadow, slitting his throat and vanishing without a trace.
Without noise. You come from a small village, of course you don't know what
happens in the world."
"Right. I see this is a touchy subject. But what about Lilith?"
"A little tip, don't get caught up with her. Greet her in the halls if you see her, say
good morning during breakfast but don't let it go too far. You only get burned."
Quickly he added, "Emotionally."
The rest of the lesson An was trying to teach Asher how to stich a wound without
touching the patient, or in this case the pig. They inflicted more and more wounds for
them to practice on, and Asher's practiced, steady hands were quick to learn the new
movements. At the end of the lesson his needle danced over the pig's skin, in and out,
closing the wound.
"Well done", Saratsu praised. "We are done for today."
While the others were leaving the room quickly, Asher stayed behind.
"Can I talk to you, cleric?", he asked.
"Of course, of course. What's troubling you?"
"I wish to write a letter to my family back home. I wish to know how they are. Could
you record some words for me, to send to them. If we send the letter to the monastery
in Yevum I know they will make it get to my family."
"It can be arranged. What would you like me to write to them." Saratsu picked some
paper from his desk and dipped the green feather into dark ink. He wrote fast and
Asher felt it hard to find the words. He didn't know what to say but wanted to know
how they were doing. Know how his brother was keeping up and if mother was doing
better or worse. Fear crept into his chest, making it harder to breathe. Should he write
something to Ameline? No. It wouldn't make a difference. Not anymore.
"You know what," Asher said, "I think I will sleep about and we can discuss what I
want to write to them another day."
"Alright, as you say. You know where to find me." Asher stood up and left the room
of the medic teacher.
Walking around the corner of the hallway towards his room. The door was open, not
like he left it. Slowly opening the door, he saw An lying on his bed, shoes on the side
staring at the ceiling.
"Finally", he said. "What took you so long?"
"What are you doin in my room, An?"
A big smile appearing on An's face, making it hard for Asher not to smile himself.
How does he do it, Asher thought.
"I am here to help of course, like I have been doing all this time, you see. Sarina has
given me this vital role of showing you around and all, but also to teach you what you
will need. Nobody of them knows what it's like to be Scorched. But we do. So what
do you say, let's burn some stuff?"
"I can't", Asher said with disappointment. "I can't even light a candle. I tried it all the
way from Yevum to Asta. Nothing."
"Don't worry. I am a good teacher." He sat up on the bed, putting his shoes back on.
"If we fail, we can ask Lilith, maybe she'll help. We all learned from her actually."
"And from whom does she learn all this from?"
"She doesn't", An said walking past him out the door. "Come on, it'll be fun. You'll
see."
Asher debated for a second to stay in his room, but what was he supposed to do?
Everybody either looked at him as a child or hated him. And being alone with his
memories and worries wouldn't be nice neither. An was after all the only one being
friendly to him. Why was that?
The two young men walked towards the square enveloped garden area. White quartz
surrounding them, with little bits of rain falling on the lush green in the middle. The
garden was slopen a bit, so the water could run to one side and flow further through
pipes to the nearest river to avoid flooding.
"Well, I would have liked some sunshine but who are we to ask the Enlightening for
anything, right?" An fished some dry cinder out of his pocket as well as some pieces
of wood.
"So, first of all I will show you how it should look and then you try, right?"
"I don't think it is that simple", Asher said.
"Everything is that simple at the end. It only looks complicated when you don't know
it. For me it is like using my arms to lift something. Just another part of my body
doing the lifting. Here, watch." Held out one of the pieces of wood with his left hand,
his right hand touching the further end of the piece. Within a second flames danced
through his fingers, without leaving a trace on his hand. He lifted his hand, smiling at
Asher with the whitest teeth.
He threw the burning piece of wood into the rain and after a few more seconds the
flames vanished with white and black smoke.
"If you ever get Lilith to do this, her piece will keep on burning for hours in the rain
if it has enough wood. Quite something she is. But now it's your turn." She handed
pieces of cinder over to Asher. He held a bubble of cinder in his hands trying to, do
what? He didn't know how to begin.
"What am I suppose to do now?", he asked.
"You need to find the flame within you. It might be tiny for you but it should still be
there. It is in all of us, burning hot. Find the fire and then guide it to the cinder."
Find the flame within you. Asher closed his eyes, thinking it would be the right thing
to do. Searching through his body in hope to find it somewhere. What he found was
something else. He found memories and images of what had been and what could be.
He saw his mother lying on her bed, bones breaking through her skin. Saw his father
angry and drunk with only Elyas to take the hit. There was no flame within him.
"I can't", Asher said.
"Keep trying."
"Why should I", Asher opened his eyes. "You said it yourself. Even if I find the flame
it will be tiny. I won't be able to do anything. I am useless like this. Why keep trying
when it obviously doesn't work."
"Because", An said, no smile on his face, "either you try harder or you will die. Even
in your little town you know the next war is coming. It is as certain as the rain. It
either raining or it's about to rain. We are either at war or close to it. They will look
after Lilith trying to make her into a weapon they can use. But we? We are
expandable. Only good to heat up the metal tools used to heal soldiers on the
battlefield. If you can't even do that." He broke off, looking down.
War was always coming, An was right.
"Alright", Asher said after a pause. "I will keep trying." He looked inside of him,
searched for it, for the tiny flame he knew must be inside somewhere. He felt the cold
stone on which he sat, heard the soft rain falling. Where was the flame hiding? His
mind racing from one part of his body to the next. Was it maybe too small to find it?
And then without any warning the cinder in his hands began to come alight. Small
yellow and red waves of fire licking at his hands. Their touches warm and
demanding.
"I did it", Asher cried out. An was looking over his shoulder, almost angry.
A slow clapping in Asher's back. "Well done, Newby." Lilith chuckled. The flames in
his hand dying down. All what's left was dark grey ash picked up by the wind and
fleeing from Lilith. It wasn't him afterall. The triumph in his belly vanishing.
"Well I guess it was a long day for all of us", said An. "Let's try another time maybe
without interruption. You will get hang of it eventually trust me." He glanced over to
Lilith, who shrugged and walked away. Her frizzy hair uncombed, wild like a storm.
With the setting sun, darkness swallowed Asta, the white city. From Asher's room
higher up than most other buildings he had a grand view of a huge chunk of the city.
Lanters were being lit all around and people would lay their work down for the day.
Asher missed working with his wood terribly during his journey. He didn't take any
of the tools with him, because they weren't his. Everybody owned the tools. Iron was
too precious and expensive to be owned by a lonely worker. All he smuggled with
him was a single knife, sheathed in black leather. A wet stone for sharpening and oil
against rust were tucked in a small satchel. He would need to find some good wood
tomorrow to carve and work. He missed the calming work routine.
A knock on his door startled him, he slid the satchel with his precious knife under his
bed and walked to the door.
"An, what are you doing here?"
"I am happy to see you too", An said. His smile as wide as his face, his words a little
slurred. Was he drunk? "We have been thinking, well mostly I did the thinking as
always, but don't tell them I said that. They are all a little simple in the head, if you
know what I am saying." He chuckled. "Anyway I came here to invite you to our
nightly get to together. Just the five of us now." With one hand he was holding
himself up at the door frame, the other hand on his hip. He was drunk. Asher could
tell.
"I don't know. The other ones don't seem to like me at all. So why would I want and
spend the evening with them."
"Good point", An still smiling. "But you miss the crucial argument, we have liquor.
Good one at that. I will show you, now come and don't be like that always."
"Like what."
"You know, all sad and thoughtful. You live too much in your head, Newby. Sorry.
You'll see it's nice to loosen up a bit from time to time. Tomorrow morning you can
be all sad and thoughtful again."
"I don't drink", Asher said. He promised himself to never touch a bottle. Not with
what his father had become. An's eyes widening, his mouth gasping as if Asher said
something unheard of.
"Did you try some before? This is not some cheap stuff, you should know. It's
stronger than Rasborrow beer, but taste like cherry. It's reeeeeally good."
"It's not the taste or anything. I just don't like drinking that's all."
"Alright, alright, alright." An's eyes staring into nothing like he was thinking about
something vital. "Well, you can come anyway, though. I would pull you with me if I
could, but you know the whole burning up in flames is not my style. So just come
with me."
What was the alternative? Sitting in his empty room, alone with his thoughts and
fears. He had nothing to keep his mind occupied. How bad could it be going with
him? He other's didn't look like they liked him anyway, so what was there to lose?
"Alright, I come with you. But I won't drink."
"Sure", An slurred. "No need to. It will probably get you some points with the others.
More for us, you know."
An's room was along the hallway and around the corner. He was swaying a bit from
left to right, which was dangerous, one stumble and he would crash into Asher. He
tried to put more distance between each other, without success. Without knocking An
opened the door. Asher heard glimpses of laughter but it died immediatly when they
saw him standing in the door. The smell of alcohol in the thick air. Lilith with a bottle
of red liquid sitting near the small fire place on the ground, legs crossed. Patric and
Kathrine sitting with their backs to the bed, also on the ground. A couple of candles
standing on the bedside table. All eyes were on Asher. He couldn't see their faces, so
he wasn't sure if they were angry to see him or not. A big book shelf, made out of
white birch, was standing beside An's bed, filled to the brim with books bound in
leather. A painting hanging over the fireplace, but the light was to dim to make out
what it was about.
"I am back, and see who I found", An said pointing towards the awkwardly standing
Asher.
"I see you brought something with you from the toilet", Lilith said. The others
chuckling. It washed the half hearted smile from Asher's lips.
"Very funny, but no. Come on five him a chance. It's not every day we get someone
new close to our age. He is one of us now." The others silently looking at An and
Asher.
Kathrin vanquished the silence. "You right, Anderius. Come on and sit with us over
here." She pointed to her left, Patric sitting to her right.
"Thanks", Asher said. Not sure what to say at this moment. Lilith following him
walking throught the room with her eyes.
"Here", Lilith passed him the half empty bottle. For a moment he wasn't so sure
anymore. His conviction began to falter. One drink wouldn't do him any harm. It
would probably be good for him. Loosening up a little after all this, being part of the
group. He took the bottle. The glass was warm from were Lilith held it. Should he
try?
"He doesn't drink. Pass it down to me will you? I am feeling thirsty already again."
Thankful for An taking the decision away from him, he passed the bottle down to
Kathrine.
"Why don't you want a drink", Patric asked. A simple question with so many answers.
The others waiting patiently for him to talk. Even Lilith didn't comment on it. An
sipping on the bottle.
"My father often drinks a beer to much. So I swore myself not to touch any drink."
Patric was nodding knowingly. He looked down at his hands. Could he understand
Asher? For the first time since he arrived here, he didn't consider the others having
just as bad a life as he had. So much pain waiting behind their angry and hostile
faces. Maybe even hiding behind An's cantagious smile.
"That sucks", Patric said.
"It does."
"Well", Kathrine began, "I want to apologize for earlier in the dining room. I didn't,
it's just hard."
"What's hard? What is it I am doing wrong all the time?" The question lingered on his
lips the whole day. Finally shared with the world, the vacuum it left behind almost
swallowing him whole.
"We all awoke when we were children, too small to even remember in most cases."
Kathrine's voice was soft and she didn't seem as drunk as An. "We don't remember
touching our parents at all, feeling their warmth on our skin. Playing on their laps,
playing with other children. Nothing. You on the other hand, had an almost full life to
live before it was taken from you. You felt lips on yours because you could act on
your feelings. Do you not see how that might be upsetting to us?"
"It doesn't mean my life was perfect though."
"I am not saying it was perfect. You sure had your problems, your father apparently
being one of them. But at least you had a life. We didn't. Not a normal one."
"But why do you hate me for it so much? I can't do anything about it. I didn't ask for
this anymore than you did."
"They are not angry with you", Lilith said. Behind her small flames playing with her
shadow. "They envy you. What do you think Cathrine and Patric would give to spend
a night together without the impossibility to touch each other. What would they give
to kiss like you have? Everything."
The others silent, only the fire crackling.
"What about you then?", Asher asked. Feeling anger rising inside of him. "You don't
seem to see yourself with the others. Why do you hate me so?"
"Because", her voice rising, "you disgust me. Coming in here with these sad little
puppy eyes. What pain did you endure in your life, tell me? It doesn't even compare
to ours. You can't even imagine it. So stop feeling sorry for yourself. If anyone should
walk these halls with their head down it shouldn't be you."
"And how do you know all that? You don't know a thing about me." Asher voice
sounding agitated. Anger and frustration bubbling inside his stomach, aching to be
released.
"I don't need to know your life", Lilith hissed between her teeth.
"Why not? Because yours was so much harder? How would you even begin to
quantify pain. Your suffering doesn't discard mine. I have felt enough pain in my
life." Asher needed to stand up. He was shouting the words now. "I believe you had
your fair share of pain. But this is not a rainin' competition. Stop making it into one."
With that he stormed out the room, he heard An say something but he didn't
understand. He ran. As far and fast as he could.
