Chapter 4


The night was agonizingly long for Malina. She saw the moons wander from one side
to the other, trying to keep her head from replaying conversations and memories. She
didn't know if she slept at all, but she felt her whole body ache and her mind mushy.
Her back hurt the most, it felt stiff. She never ever had slept not in a feather bed. This
was unacceptable. The conversation with Dago repeating in her head over and over.
She came to a conclusion, chatting with the moons shining throught he thick canopy.
The rain hadn't stop the whole night but their tarp was holding firm. Dago had been
sleeping next to her the whole night, snoring quietly.
Her father was a good and honest man, Malina was sure of it. She knew him better
than anybody else. Especially better than Dago. So why would she believe in a shady
man, someone like Dago, who was in the business of dreamshrooms. A nagging
feeling still left in the back of her head, trying to get free, but Malina did her best to
surpress it completely. She was her father's precious pearl and he was honest, good,
hard working man. He was everything she had left, her mother being gone and their
family in shambles. Dago was just making fun of her, trying to make her afraid. What
a good chuckling he must have had after that conversation.
Malina didn't dare to get out of her clothes at all, normally she slept in a nightgown
with a warm blanket or two to snuggle with. But here she only had a thin wool
blanket over her day clothes. It wasn't freezing cold here as she thought it would be,
but it was cold at night. A lot colder than down in Nigita.
The men were huddling around a small fire, feeding on the remaining dry firewood
they had. A faint mist of rainddrops flooting through the air making it feel moist all
around. For breakfast they had simple bread with some old and hard cheese and ham.
Not something she would normally eat in the morning, but she was starving. The
foraging yesterday left her hungry, very hungry. The food filled her empty stomach
and warmed her from the inside. The cold of the night slowly leaving her body. The
men eyeing her weirdly, but saying nothing.
"Slept well, Missy", Dago asked. Was that a grin on his face? He was joking with her.
He must be. The other man stared grumpy at her, their beards full of breadcrumbs.
The forest silent all around them. Malina felt like she was watched again, looking
around between bites.
"We will walk some way west, find some more juicy shrooms and then head back to
Nigita. I want some more bags full before we leave here."
They packed up their things, Malina the only one without a backpack, and started
walking through the woods again. The air moist, the ground soft with moss and grass.
They had to climb over fallen trees or get around big thorny bushes. Malina made
sure not to get scratched through her clothes, but she wasn't successful doing it. Her
arms and face bore several scratches. She hoped they wouldn't leave a visible mark
on her face. Birds singing in the distance.
A shrill whisteling cut through the background noise. Dago froze in front of her, the
other men too. They drew their knifes, longer ones than the ones to cut shrooms with.
The shining metal reflecting the little light there was in these woods. So much iron,
how could they pay for these? The small swords must have cost a fortune.
Her questioning was cut short when arrows hit the three men, knocking them to the
ground with cries of pain. Malina wanting to shout herself but the sounds were stuck
somewhere down her throat. She fell down to her knees, hands to her chest and
praying to the Enlightening. Dago kept standing in front of her, hands in his pockets
watching the woods around him. From every side men, and to Malina's surprise,
women, with bows came from the shadows, down from trees and closed a circle
around Dago and Malina.
The savages found them, they were surrounded. How could this happen? Dago had
made sure of it, they were not too far into the forest. Not near of any territory. Father,
Malina thought. Why did you make me go?
Naked female necks. It was the first thing Malina noticed. About half of the savages
leading Dago and herself through the forest on hidden paths, were woman. All
wearing nothing around their necks. A little less, but still outragious, were their naked
breasts. The left breast poked through a hole in the clothing. Decorated with colorful
patterns and rings going through the skin. Malina was baffled. How could these
people live like this? Did they not have any sense of shame?
"Let me do the talking", Dago whispered to her. "I know my way around these
people. Well sort of."
"You met them before", Malina hissed. "How could you lead us so close into their
territory? Are you mad?"
"Stop this angry act of yours. It doens't help anyone, Missy. I didn't lead us into their
territory. The forest so close to the border belongs to noone. Or so I heard anyway.
Something must be going on here. They never venture so close to the edge."
"It seems somehow you were mistaken. I can't understand how that might have
happened. You seemed so wise, Master Dago." Malina was terrified by the men
around her. She counted five men and four women. The men were muscular in stature
with shoulder long hair mostly. Some of them seemed to be twice as wide as her
father. She always though he was strong.
The forest cleared before them and Malina was guided towards what could only be
their village. But in this case they were no houses of clay, stone or wood with
shingled roofs. They were building their houese betweent the trees with a big clearing
in the midde in which the group arrives. Here the rain poured down from above. The
buildings were small and connected with little bridges made from single branches. It
looked like suicide walking over these to Malina. Closer to the trees there were
seating areas carved from fallen trees. Fires burning all around with people preparing
food.
"Follow me", a man with blue color around his eyes and long black hair said. He was
a good arm length taller tham Malina. He showed them towards one of the seating
areas, covered by a giant leaf roof. A man with a ring made of iron through his nose
sat there waiting for them. He had broad shoulders, keen blue eyes and long ebony
hair.
"Dago", he exclaimed his arms showing them to their seats opposite to him. "Did we
not have an agreement. I thought I was clear about it."
"Karman, it is good to see you in good health again", Dago said, without any fear in
his voice. Malina trying to keep tears from rolling down her eyes. She knew the
stories of what could happen to young woman in these woods. "I think you are
making my little friend here quite uncomfortable. Could you please assure her
nothing will happen to her?"
Karman looked at Malina. These eyes were like clear blue water on a sunny day. "No
doubt you know our reputation, but reputation and mouth of word does not mean
truth. Truth is important. We do not take what is not ours, not with or without force.
We do not kill those you come unarmed and without the desire to hurt. Your friends
broke one of these rules, we spoke to your friend here years ago. But even so, your
men were drawing weapons when they should not have. Why is that?"
Malina still fighting back tears. His words didn't make much sense in her head. The
fear eating away at her, making her understand words but not their meaning.
"See, nothing will happen so stop doing that face like you will meet the
Enlightening." Dago waited for a reaction from Malina, but she kept on staring at
these blue eyes. "Anway, I didn't know they had weapons on them. You allowed small
knives to be carried for cutting the shrooms and that is all I had provided to them."
"I see", Karman said. "What is with your companion?"
"Ah, I think she is thinking about killing her father. After all what happened I sure
would think about it."
Killing her father? Was that some kind of plan of his? Feeding her lies of her father
and than turning her against him?
"Why would I want to hurt my father? You are the one who brought me here. You are
the one who tells me lies about my father. You are the one you let us get capture by
these-" She broke of. Be smart now, Malina thought. "By these people."
"Ah, that what this is about. You are mistaken. You are not captives here. I simply
wanted to talk and set new boundries. Rest assured, no harm will come to you."
Malina's muscles relaxing, could he speak the truth? She saw no lie in his eyes but
she wouldn't know how to look for them anyway. When she didn't say anything,
Karman turned back to Dago again.
"So, things are changing here. We cannot leave the woods so close to the edge
anymore."
"Some war going on here, eh? Or what's this about? There is enough land here. The
woods are gigantic. Why settle here?"
"We cannot speak to you about it and you will do good not to challenge me on this."
"I understand", Dago said. "So no agreement anymore? This will kill me, you know?"
"We allow you passage to these parts of the woods in exchange for trust and the
goods we had declared before. But you will have to come alone from now on. We
cannot allow more strangers to forage our territory."
Walking away with their bodies unscathed Malina felt it to be a miracle she was still
alive. Maybe stories and truth were further apart than she might have thought before.
"You alright, Missy", Dago asked.
"I should ask you this question. They were your men now lying dead in this misty
forest."
"Ah, the men. I almost forgot about the. There are plenty where they came from." He
said it with no regret in his voice, no guilt. Or did Malina just not catch it right? "So
you think I was lying about your father then?"
"Obviously", she said. Her eyes glued to the path in front of them.
"You should spend some more time with these people here. Would get your head right
again, maybe."
"How would I spend more time with them? They are dangerous savages. Eventhough
they didn't hurt me right here doesn't mean they wouldn't do so in the future."
"You are so narrow minded. Where do you think these people come from? They were
all runaways someday and so will you. Trust me, Dago knows people."
