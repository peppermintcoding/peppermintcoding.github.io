Chapter 11


A knock on the door made Asher jump up from his bed, he told the outsider to come
in, half expecting it to be An as almost every morning. This time it wasn't the smiling
young man, it was a serious and concerned looking Sarina stepping over the
threshold. Her tied up on top into a knot, arms resting on either side. She seemed
tense.
"Good morning, Asher," she said and Asher told her to come in. "I am sorry to bother
you so early but something has happened that needs discussing in private I fear."
The first thought Asher's mind let him think, was his mother. Another letter had come
from Yevum and Sarina was breaking the sad news. Or was it because of the letter
Lilith, An and he stole from Saratsu? Would he be in trouble now eventhough Lilith
promised to take care of it somehow? Or was it something with his brother? He felt
guilty for not thinking of him earlier and more these last few weeks.
"It's about Patric and Kathrine," she said and looked Asher straight in the eyes.
"What happened?"
"It appears, Patric and Kathrine ran away last night. We are inclined to think, he
wanted to return home with her. It pains me to see, they have lost their trust in me and
Lord Amberic to care and protect them. Or maybe the reason is unrelated to that, we
don't know. I simply wanted to come in here and tell you in person."
"What does that mean now?"
"It means we have doubled the guards outside and inside the complex. I didn't want it
this way but apparently I have no choice in the matter anymore. The order comes
from the Highlord himself."
"So it means I can't leave here anymore?" Asher was baffled. It never felt like a
prison here, but it surely looked like one if he thought about it. But they never
explicitly forbid them to leave. Where would they go anyway?
"It does, yes. Tell me, are you considering leaving us and going back where you came
from?"
"I think I would be the last person to leave, I can't go back home. It would mean
certain death most likely. You don't have to worry about me."
"So I should worry about someone else, then?"
"Oh no, you don't. Well, I don't know for sure, but I don't believe Lilith or An would
leave on their own will."
"We are not concerned with Lilith, but Anderius might be foolish enough to go. But
we can't have it in these times. Preparations for the war have already begun and it's
time for you to show your value to the Highlord and Lord Amberic."
"The war has already began?" Did that mean Asher had to go into battle now, caring
for the injured like he was taught? He wasn't ready yet. He could barely stitch up
someone with these clunky tools.
"It hasn't, but at this point it is inevitable. Highlord Rember will declare war in a
weeks time and you will be going with the troops, just like An will." Sarina paused
for a moment looking around the room. "Lilith will stay behind, don't worry she will
be fine."
"What will happen to Kathrine and Patric?" he asked again.
"We will send people after them trying to persuade them to come back, but that is not
your concern right now. I simply came here to inform you in person, I thought it was
the proper way, given you became quite close with them."
"Thank you," Asher said and Sarina left the room without another word. Asher
followed her out the door, after she vanished behind the next corner.
An and Lilith were already sitting in the dining room, their faces grim and sad. An's
eyes looked reddish and Lilith looked like she hadn't slept the whole night.
"What's up with you two?" Asher asked. The two shot him confused glances. "I guess
Sarina came to you, too, to talk about Kathrine and Patric. I never thought they would
run away, to be honest with you."
"Please, can you shut your rainin' mouth about it. You seem to be blind to everything
like always." For a moment Asher didn't believe, these words came from An's mouth.
After all this time, Lilith could have said these words, but An too? Apparently
something was going on again, and he was the only one who wasn't in on the secret.
"Then please enlighten me," he said.
Silence. An stood up from his seat and left. Lilith watching him go and Asher sat
there clueless.
"Don't take it to personally," Lilith said.
"It just sounds like I am at fault here. I didn't make them run away."
"Maybe not, maybe yes. But still, it's just another reminder, that we will never get
what we want in our lives. An especially."
"What do you mean by that? I don't understand. Was he in love with Kathrine, too?"
"Not quite, but it's not important." She pushed some bread and cheese over to Asher,
who tried to eat some, his stomach feeling tense.
"Let me ask you something," Lilith began again. "Why do you think the two ran
away?"
"I guess they wanted to run away together, so they could be together. Oh wait, that
doesn't really make any sense does it."
"No it doesn't."
Asher thought for a second. "So they didn't run away after all?"
Lilith simply shook her head, looking at him. Her eyes teared up and she brushed
them away with her sleeve.
"So what did really happen?" Asher asked.
"It's simple, really. It's an age old story I guess. Two people in love you can't be in
love. Hardulan and Siphone came from different worlds, socially. So she killed
herself with a dagger through her hurting heart after they executed Hardulan. Or
sometimes it is war who takes away. Or in our case, the curse will never let us have
something even remotely similiar to a physical relationship. So they did the
inevitable."
"How do you know? Maybe they really ran away."
"Why do you cling to a different explanation so desperatly?"
Asher bit a small piece from his bread and chewed slowly. It didn't taste good. "I kind
of feel like it was my fault. I told them how it is to kiss someone. I told them about
Ameline. Don't you think it had something to do with this? Isn't that why An is angry
with me?"
"He is angry because he is angry. You were just the person he lashed out against,
because you talked like it was any other day, when two of our friends died yesterday.
We heard it from one of the guards, Raegar, he told us on our way to breakfast. Not
that we needed confirmation of something we both knew."
"So you already knew?"
"Kathrine came to me last night, after we snatched that letter of yours. She was quite
out of it, I thought I had her convinced to stay and sleep on it, but apparently she
couldn't anymore. I mean who can fault them. They have been too close to each other
for years now. It is a miracle they lasted as long as they did."
"I see," Asher said. The thoughts circling in his mind. "I need to go and apologize to
An."
"Wait," Lilith said, "give him some space for now. I think he really needs that at the
moment."
They sat in silence and ate a few bites. Asher was the first one to break the silence
again, "Why would Sarina tell me they ran away? Why lie?"
"Good question. The lie is not for you though, it's for Lord Amberic. He would
change a lot around here, when he finds out two of his little toys died because of too
much body contact. Sarina put in some good words with him, so at least the older
ones of us, don't have to live in lonely prison rooms without any human interaction.
So she tells everybody the story how they ran away, and that it won't happen again,
because the guards have been doubled. So I also guess it is good bye for our nightly
bar trips, then. It's quite a shame."
"Why do you seem so calm about it?"
"Anger can be dangerous. Maybe not for An, maybe not for you. But for people with
power, they are easy to abuse it when angry or guided by their feelings. I can't give
into them. Horrible things can happen, and I won't let them."
Asher chewed the last of his slice of bread, drank a bit of water. He wanted to ask her
what she meant with horrible things, and if they had already happen once or twice.
But he didn't dare.
"You didn't seem to concealed your emotions, when I first got here. I remember quite
vividly, you being furious at me sometimes."
"Ah, right. Well, you made it quite hard to not be angry at you. I am no stone statue
after all, but a human like everybody else. Even if they sometimes forget. But let's
talk about something else. I don't want to keep thinking about this all the time. I have
an idea for your letter."
"Alright, what is it? Do you know a cleric or something too?"
"No, not quite. But I know someone, who knows a cleric well enough to ask him
something like this. You remember the guard Raegar, I told you about him. He knows
someone and agreed to get the letter to them."
"And he will do that because he is a good man?" Asher was sceptical.
"What do you think he would want from me? I don't actually have anything to offer,
not even my body if I wanted to. So yes, he is just a good person. Oddly enough."
Asher wasn't sure if he believed her, but it was the only option he could see. They
finished their breakfast and went to Asher's room to get the letter and give it to
Raegar before his shift was over.
Asher was waiting anxiously behind the main entrance door. The heavy wood doors
would open any second now. He was walking up and down like a cat ready to
pounce. Lilith was leaning against a pillar, she seemed relaxed.
"Can you stop that already," she said. "You will make me nervous with that, too."
"I didn't know, you could even be nervous at all. You always seem quite relaxed and
confident to me, if I am being honest. How would my anxiety change anything about
that?"
"It has it's perks to know you could burn anyone alive, who might try to overstep
their boundries. And I also have nothing to lose in a sense, so why worry at all? It's
not like they could do anything to me, right?"
"What about them banishing you from here? Where would you live? How? Doesn't
that scare you a bit?"
Lilith hesitated, put her weight on her other foot and said, "First of all, they won't
trow me out of here. There has to be something big to get Lord Amber to abandon
this whole project. But on the slight off chance it would happen, I would simply not
do anything. I am not afraid of dying."
"I whish I was a little more like you," Asher said with respect in his voice. "You
should lend me some of that confidence of yours sometime."
Before Lilith could answer the door opened without a creak and four guards came
rushing in, Raegar the last of them. Lilith gave Asher a quick smile and they let the
guards through. All armed with swords on their sides and spears in their hands. For a
moment Asher wondered what would happen if he were to attack one of them. Were
they instructed to kill him, or where they instructed to knock him out without serious
danger to his life? He didn't want to find out though.
"And," Lilith asked Raegar when the others were out of range, her voice a soft
whisper.
Raegar leaned in to her and Asher, handed back the letter to Asher and spoke, "Well
most of it is accurate of your cleric here, but my man told me something quite
interesting."
"Spill it, quickly," Asher said. He was still wanting to walk up and down. Standing
still didn't feel right.
"At the bottom of the letter, the last sentence doesn't make any sense. My cleric told
me, it means 'Caution to the reader of this letter, do not take note of this sentence'. I
don't know what that means, but it sounds like a hidden message, doesn't it."
Asher thought for a moment. This didn't make any sense. The hidden message was
his brother talking about his work not something like this. This didn't mean anything.
"I don't know," he said after a while.
"Well, I think it is safe to say you were right in some way about Saratsu lying. He
didn't share this with you so it must mean something, right?"
Raegar cleared his throat, "My cleric said something else, too. Sometimes, clerics
hide messages in letters, because they are the only one's who can read them. Well,
apart from the upper class maybe, but still. So I think this has to mean something. It
is even directly addressed to the reader, so I don't know, but something is strange
about this, I tell you. I have to go though, I will get into trouble if I don't hurry up.
Lilith." He bowed slightly to her and quickly went away. Asher shouted a "Thank
you", after him, but he didn't turn around again.
"What was that about?" Asher asked.
"What?"
"He literally bowed to you a little. How do you get him to do something for you and
then he shows even more respect to you? What does he get out of this?"
"Like I said already, he doesn't get anything out of it, so leave it Asher. Focus more
on the letter. There is something foul going on here. Do you know what it might
mean?"
Asher was completely clueless. It might be a hidden meaning in there, but he didn't
know why or what it meant. He didn't talk with Ogerius about anyhting therelike. He
knew about the hidden meaning behind Elyas's work, but nothing else was discussed.
"Apologies, but I have to go now, Asher," Lilith said. "My break is over and they are
expecting me back. We can talk more about it later. At An's at sundown?"
"Sure, I will be there, if he doesn't kick me out before."
"Talk to him. Everything is just fine, I promise." And she was off, the sound of her
boots getting quieter and quieter with every step she took. The quartz building did its
best to conserve the sound and let it bounce from wall to wall, but at the end, the
sound vanished completely and Asher was alone.
Asher waited in front of An's door for half an eternity, unable to knock on the heavy
oak door. He passed some time with examining the craftsmanship of the door.
Nothing special, plain wood planks interlocked with each other without the help of
any metal in them. It was like most doors.
He knocked, twice and waited. No answer from inside. A big part of Asher had hoped
Lilith would be there already and he didn't have to face An alone, but it didn't look
that way.
Still no answer. Asher opened the door slowly, not sure what he was expecting. An
was sitting on the ground, with his back against his bed, exactly where Asher and
Kathrine had sat the first day they all met up.
"Come in," An said. "Don't be shy now."
"How are you doing?"
"Well, how am I doing? Not great, but not so bad anymore I guess. The anger made
room for a deep sadness. I don't feel the energy to even anger myself. My body
doesn't seem to be able to do that anymore."
"Can I sit?"
"Sure." An made some more room between them and Asher sat down with his back
against the bed. Something was strange though. Not only didn't Asher see any alcohol
bottle or clay mugs, he neither smelled the alcohol.
"I am a bit surprised. Isn't it normally especially these times when people drink the
most."
"And that's precisely why I am not doing it. I don't like to drink when I am sad. It
doesn't really help, you know? Well, you don't probably but I am telling you now.
When you are sad, sometimes you just have to be sad for a bit without trying to
escape the pain. It will catch up with you anyway."
"I guess so. Have you heard, we will be moving tomorrow?"
"Yes, I have heard. Lucky them, I suppose. They did it just in time to not have to
experience another war. Sometimes I feel like people are only fighting with each
other, because that's just what you do when you are a Highlord. They have too much
time and wealth to think about some stupid way to throw away human lives."
"My father used to say the exact same thing. Lucky for him, they rather wanted him
to make siege equipment and other things than to go to war as a undertrained soldier.
But still, war always means some of your friends and family will die. It always
happens."
The two sat in silence. Lilith still didn't show, she must have been held up by
something, the sun vanishing behind the horizon.
"You know what I can't get out of my head?" Asher asked.
"What?"
"I am concerned what will happen to that racoon family, Kathrine was feeding all this
time. I actually wanted to go down there and give them something myself, but now
that the guards have been doubled I don't know if that is a good or even possible
idea."
"Those rainin' creatures," An laughed. "That's what you are concerned with right
now?" He laughed full heartidly. "You know, Kathrine and me, we never really
worked together, because, well, because stuff, you know. But I already miss her, the
day she is gone. Isn't that weird? How she didn't seem important when she was here,
but now that she is gone it matters so much to me?"
"I honestly miss her voice the most."
"Like sweet, sweet rum. So smooth and clear."
"I don't know about the alcohol, but I think I agree."
"So your father gets really bad when he is drunk? What exactly does that mean?"
Asher stared blank at the wall in front of him. The little fireplace, half in the wall,
looked forsaken and lonely. Memories bubbled up from the deepest prison Asher had
build for them. So many memories. Even the good ones, he didn't want to think
about. They were all with the same person and Asher didn't want to think about this
person at all. "It means he did bad things to all of us, even when he wasn't drunk. But
with time and more beer it got worse and worse. Once he broke my arm and told
everybody I fell from a tree playing. The worst thing about it was, how he told the
story. Everybody heard the story, at least twice because he couldn't keep his mouth
shut about it. He made the story funny, I mean not for us, but for everybody else who
listened. And after all that, even when he sobered up enough, he never said sorry. Or
that he was doing something wrong. Or anything. He never talked about it." Asher's
eyes as dry as a fire fixed on the fireplace. He was done losing tears because of his
father.
"That's messed up," An said. The door swung open without a notice and Lilith came
into the room. Her hair messy and she was sweating. She held her boots in her left
hand, walking around only in her socks.
"Apologies," she said and closed the door, "it took longer than I thought today, but
now I am done for today. What are you guys talking about?"
"Asher is vastly concerned with the well being of our little racoon family here. I think
we need to find a way to sneak out sometime and give them some food or he will try
something stupid himself."
"I never understood why Kathrine liked them and fed them," Lilith said and she sat
down on the oposite side of Asher and An, her back resting next to the fireplace. "I
would have never guessed from her appearance, she would care for those little
creatures."
"Well, you shouldn't judge someone entirely by their appearance, or we would never
have hung out with Asher." An laughed and Lilith with him.
"Alright, very nice," Asher said. "I like you too."
"Very sweet. So what were you two up to today?" An asked, his smile almost honest.
"We were investigating the stolen letter," Lilith said and built up logs of wood in the
fire place. "And we actually found something interesting. Tell him, Asher." She
ignited the fire with a swift hand movement. It started licking at the logs quickly and
a big wave of embers flew up the chimney.
"Yes, the last sentence on that letter was questionable," Asher said with his eyes fixed
on Lilith in front of the fire. Her hair melted with the fire in the background into one
waving red. "It basically told the reader to ignore this sentence completely. But why
write it down only to explain not to read it. I don't get it. Half of me believes the
cleric of Raegar lied as well, but that doesn't seem very likely, does it?"
"No, not at all," Lilith said. "He had no reason to lie and why would he make
something like this up. I think there is something your cleric from Yevum is hiding
from you. But why write something so cryptically."
"That's the big question," Asher said and the room fell silent. The fire crackled in the
fireplace and the air was getting warmer inside the room. An stood up and opened a
window to let some fresh air in.
After a while of silence Asher said, "I have to get home and find out what it is all
about. Maybe that's what is means."
"But," Lilith said, "why write in this way, when the purpose of it was to not get read
to you anyway. It doesn't make sense. And you can't get back to Yevum. They would
never let you do that."
"I mean, there is always a way," An said and looked from Lilith to Asher and back to
Lilith. "You have never planned to run away for real. Not even fantasized about it?"
"No I haven't," said Lilith and looked to the ground. Something was wrong. Asher
couldn't shake the feeling, she was withholding something from them.
"I have," An said without noticing what was wrong with Lilith. "Often actually, to
keep me busy on those rainin' days, when it pours without end."
"So what would you suggest," Asher asked.
"If you want to know so badly, I will share some of my secret ideas with you, my
friend. Not all of them are actually possible, but there is something I always felt
would be an easy out."
"What is it?" asked Lilith seemingly interested now, but her eyes looked sad.
"Lucky for you, Asher, the Enlightening has heard your prayers and given you the last
and vital part to my plan of leaving this all behind and sit next to a fire somewhere
else with hopefully a good drink and some sun in my eyes. Tomorrow, we will go to
war and some of us might never come back from there alive."
Asher felt cold, goosebumps appearing all over his arms and legs.
